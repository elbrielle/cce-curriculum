## Lesson Objective

Students will explore professional sales careers through H&L, complete the PowerSkills Communication module, deliver an oral professional presentation on a career of their choice, and describe appropriate appearance standards for professional settings.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can deliver a 3-minute oral presentation on a career I explored this year, describe appropriate professional appearance for an interview, and demonstrate active listening and assertive communication skills."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(4)(B):** Identify skills that can be transferable among a variety of careers.
- **d(4)(C):** Prepare and deliver an oral presentation on a career topic.
- **d(6)(B):** Describe appropriate appearance for an interview setting.

## Materials Needed

- Chromebooks with internet access
- Hats & Ladders student accounts (PowerSkills: Communication module)

- Printed Interview Appearance Guide (1 per student)

## Vocabulary

- **Oral Presentation:** A formal speech delivered to an audience on a specific topic. Students present on a career they have researched.
- **Professional Appearance:** The way you dress, groom, and carry yourself in a workplace or interview setting.
- **Active Listening:** Fully concentrating on what someone is saying, understanding their message, and responding thoughtfully.
- **Sales Management:** The process of leading a sales team, setting targets, developing strategies, and building client relationships.
- **Assertive Communication:** Expressing your ideas and needs clearly and respectfully, without being passive or aggressive.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

The H&L PowerSkills Communication module is the preparation activity for this week's presentations. It teaches active listening, accountable talk, and assertive communication through interactive workplace scenarios. After completing the module, students apply these skills in their career presentations.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Modeling:** Teacher delivers a sample 3-minute career presentation as a model. Show the structure: introduction (career name and why you chose it), body (3 key facts), conclusion (career plan connection).
- **Two Stars and a Wish:** During peer feedback, students give two specific compliments (stars) and one constructive suggestion (wish).

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: Sales Careers + PowerSkills Communication

**WARM-UP: Have you ever tried to convince someone to buy something? What strategy did you use?**

#### H&L Sales Pathway Exploration (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "This week we explore sales careers AND you give career presentations. Start by exploring sales careers in H&L -- find the Professional Sales pathway in the Marketing cluster."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore Sales Hats within the Business, Marketing, and Finance cluster. The workbook (Ch 5) confirms Marketing and Sales and Retail Management as pathways. The workbook's "Pitching Investors" project (Ch 5) has student groups pitch a business idea (sports app, food business, or pet business) to investor profiles -- an excellent tie-in for this presentation week. Students use the Hat Finder to explore: Sales Manager, Account Executive, Retail Manager.

#### PowerSkills Communication Module (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Before you present on Friday, practice your communication skills with the H&L PowerSkills Communication module. It teaches active listening and assertive speaking through workplace scenarios."

> [H&L PLATFORM] As noted in the 2SW Wk 5 corrections, there is no standalone "Communication" module in the Powerskills workbook. For presentation prep, use the Powerskills workbook's "Giving and Receiving Feedback" module (p. 22-24) for peer feedback practice, and the "Advocacy" module (p. 30-32) for speaking up confidently. The workbook's "Persuasion" Powerskill (Ch 13, p. 213) is also excellent for presentation skills -- students create a persuasive video script using audience awareness, emotional appeal, and calls to action.

**EXIT TICKET: Name two communication skills from the PowerSkills module and explain how each helps during a presentation. (d(4)(B))**

### Day 2: Presentation Prep: Choose Your Career

**WARM-UP: If you had 3 minutes to tell the class about your dream career, what would you say?**

#### Presentation Assignment + Research (40 min)

> **Teacher:** "You are delivering a 3-minute oral presentation on ANY career from ANY cluster you explored this year. Structure: introduction (career name, why you chose it), 3 key facts (education, DFW salary, daily tasks), conclusion (connection to your career plan)."

Teacher distributes the Career Presentation Rubric. Students choose their career topic, research using H&L and BLS, and create an outline with intro-body-conclusion structure.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about why organizing a presentation into introduction, body, and conclusion helps the audience understand?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name the career you will present on and list your 3 key facts. (d(4)(C))**

### Day 3: Interview Appearance + Practice

**WARM-UP: You have a job interview tomorrow. What would you wear? What would you NOT wear?**

#### Professional Appearance Discussion (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Before we practice presentations, let us discuss how you LOOK in professional settings. This is not about expensive clothes -- it is about being clean, neat, and appropriate."

Teacher presents business professional vs. business casual vs. inappropriate choices. Students complete the Interview Appearance Guide.

**DOK 2: How would you describe the difference between business professional and business casual?**

#### Practice Presentations (25 min)

Students practice with partners using the rubric for feedback. Teacher circulates checking readiness.

**EXIT TICKET: Describe what you would wear to an interview for your chosen career. (d(6)(B))**

### Day 4: Career Presentations Day 1

**WARM-UP: Take 2 minutes to review your outline. Deep breath. You are ready.**

#### Student Presentations (40 min)

> **Teacher:** "Presentation day. 3 minutes per person. Introduction, 3 key facts, conclusion. Speak clearly, make eye contact. After each, Two Stars and a Wish."

First half of class delivers presentations. Timer set for 3 minutes. Peers provide Two Stars and a Wish. Teacher scores with rubric.

**DOK 4: As you listen, evaluate which careers have the strongest combination of salary, demand, and alignment with the presenter's personality.**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one thing you did well or one thing you learned from watching others. (d(4)(C))**

### Day 5: Career Presentations Day 2 + Reflection

**WARM-UP: What did you learn from yesterday's presentations that you want to apply to yours today?**

#### Student Presentations (30 min)

Second half presents. Same format.

#### H&L Favorites + Reflection (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "Great work this week. Update your H&L favorites. Reflect: What was the hardest part of presenting? How did PowerSkills help?"

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students update favorites and Career Plan.

**EXIT TICKET: Rate your presentation 1-10 and name one thing you would improve. (d(4)(C))**

## Formative Assessment

- PowerSkills module completion (Day 1). (d(4)(B))
- Presentation outline quality (Day 2). (d(4)(C))
- Interview Appearance Guide (Day 3). (d(6)(B))
- Two Stars and a Wish quality (Days 4-5). (d(4)(C))

## Summative Assessment

Career Oral Presentation (Days 4-5): 3-minute presentation scored on content accuracy, organization, delivery (eye contact, volume, pace, confidence), and career connection. Primary d(4)(C) artifact.

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide presentation outline template with fill-in-the-blank structure.
- Allow note cards during presentation.
- Offer small-group presentation option for anxious students.

### Extensions

- Create a professional slide deck to accompany the presentation.
- Present WITHOUT notes for bonus.
- Record and self-evaluate using the rubric.

### ELL Language Support

- Bilingual presentation outline template with Spanish sentence starters.
- Allow ESL students to present to a small group if needed.
- PowerSkills module provides low-stakes digital practice before live presenting.
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peer for practice round.
