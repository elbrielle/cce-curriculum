## Lesson Objective

Students will finalize their H&L Career Plan, select their top career pathway, confirm their HS course plan, optionally retake the RIASEC assessment, deliver a capstone career plan presentation, and complete a comprehensive end-of-year reflection.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can present my finalized career plan including my selected pathway, high school courses, postsecondary goals, and explain how my career interests have evolved throughout the year."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(8)(A):** Select a career pathway related to personal interests.
- **d(8)(B):** Document high school courses, postsecondary education, and training requirements.
- **d(8)(C):** Write an individual plan to start a career.
- **d(4)(C):** Prepare and deliver an oral presentation (career plan presentation).

## Materials Needed

- Chromebooks with internet access
- Hats & Ladders student accounts (Career Planner + District Course Planner)
- Texas OnCourse: https://texasoncourse.org
- Irving ISD CTE Pathways poster
- My Career Journey reflection sheet (updated throughout year)
- Printed Career Plan Presentation Rubric
- Printed End-of-Year Reflection handout
- Certificates of Completion (optional)

## Career Connection

This is the CAPSTONE WEEK -- 36 weeks of career exploration culminating in a final Career Plan. Students have explored Manufacturing, IT, Law, Health Science, Agriculture, Human Services, Business, Aviation, Automotive, Trades, Architecture, Construction, Finance, Education, Arts, Marketing, and more. They have RIASEC assessments, resumes, cover letters, interview skills, and personal budgets. Now they pull it all into a Career Plan that serves as a genuine roadmap for their future.


**Irving ISD Pathway: Students present their individually selected Irving ISD CTE pathway -- any pathway from any high school explored during the year.**


## Vocabulary

- **Career Plan:** A comprehensive document including career goal, selected pathway, HS course sequence, postsecondary requirements, transferable skills, and action steps.
- **Capstone:** A culminating project demonstrating everything learned throughout a course.
- **District Course Planner:** In H&L, the tool showing Irving ISD-specific course offerings mapped to pathways.
- **Postsecondary Goal:** Plan for after HS: university, community college, trade school, military, apprenticeship, or employment with certification.
- **RIASEC Growth:** Change in career interest profile over time reflecting new experiences and self-awareness.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

The H&L Career Planner and District Course Planner are the primary tools. Students finalize the Career Plan built all year, ensuring it reflects current interests. The optional RIASEC retake provides a powerful before-and-after comparison. The downloadable Career Plan from H&L is the official d(8) artifact -- shareable with families and counselors during the 8th-to-9th grade transition.


## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Modeling:** Teacher models a complete career plan presentation: career goal, RIASEC connection, pathway, courses, postsecondary goal, and growth reflection.
- **Celebration:** Final presentations should feel like a CELEBRATION. Consider inviting an administrator, creating certificates, and decorating the room. Students have worked hard all year.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: Finalize Your H&L Career Plan

**WARM-UP: Open your H&L Climber Profile. How many careers have you favorited this year? Which cluster has the most?**

#### Career Plan Finalization (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "This is the last week. Everything you have done -- every assessment, cluster tour, career profile, activity -- builds to your final Career Plan. Finalize it in H&L: favorites, Building Blocks, pathway selection, and course plan. Make sure it reflects your CURRENT interests."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students finalize their H&L Career Plan: personality type, Work Values, Building Blocks, favorites, pathway, and course plan via District Course Planner. The workbook (Ch 16: My Next Steps) provides written templates: "My Career and Course Plan," "Lifestyle Snapshot," and "Being a Career Thinker." These workbook pages serve as a backup/complement to the app's Career Plan. Confirm the app's submission and download process with your H&L district admin.

#### Optional RIASEC Retake (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "Optional: retake the RIASEC assessment and compare to your Week 0 results. See how you have grown."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students optionally retake the personality assessment ("Discover My Core") and compare to their original results from Week 0. The workbook (Ch 1, p. 12) includes a reflection section for recording results. Whether the app allows a retake with comparison is an app-specific feature -- confirm with your H&L district admin. If not available in-app, students can compare their original workbook page to a fresh self-assessment.

**EXIT TICKET: Name your selected career pathway and the Irving ISD high school where it is offered. (d(8)(A))**

### Day 2: Write Your Career Plan + Presentation Prep

**WARM-UP: In one sentence: your career goal. In one more: your first step toward it.**

#### Written Career Plan (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Write the final version of your Individual Career Plan. Sections: (1) Career Goal, (2) RIASEC connection, (3) Selected pathway and HS, (4) 4-year course plan, (5) Postsecondary goal, (6) Three action steps, (7) Personal growth reflection. Bring this to your HS counselor when registering for 9th grade."

Students write final Career Plan using structured template referencing H&L data.

**DOK 4: Create criteria for evaluating whether your career plan is strong enough to guide your decisions for 4-6 years. Apply those criteria to your own plan.**

#### Presentation Outline (15 min)

Students outline their capstone presentation: career goal, RIASEC, pathway, courses, postsecondary, and growth reflection. 3-5 minutes.

**EXIT TICKET: What is your postsecondary goal and first step to get there? (d(8)(B), d(8)(C))**

### Day 3: Capstone Presentations Day 1

**WARM-UP: Review your outline. Remember eye contact, volume, confidence. This is YOUR plan -- own it.**


#### Career Plan Presentations (40 min)

> **Teacher:** "This is the highlight of the entire course. Stand up and tell us about YOUR future. Present with pride."

First half presents (3-5 min each). Structure: goal, RIASEC, pathway, courses, postsecondary, growth. Two Stars and a Wish feedback. Teacher scores with rubric.

**DOK 4: Evaluate which classmates' plans are most realistic and well-researched. What makes a career plan convincing?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one classmate's plan that impressed you and what made it strong. (d(4)(C))**

### Day 4: Capstone Presentations Day 2

**WARM-UP: What is the BEST career plan presentation you have seen so far?**

#### Career Plan Presentations (30 min)

Second half presents. Same format and rubric.

#### H&L Career Plan Download (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "Download your finalized Career Plan from H&L. Keep it. Share it with your family and counselor."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students download/print finalized H&L Career Plan. The workbook's "My Career and Course Plan" (Ch 16) provides a written version students can also complete and take home. Confirm the app's download/export process with your H&L district admin. Students should have both a digital (app) and written (workbook) version of their career plan as the course deliverable.

**EXIT TICKET: Download your plan and write one sentence about what you will do with it over the summer. (d(8)(A), d(8)(B), d(8)(C))**

### Day 5: End-of-Year Reflection + Celebration

**WARM-UP: On the first day of this class, I thought career planning was _____. Now I think career planning is _____.**

#### End-of-Year Reflection (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "This is our LAST DAY. You have come so far since Week 0. Now you have a Career Plan, resume, cover letter, interview skills, budget, and knowledge of dozens of careers. I am proud of every one of you."

Students complete End-of-Year Reflection: How have interests changed? Most surprising learning? Best cluster? What excites you about HS? Transferable skills developed? Advice for next year's students? Final My Career Journey update.

**DOK 4: Write a letter to next year's CCE students with your top 3 pieces of career exploration advice based on specific experiences.**

#### Celebration + Certificates (15 min)

Teacher distributes Certificates of Completion. Students share one highlight. Teacher previews how Career Plans connect to 8th-to-9th grade registration.

**EXIT TICKET: Name the ONE most important thing you learned about yourself this year. (d(8)(A), d(8)(C))**

## Formative Assessment

- H&L Career Plan completeness (Day 1). (d(8)(A), d(8)(B))
- Written Career Plan quality (Day 2). (d(8)(C))
- Presentation delivery via rubric (Days 3-4). (d(4)(C))
- Career Plan download confirmation (Day 4). (d(8)(B))

## Summative Assessment

Three-part capstone assessment: (1) Written Career Plan with all sections (d(8)(A), d(8)(B), d(8)(C)). (2) Oral career plan presentation scored on content, organization, delivery, and growth (d(4)(C)). (3) End-of-Year Reflection demonstrating self-awareness growth. The H&L downloadable Career Plan is the official course artifact.

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Career plan template with sentence starters for every section.
- Allow note cards during capstone presentation.
- Option to present to teacher individually rather than full class.
- Simplified reflection with guided questions.

### Extensions

- Create a visual career roadmap poster from 8th grade to age 25.
- Write a letter to future self (to open in 12th grade).
- Create a digital portfolio of the year's best work.

### ELL Language Support

- Bilingual End-of-Year Reflection with Spanish prompts.
- Career plan presentation can mix English and Spanish.
- My Career Journey final update in bilingual format.
- Bilingual Certificates of Completion.
