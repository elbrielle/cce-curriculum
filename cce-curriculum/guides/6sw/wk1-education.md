## Lesson Objective

Students will explore the Education & Training career cluster through Hats & Ladders, compare traditional and alternative certification pathways for teaching, explain the value of community service and volunteerism as career preparation, and investigate how the eDynamic Unit 7 concept of adaptability applies to education careers.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe at least three Education & Training careers with their certification pathways, explain the difference between a traditional teaching degree and alternative certification, and give two examples of how community service builds career-relevant skills."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(1)(B):** Explore and describe the CTE career clusters.
- **d(1)(C):** Identify various career opportunities within the Education & Training cluster.
- **d(2)(A):** Research and describe applicable academic, certification, and training requirements.
- **d(2)(B):** Use available resources to evaluate educational and training options.
- **d(4)(E):** Explain the value of community service and volunteerism.

## Career Connection

Education professionals shape every other career field -- every doctor, engineer, lawyer, and entrepreneur had a teacher who helped them get there. In Texas, there are two main pathways into teaching: the traditional route (4-year Education degree + student teaching) and the alternative certification route (any bachelor's degree + alt-cert program). Irving ISD offers the Teaching & Training pathway at all high schools, leading to an Educational Aide certification, and Early Childhood Education at Cardwell/Ratteree, leading to a Child Development Associate credential. Education is also uniquely connected to community service -- a core value of the profession.


**Irving ISD Pathway: Teaching & Training (District Wide) leads to Educational Aide certification. Early Childhood Education (Cardwell/Ratteree) leads to Child Development Associate. Both pathways provide entry into education careers.**

## Vocabulary

- **Alternative Certification:** A pathway to becoming a teacher without a traditional Education degree. Requires a bachelor's degree in any field plus completion of an approved certification program.
- **Student Teaching:** A semester-long supervised classroom experience required as part of a traditional Education degree.
- **Community Service:** Unpaid work done to benefit the community. Community service builds career skills like leadership, communication, and empathy.
- **Educational Aide:** A paraprofessional who assists teachers in the classroom. Irving ISD's Teaching & Training pathway leads to this certification.
- **TEA:** Texas Education Agency -- the state organization that oversees public education and teacher certification in Texas.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In H&L, the Education & Training cluster includes pathways for Teaching/Training, Professional Support Services (Counselor, Librarian), and Administration. Students can explore Hats like Teacher, School Counselor, Principal, Instructional Designer, and Early Childhood Educator. The eDynamic Unit 7 connection is about adaptability -- education professionals must constantly adapt to new technologies, curriculum changes, and student needs. This is also the primary week for d(4)(E) community service, since teaching is fundamentally a service profession.


## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Think-Pair-Share:** After researching traditional vs. alternative certification, students think about which pathway appeals more to them, pair to discuss, and share reasoning with the class. This normalizes the idea that there are multiple valid pathways into teaching.
- **Stop and Jot:** During the H&L cluster tour, students pause twice to note one education career they did not know existed and one question they have about the field.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: Education & Training Cluster Tour

**WARM-UP: Name your favorite teacher you have ever had. What made them a great teacher? Was it their knowledge, their personality, or something else?**

#### H&L Education Cluster Tour (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "We are starting the LAST six weeks of the year, and we are beginning with a career cluster that is very personal to me: Education and Training. These are the people who teach, counsel, lead, and support students like you. Open H&L and explore the Education and Training cluster."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore the Education and Training cluster in H&L. The workbook (Ch 6) lists two pathways: Early Learning ("Teach young children in preschool and early elementary school") and Teaching and Training ("Teach students of all ages, create learning materials, coach individuals or groups"). Key workbook activities: "Community Classroom" (designing learning environments), "Job Search" (job search skills), and "Teaching Toolbox" (creating instructional materials). Students explore the cluster in the app.

#### Education Career Profiles (15 min)

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore Hats: Teacher (various levels), School Counselor, Principal, Instructional Designer, Early Childhood Educator, Librarian. They check education requirements, salary data, and demand levels.

**DOK 2: How would you compare the education requirements for becoming a high school teacher versus becoming a school principal? What additional steps are needed for leadership roles?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name two Education & Training careers and describe one surprising thing you learned about each. (d(1)(B), d(1)(C))**

### Day 2: Traditional vs. Alternative Certification

**WARM-UP: Did you know you can become a teacher WITHOUT an Education degree? What do you think of that?**

#### Two Pathways Into Teaching (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Here is something most students do not know: there are TWO main ways to become a teacher in Texas. The traditional way is to major in Education in college, do student teaching, and pass the certification exam. The alternative way is to get a bachelor's degree in ANY field -- math, science, history, art, anything -- and then complete an alternative certification program. Both pathways lead to the same teaching certification."

Teacher presents both pathways side-by-side: Traditional (4-year Education degree, student teaching semester, TExES exam) vs. Alternative Certification (any bachelor's degree, alt-cert program like iTeach or Texas Teachers, classroom experience, TExES exam). Students research using TEA certification website.

#### Pathway Comparison Worksheet (20 min)

Students complete the Education Pathway Comparison worksheet. They compare: timeline, cost, when you start earning, pros and cons of each. They also research what subjects have the most teacher shortages in Texas (math, science, special education, bilingual).

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about why Texas offers alternative certification? What problem is it trying to solve, and does it work?**

**EXIT TICKET: Describe the two main pathways into teaching in Texas and name one advantage of each. (d(2)(A), d(2)(B))**

### Day 3: Community Service and Career Skills

**WARM-UP: Have you ever volunteered or helped someone without getting paid? What did you do? What did you learn from it?**

#### Why Community Service Matters (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "The TEKS for this course say you need to explain the value of community service and volunteerism. Here is why it matters for YOUR career: every time you volunteer, you are building skills that employers value. Tutoring a younger student builds communication and patience. Helping at a food bank builds teamwork and empathy. Organizing a school event builds leadership and project management. Community service is not just about being nice -- it is career preparation."

Teacher presents 5-6 examples of community service activities and the transferable skills each one builds. Discussion: How does community service connect specifically to Education careers? (Teaching IS a service profession.)

#### Community Service Reflection (25 min)

Students complete the Community Service Reflection handout. They list community service or volunteer experiences they have had (including school activities like TSA, tutoring, mentoring). For each, they identify the transferable skill it built. Then they write a reflection connecting community service to their career interests.

**DOK 4: Create an argument for why community service experience should be weighted as heavily as grades when evaluating college or job applications. Use specific examples of skills gained through service to support your argument.**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one community service experience you have had and identify the career skill it helped you develop. (d(4)(E))**

### Day 4: eDynamic Unit 7 + Teaching in a Changing World

**WARM-UP: How has school changed since your parents were students? Name two differences.**

#### eDynamic Unit 7: Progressive and Adaptable (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today we are working through eDynamic Unit 7. This unit is about careers that are constantly adapting to new technology and changing needs. Education is the PERFECT example -- think about how teaching has changed with technology, online learning, and AI tools. The professionals in education must be progressive and adaptable."

Students work through eDynamic Unit 7 content. The unit covers Agriculture, Energy, and Health Science clusters through the lens of adaptability, but teacher guides students to apply the adaptability concept to Education careers specifically.

> [VERIFY IN eDynamic] Confirm the specific activities and content within eDynamic Unit 7. The unit description focuses on Agriculture, Energy, and Health Science as 'progressive and adaptable' fields. Teacher should bridge the adaptability concept to Education careers.

#### Education + Technology Discussion (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "How is technology changing education right now? AI tutors, virtual reality field trips, online courses, adaptive learning software -- the teaching profession looks different every year. This is why adaptability is the most important skill for future educators."


**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about why a teacher who graduated 20 years ago needs different skills than a teacher graduating today? What specific technology skills would a new teacher need?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one way technology is changing education and describe the skill a teacher would need to adapt to that change. (d(1)(C))**

### Day 5: Education Favorites + Irving ISD Pathways

**WARM-UP: Could you see yourself as a teacher? A counselor? A principal? Why or why not?**

#### Irving ISD Education Pathways (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Irving ISD offers two pathways that connect to Education careers. Teaching and Training is available at ALL high schools and leads to the Educational Aide certification. Early Childhood Education is at Cardwell and Ratteree and leads to the Child Development Associate credential. Both give you real classroom experience while you are still in high school."

Teacher presents the specific Irving ISD education pathways: courses, certifications, and how they connect to college education programs.

#### H&L Favorites + Reflection (25 min)

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students favorite Education & Training careers in H&L. They update Building Blocks with tutoring, mentoring, and volunteer experiences. They review how their RIASEC type (especially Social type) connects to education careers.

**DOK 2: How would you describe the connection between the Social RIASEC type and Education careers? Can people with OTHER RIASEC types also succeed in education?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name the education career you find most interesting and explain whether the District Wide Teaching & Training pathway or the Cardwell ECE pathway appeals to you more. (d(1)(C), d(2)(A))**

## Formative Assessment

- Stop and Jot from cluster tour (Day 1). (d(1)(B))

- Community Service Reflection completeness (Day 3). (d(4)(E))
- eDynamic Unit 7 progress (Day 4). (d(1)(C))

## Summative Assessment

Community Service Reflection + Education Pathway Comparison (Day 5): Students submit both artifacts. Scored on: education career knowledge (d(1)(B), d(1)(C)), pathway comparison quality (d(2)(A), d(2)(B)), and community service connection to career skills (d(4)(E)).

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide sentence starters for the community service reflection: 'When I volunteered at _____, I built the skill of _____ because _____.
- Offer a simplified pathway comparison with pre-filled data for one pathway.
- Allow students to describe hypothetical community service if they have limited real experience.

### Extensions

- Students research the teacher shortage in Texas and propose one solution based on what they learned about alternative certification.
- Interview a teacher at Bowie Middle School about their career pathway and share findings with the class.
- Compare teaching salaries in Texas to three other states and analyze whether pay affects teacher supply.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Education = Educacion, Certification = Certificacion, Community Service = Servicio comunitario, Volunteer = Voluntario.
- Bilingual community service reflection handout with Spanish sentence stems.
- Education careers have strong visual and relatable context for ESL students (they interact with teachers daily).
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers during pathway comparison research.
