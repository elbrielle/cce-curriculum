## Lesson Objective

Students will explore the Arts, A/V Technology & Communications career cluster through Hats & Ladders, investigate graphic design and digital media careers, write their first resume using Xello, begin learning the steps of an effective job search, and create a design portfolio piece using Canva or Adobe Express.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe at least three careers in the Arts/AV cluster, write a resume that includes my education, skills, activities, and career objective, and identify the first three steps of an effective job search."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(7)(A):** Write a resume.
- **d(1)(C):** Identify various career opportunities within the Arts/AV cluster.
- **d(6)(A):** Identify steps of an effective job search.

## Career Connection

Graphic design and digital communication careers are everywhere in our visual world -- every website, app, advertisement, social media post, and brand logo was created by a designer. In the DFW area, companies of all sizes hire graphic designers, animators, video editors, UX designers, and social media managers. Irving ISD offers Digital Communications and Graphic Design pathways at all high schools, leading to the Adobe Certified Professional credential. This week also launches the job-readiness skills that carry through the rest of the six weeks: resume writing (d(7)(A)) and job search introduction (d(6)(A)).


**Irving ISD Pathway: Digital Communications (District Wide) and Graphic Design (District Wide) both lead to Adobe Certified Professional certification. Available at all Irving ISD high schools.**

## Vocabulary

- **Graphic Designer:** A professional who creates visual content for print, digital, and social media using tools like Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, and InDesign.
- **Resume:** A one-page document summarizing your education, skills, activities, and career objective. Used when applying for jobs, internships, or college programs.
- **Portfolio:** A collection of work samples that demonstrates a designer's skills and style. Essential for creative careers.
- **Job Search:** The systematic process of finding employment, including identifying openings, preparing application materials, and networking.
- **UX Designer:** A User Experience Designer who focuses on how people interact with websites, apps, and digital products. One of the fastest-growing design careers.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In H&L, the Arts, A/V Technology & Communications cluster includes pathways for Visual Arts, A/V Technology, and Performing Arts. Students can explore Hats like Graphic Designer, Animator, Video Editor, UX Designer, Photographer, and Social Media Manager. This is also a pivotal week because it bridges CAREER EXPLORATION (what do I want to do?) with JOB READINESS (how do I present myself?). The resume students write this week in Xello becomes a living document they will refine through the end of the year. eDynamic Unit 8 introduces job search steps and personal characteristics for career success. Unit 6 covers Arts/AV and Hospitality clusters.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Modeling:** Teacher models a complete middle school resume on the projector using Xello before students begin independently. Walk through each section: header, career objective, education, skills, activities/awards, and community service. Show students that they have MORE to put on a resume than they think.
- **Chunking:** Split the week clearly: Days 1-2 = Arts/AV career exploration + eDynamic, Days 3-4 = resume writing + job search intro, Day 5 = design portfolio piece. This prevents students from feeling overwhelmed by covering both career exploration AND job documents in one week.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: Arts, A/V Technology & Communications Cluster

**WARM-UP: Open your phone's camera roll (or think about it). Who took most of the photos and videos on there? You are already creating visual content. Could that become a career?**

#### H&L Arts/AV Cluster Tour (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "This week is one of my favorites because it combines TWO big things: exploring creative careers AND writing your first resume. Let us start with the careers. Open H&L and navigate to the Arts, AV Technology, and Communications cluster. This is the cluster for people who create the visual world around us."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore the Arts, Audio & Visual Technology, and Communications cluster in H&L. The workbook (Ch 4) lists three pathways: Graphic Design and Multimedia Arts ("Design graphics, clothes, accessories; create special effects, animations, and visuals for games, movies, music videos, and ads"), Digital Communications ("Use social media, videos, and sound to connect with others"), and Printing & Imaging. Key workbook activities: "Digital Storytelling," "Game On!" (game design), and "Creative Entrepreneurs" (rebranding a fashion, videography, or photography business). Students use the Hat Finder to explore Hats: Graphic Designer, Animator, Video Editor, UX Designer, Photographer, Social Media Manager.

#### From the Field Interviews + Salary Research (15 min)

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students watch From the Field interviews for design careers and pull DFW salary data for 3+ Arts/AV careers. (The workbook's Hat Research template (Ch 4: Arts, A/V Technology & Communications) provides a structured sheet for recording salary and demand data from the app.)

**DOK 2: How would you compare the daily work of a Graphic Designer versus a UX Designer? Which role focuses more on visual creativity and which focuses more on problem-solving?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name two Arts/AV careers and describe one thing each professional creates or produces. (d(1)(C))**

### Day 2: eDynamic Units 6 + 8 + Design Career Deep-Dive

**WARM-UP: If you could create any visual content for a living -- logos, movies, video games, websites, social media -- what would you choose?**


#### eDynamic Unit 6: Entertaining and Exploring (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "eDynamic Unit 6 explores the Arts/AV/Communication cluster and the Hospitality & Tourism cluster. Today focus on the Arts/AV content -- it will deepen what you explored in H&L yesterday."

Students work through the Arts/AV portion of eDynamic Unit 6. The unit covers career pathways in Arts, AV Technology & Communication and Hospitality & Tourism, including workplaces, tasks, required skills, and training.

> [VERIFY IN eDynamic] Confirm which specific activities within eDynamic Unit 6 cover Arts/AV careers versus Hospitality. Direct students to the Arts/AV content specifically.

#### eDynamic Unit 8: Job Search Introduction (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Now switch to eDynamic Unit 8. This unit covers how to connect your interests to career choices AND introduces the job search process. We are starting the job-readiness portion of the course that will carry through the rest of the year."

Students work through the job search and personal characteristics sections of eDynamic Unit 8. The unit helps students understand how personal assessment of interests connects with jobs, the importance of job search steps, and how personal characteristics lead to career success.

> [VERIFY IN eDynamic] Confirm which sections of eDynamic Unit 8 cover job search steps versus career pathway selection. Students should focus on job search and professional skills content this week.

**EXIT TICKET: Name two steps of an effective job search that you learned from eDynamic today. (d(6)(A))**

### Day 3: Resume Writing: You Have More Than You Think

**WARM-UP: If someone asked you to list everything you are good at, every activity you participate in, and every accomplishment you have had -- how long would the list be? Try it.**


#### Teacher Resume Modeling (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today you are writing your first resume. I know what you are thinking: 'I am in middle school. I have nothing to put on a resume.' WRONG. You have education, skills, activities, awards, community service, and career interests. Let me show you on the projector how to pull all of that together into a professional document."

Teacher models a complete middle school resume on the projector using Xello (or printed template): Name/Contact Info, Career Objective (1 sentence about career goal), Education (school, grade, GPA if strong), Skills (technology skills, languages, soft skills), Activities & Awards (TSA, sports, clubs, honor roll), Community Service. Show students that middle schoolers DO have resume content.

#### Xello Resume Builder (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Open Xello and go to the Resume Builder. Xello will walk you through each section. Fill in everything honestly. If you are not sure about something, raise your hand. Your resume does not have to be perfect today -- we will keep improving it."

Students log into Xello and use the Resume Builder feature to create their first resume. Teacher circulates helping students identify skills and activities they may be undervaluing. Students who finish early can also use CareerOneStop Resume Guide as a reference.

**DOK 2: How would you describe the purpose of a resume? Who reads it and what are they looking for?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name three things you included on your resume today. Which section was the hardest to fill in and why? (d(7)(A))**

### Day 4: Resume Refinement + Job Search Steps

**WARM-UP: If you were hiring someone for your dream job, what would you look for on their resume?**

#### Resume Peer Review (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Trade Chromebooks with your partner. Read their resume like you are an employer. Give them two stars (things that are strong) and one wish (something to improve). Be specific and kind."

Students exchange resumes and provide written feedback using the Two Stars and a Wish format. Teacher circulates ensuring feedback is constructive and specific.

#### Resume Revision (10 min)

Students revise their resumes based on peer feedback. They strengthen weak sections and fix any formatting issues.

#### Job Search Steps Review (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "A resume is just one tool. You also need to know HOW to search for a job. Let me walk you through the basic steps: (1) Know what you want -- use your career exploration data, (2) Search for openings -- job boards, company websites, networking, (3) Prepare your materials -- resume, cover letter, references, (4) Apply, (5) Interview, (6) Follow up. We will practice steps 3-6 over the next few weeks."

Teacher presents the 6 steps of an effective job search. Students take notes. Connect each step to tools they already know: H&L Career Planner for step 1, CareerOneStop for step 2, Xello for step 3.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about why 'knowing what you want' is listed as the FIRST step of the job search? How does the career exploration you have done all year prepare you for this step?**

**EXIT TICKET: List the 6 steps of an effective job search in order. Which step do you feel most prepared for? (d(6)(A))**

### Day 5: Design Portfolio Piece + Cluster Favorites

**WARM-UP: Professional designers have portfolios showing their best work. If you had to show ONE thing you created this year to an employer, what would it be?**

#### Canva/Adobe Express Design Challenge (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "For the rest of class, you are a Graphic Designer. Your client has asked you to create a professional social media graphic, a logo, or a poster for a cause you care about. Use Canva or Adobe Express. This piece goes in your portfolio -- think of it as your first professional design work."

Students use Canva for Education or Adobe Express to create a design portfolio piece. Options: personal brand logo, social media graphic for a cause, event poster for a school activity, or infographic about a career they explored this year. Teacher connects the activity to what real graphic designers do daily.

#### H&L Favorites (15 min)

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students favorite Arts/AV careers in H&L and update Building Blocks with design skills and digital media experiences. (Ch 4: Arts, A/V Technology & Communications provides the career profiles for favoriting; Ch 16: My Next Steps provides the Building Blocks template.)

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about why a portfolio is MORE important than a resume for creative careers like Graphic Design? What does a portfolio prove that a resume cannot?**

**EXIT TICKET: Describe the design piece you created today, who the audience is, and which Arts/AV career would create this type of work professionally. (d(1)(C))**

## Formative Assessment

- H&L Arts/AV exploration and From the Field notes (Day 1). (d(1)(C))
- eDynamic Units 6 and 8 progress (Day 2). (d(6)(A))
- Resume completeness check in Xello (Day 3). (d(7)(A))
- Peer review quality and resume revision (Day 4). (d(7)(A))

## Summative Assessment

Resume (Xello) + Design Portfolio Piece (Day 5): Students submit their completed Xello resume and their Canva/Adobe Express design piece. Scored on: resume completeness and professionalism (d(7)(A)), Arts/AV career connection in design piece (d(1)(C)), and job search steps knowledge (d(6)(A)).

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide a printed resume template with section headers and sentence starters for students who struggle with Xello.
- Offer pre-made Canva templates that students can customize rather than designing from scratch.
- Allow students to complete the resume in bullet-point format if full sentences are too challenging.

### Extensions

- Students create a complete personal brand package: logo, resume, and social media banner all in a consistent visual style.
- Research the Adobe Certified Professional exam topics and create a study plan for the District Wide pathway.
- Compare the career trajectories of a Graphic Designer versus a UX Designer over 10 years using BLS data.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Resume = Curriculum vitae / Resume, Portfolio = Portafolio, Graphic Design = Diseno grafico, Job Search = Busqueda de empleo.
- Bilingual resume template with Spanish section headers and sentence stems for career objective.
- Canva and Adobe Express have multilingual interface options. Show ESL students how to switch if helpful.
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers during resume peer review to ensure feedback is understood.
