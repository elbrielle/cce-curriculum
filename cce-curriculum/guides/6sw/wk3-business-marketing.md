## Lesson Objective

Students will explore the Marketing career cluster through Hats & Ladders, investigate business and marketing careers, analyze how changing economic conditions create and eliminate marketing jobs, connect entrepreneurship concepts to marketing careers, and use Google Applied Digital Skills to practice real-world marketing skills.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe at least three marketing careers, explain how economic changes affect marketing job availability, and create a basic marketing plan that demonstrates entrepreneurship principles."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(1)(C):** Identify various career opportunities within the Marketing cluster.
- **d(3)(I):** Define entrepreneurship and identify entrepreneurial opportunities in marketing.
- **d(5)(C):** Analyze effects of changing societal needs and economic conditions on employment trends and career choices.

## Career Connection

Marketing is the engine that drives every business. Without marketing, even the best product fails because nobody knows about it. In the DFW area, marketing professionals work at corporations (AT&T, American Airlines, Toyota), agencies (hundreds of marketing and advertising firms), and as freelancers/entrepreneurs. The Marketing cluster includes traditional roles (Marketing Manager, Brand Manager) and rapidly growing digital roles (Social Media Manager, Content Creator, SEO Specialist, Digital Marketing Analyst). Irving ISD offers the Business & Marketing pathway at all high schools, and Sales Management at Singley and Nimitz, both leading to the Entrepreneurship & Small Business certification.

**Irving ISD Pathway: Business & Marketing (District Wide) and Sales Management (Singley/Nimitz) both lead to Entrepreneurship & Small Business certification.**

## Vocabulary

- **Marketing:** The process of promoting, selling, and distributing a product or service. Includes advertising, market research, branding, and social media.
- **Market Research:** The process of gathering information about consumers' needs and preferences to guide business decisions.
- **Brand Manager:** A professional responsible for maintaining and growing a company's brand identity and reputation.
- **SEO (Search Engine Optimization):** The practice of optimizing website content to rank higher in search engine results. A growing digital marketing specialty.
- **Economic Conditions:** The state of the economy at a given time, including factors like employment rates, consumer spending, and business growth that affect career availability.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In H&L, the Marketing cluster includes career profiles for Marketing Manager, Social Media Specialist, Market Research Analyst, Brand Manager, and Advertising Account Executive. This week connects back to the entrepreneurship concepts from the 3rd Six Weeks -- many marketing professionals work as freelancers or start their own agencies, making this a natural fit for d(3)(I). The eDynamic Unit 4 covers Business Management & Administration, Finance, and Marketing/Sales/Service clusters, helping students classify pathways and examine required skills. Google Applied Digital Skills provides free, practical lessons on digital marketing that supplement the career exploration.


## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Jigsaw:** Assign each team a different marketing role (Marketing Manager, Social Media Manager, Market Research Analyst, Brand Manager, SEO Specialist). Teams research and present their role, so every student learns about multiple marketing careers.
- **Sentence Stems:** For the economic conditions analysis: 'When the economy is strong, marketing jobs _____ because _____. When the economy slows down, marketing jobs _____ because _____. This means that marketing professionals need to _____.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: Marketing Cluster Exploration

**WARM-UP: Name a commercial, social media ad, or brand logo that you remember. What made it stick in your mind? Someone's JOB was to create that.**

#### H&L Marketing Cluster Tour (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "This week we are exploring the business side of the creative world: Marketing. Every product, brand, and company you interact with has a marketing team behind it. Open H&L and navigate to the Marketing cluster."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Note: H&L does not have a separate "Marketing" cluster. Marketing careers fall under the Business, Marketing, and Finance cluster (Ch 5). The confirmed Marketing and Sales pathway is described as: "Finding ways to tell people about products or services and encouraging them to buy." The workbook's "Marketing on the Move" activity (Ch 5) has students design billboard or bus wrap advertisements. Students use the Hat Finder to explore Hats: Marketing Manager, Social Media Specialist, Market Research Analyst, Brand Manager, Advertising Account Executive.

#### Digital Marketing Careers (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Some of the fastest-growing marketing careers did not exist 15 years ago: Social Media Manager, SEO Specialist, Content Creator, Influencer Marketing Manager. These are careers your parents never heard of when they were your age. Let us explore how technology has created entirely new marketing jobs."

**DOK 2: How would you compare traditional marketing (TV commercials, billboards) to digital marketing (social media, SEO, email)? Which is growing faster and why?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name two traditional and two digital marketing careers. Which type do you think will be more in-demand in 10 years? (d(1)(C))**

### Day 2: eDynamic Unit 4 + Economic Conditions

**WARM-UP: When the economy is doing well, do companies spend MORE or LESS on advertising? Why?**

#### eDynamic Unit 4: Enterprise and Money Matters (20 min)

Students work through eDynamic Unit 4 content. The unit covers Business Management & Administration, Finance, and Marketing/Sales/Service clusters, helping students classify career pathways, learn about required skills, and examine training requirements.

> [VERIFY IN eDynamic] Confirm which activities within eDynamic Unit 4 specifically address Marketing careers versus Business/Finance. Direct students to marketing-relevant content.

#### Economic Conditions and Marketing Jobs (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Here is a big concept: the economy affects which jobs are available. When the economy is booming, companies hire more marketing people to promote their growth. When the economy slows down, marketing budgets are often the first thing cut. This means marketing professionals need to be adaptable and have diverse skills."

Students research how economic changes affect marketing employment. They analyze: What happens to marketing jobs during a recession? Which marketing roles are most recession-proof? How has the shift to digital marketing changed job stability? Use BLS projections and current economic data.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about why a marketing professional with BOTH traditional and digital skills is more resilient to economic changes than someone with only one skill set?**

**EXIT TICKET: Explain how a strong economy versus a weak economy affects marketing job availability differently. (d(5)(C))**

### Day 3: Entrepreneurship in Marketing

**WARM-UP: Could you start a marketing business from your bedroom with just a laptop and an internet connection? What would you need to know?**

#### Marketing Entrepreneurship Discussion (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Marketing is one of the EASIEST fields to start your own business in. A social media management company, a freelance graphic design studio, a content creation agency -- all of these can be started with minimal startup costs. You just need skills, a laptop, and clients. This is entrepreneurship in action."

Teacher presents examples of marketing entrepreneurship: freelance social media management, starting a marketing agency, content creation businesses, affiliate marketing. Connect to the entrepreneurship concepts from the 3rd Six Weeks Glowforge project.

#### Mini Marketing Plan (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "You are going to create a Mini Marketing Plan for a product or service. It can be real or imagined. Your plan needs: Target audience (who are you selling to?), Key message (what do you want them to know?), Channels (how will you reach them -- social media, email, ads, events?), and Budget (how much would it cost?)."

Students complete the Mini Marketing Plan template. They apply entrepreneurship principles to create a realistic marketing strategy for a product or service of their choice.

**DOK 4: If you were starting a marketing business and could only choose ONE marketing channel (social media, email, paid ads, or events), which would you choose and what evidence supports it being the most effective for a new business?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name the product or service in your marketing plan and describe your target audience and one marketing channel you would use. (d(3)(I))**

### Day 4: Google Applied Digital Skills

**WARM-UP: What is ONE digital marketing skill you could learn today that would make you more employable in 5 years?**

#### Google Applied Digital Skills Lesson (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "Google offers free lessons on real-world digital skills that marketing professionals use every day. Today you are going to complete one of these lessons. Choose from: creating a website, building a presentation to pitch a product, or organizing a marketing project with digital tools."

Students log into Google Applied Digital Skills (applieddigitalskills.withgoogle.com) and complete a marketing-related lesson. Teacher selects 2-3 appropriate lessons in advance. Options include: 'Plan and Create a Website,' 'Organize a Project with Digital Tools,' or 'Design a Presentation to Pitch Your Idea.' Students work at their own pace.

**DOK 2: How would you describe how the digital skill you practiced today connects to a specific marketing career?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name the Google Applied Digital Skills lesson you completed and explain which marketing career uses this skill. (d(1)(C))**

### Day 5: Marketing Presentations + Cluster Favorites

**WARM-UP: If you could work at any company's marketing department in DFW, which company would you choose and why?**

#### Mini Marketing Plan Presentations (25 min)

Students present their Mini Marketing Plans to the class: product/service, target audience, key message, marketing channels, and budget. Class votes on the most creative plan, the most realistic plan, and the plan most likely to succeed. 2 minutes per student.

#### H&L Favorites + Reflection (15 min)

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students favorite Marketing careers in H&L and update Building Blocks with marketing skills (social media, design, communication, data analysis). (Ch 5: Business, Marketing & Finance provides the Marketing and Sales career profiles for favoriting; Ch 16: My Next Steps provides the Building Blocks template.)

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about why marketing requires BOTH creative skills (design, writing) AND analytical skills (data, research)? Which side appeals more to you?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name the marketing career you find most interesting and explain whether it leans more creative or more analytical. (d(1)(C), d(3)(I))**

## Formative Assessment

- H&L Marketing cluster exploration completion (Day 1). (d(1)(C))
- eDynamic Unit 4 progress and economic conditions analysis (Day 2). (d(5)(C))
- Mini Marketing Plan quality (Day 3). (d(3)(I))
- Google Applied Digital Skills lesson completion (Day 4). (d(1)(C))

## Summative Assessment

Mini Marketing Plan Presentation (Day 5): Students present their marketing plan. Scored on: marketing career knowledge (d(1)(C)), entrepreneurship connection (d(3)(I)), and economic conditions awareness (d(5)(C)).

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide a pre-filled marketing plan example for a simple product (lemonade stand) so students see the format.
- Offer 3 pre-selected Google Applied Digital Skills lessons to prevent students from spending too long choosing.
- Allow students to present their marketing plan in written format rather than orally if needed.

### Extensions

- Students create a full social media campaign (3 posts) for their marketing plan product using Canva.
- Research a real DFW marketing agency and present on what services they offer and what careers they employ.
- Compare the marketing industry in DFW to Austin or Houston using BLS data and company presence.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Marketing = Mercadotecnia, Brand = Marca, Target Audience = Publico objetivo, Entrepreneurship = Emprendimiento.
- Mini Marketing Plan template with bilingual headers and sentence stems.
- Google Applied Digital Skills has visual, step-by-step lessons accessible across language levels.
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers during marketing plan presentations.
