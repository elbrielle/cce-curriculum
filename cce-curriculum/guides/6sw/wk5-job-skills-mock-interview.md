## Lesson Objective

Students will complete the full job-seeking skill set: identify job search steps, write a cover letter, complete a sample job application, learn reference protocol, and participate in a mock interview demonstrating professional appearance and communication.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can complete a job application, write a cover letter, select appropriate references, and participate in a mock interview with professional appearance and communication."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(6)(A):** Identify the steps of an effective job search.
- **d(6)(B):** Describe appropriate appearance for an interview.
- **d(6)(C):** Participate in a mock interview.
- **d(7)(B):** Write business correspondence including a cover letter and thank-you letter.
- **d(7)(C):** Complete sample job applications.
- **d(7)(D):** Describe the protocol for selecting and using references.

## Career Connection

This is the HEAVIEST TEKS week of the entire course with six standards: d(6)(A-C) job search and interview, d(7)(B-D) professional documents. These are universal life skills. Cover letters, applications, references, and mock interviews are the same processes adults use throughout their careers. By practicing now, students build confidence for decades ahead.

**Irving ISD Pathway: Administrative Management (Singley) provides direct job skills training. This week's content applies to ALL Irving ISD CTE pathways.**

## Vocabulary

- **Cover Letter:** A one-page letter sent with a resume introducing yourself and explaining why you are interested in a position.
- **Job Application:** A formal document providing an employer with personal information, work history, education, and references.
- **References:** People who can speak positively about your character, skills, and work ethic to a potential employer.
- **Mock Interview:** A practice interview simulating a real job interview for rehearsal and feedback.
- **Thank-You Letter:** A brief letter sent within 24 hours of an interview expressing gratitude and reaffirming interest.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

H&L is lighter this week because the TEKS require hands-on practice with job documents and interviewing. Students can explore the Administrative Services pathway in H&L for career context, but the main activities are offline: cover letters, applications, references, and mock interviews. CareerOneStop provides free practice applications and interview prep tools.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Modeling:** Teacher models every document on the projector: cover letter, job application, and a fishbowl mock interview. Students see the complete process before doing it themselves.
- **Fishbowl:** One pair conducts their mock interview in front of the class while others observe and score using the rubric. This provides a model before all students practice.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: Job Search Steps + Business Pathway

**WARM-UP: Imagine you are 18 and looking for your first job. What is the very first thing you would do?**

#### 7-Step Job Search Process (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "This week we go ALL IN on job skills. The 7 steps: (1) Self-assessment, (2) Research openings, (3) Prepare resume and cover letter, (4) Complete applications, (5) Prepare for interviews, (6) Interview, (7) Follow up with thank-you note."

Teacher reviews each step in detail. Connect H&L Career Plan (self-assessment) and Xello resume (preparation) to the first three steps.

#### H&L Administrative Services (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Let us also explore Administrative Services careers: Office Manager, Executive Assistant, HR Specialist."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore Administrative/Business Management Hats within the Business, Marketing, and Finance cluster (Ch 5). The workbook confirms Business Management as a pathway: "Planning and organizing how business runs to make sure everything works smoothly." The workbook also includes "Job Applications" (Ch 12, p. 196, practice filling out a standard application for "Future Work, Inc.") and "Job Interviews" + "Practicing for a Job Interview" (Ch 11) -- use these workbook activities to support mock interview prep.

**EXIT TICKET: List the 7 steps of an effective job search in order. (d(6)(A))**

### Day 2: Cover Letter Writing

**WARM-UP: A cover letter tells an employer WHY they should read your resume. If you had 3 sentences, what would you say?**

#### Cover Letter Modeling + Writing (40 min)

> **Teacher:** "Watch me write a cover letter for a sample job posting. Then you write yours using the template."

Teacher models all sections on projector: heading, greeting, paragraph 1 (why writing), paragraph 2 (why qualified), paragraph 3 (call to action), closing. Students write their own using the template for a career-related job posting.

**DOK 3: Why is a cover letter important even when an employer does not require one?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name the three paragraphs in a cover letter and what each includes. (d(7)(B))**

### Day 3: Job Applications + References

**WARM-UP: Have you ever filled out a detailed form? A job application is like that but more detailed.**

#### Sample Job Application (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "You are filling out a real job application. Take your time and be neat -- sloppy applications get thrown away."

Students complete the printed Sample Job Application with all fields. Teacher models one section at a time.

**DOK 2: Why does an employer care about the NEATNESS of your application?**

#### References Protocol (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "References: (1) Choose teachers, coaches, mentors -- not family. (2) ALWAYS ask permission first. (3) Give them a heads-up about what job you applied for."

Students identify 2-3 potential references and practice writing a permission request.

**DOK 3: Why should you NEVER list someone as a reference without asking first?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name two people you could list as references and explain why each would speak well of you. (d(7)(C), d(7)(D))**

### Day 4: Mock Interview Prep + Fishbowl Demo

**WARM-UP: What is the scariest part of a job interview? Write it down -- we will practice until it is not scary.**


#### Top 10 Interview Questions (15 min)

Teacher presents the most common questions (from CareerOneStop): Tell me about yourself, Why do you want this job, Strengths, Weaknesses, Problem-solving example. Students write bullet-point answers for 2-3 questions.

#### Fishbowl Mock Interview (20 min)

Teacher demonstrates: volunteer interviews teacher, modeling greeting, eye contact, posture, clear answers, closing. Class scores using rubric. Then reverse -- student interviewee, teacher as interviewer.


**DOK 2: What body language and communication skills made the fishbowl interview feel professional?**

**EXIT TICKET: Write your answer to: Tell me about yourself. Keep it under 30 seconds. (d(6)(B), d(6)(C))**

### Day 5: Mock Interview Day

**WARM-UP: Review your answers. Eye contact, clear voice, sit up straight, smile.**

#### Mock Interviews (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "You and your partner take turns. 5-7 minutes each. Interviewer reads question cards and scores. Interviewee responds professionally. Then switch. Treat this like a real interview."

Students pair up for mock interviews using question cards and rubric. After both complete, they debrief together. Teacher circulates observing and providing feedback.

**DOK 4: Create a top 5 list of things that make or break a job interview. Rank and explain.**

#### Thank-You Letter (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "After EVERY interview, send a thank-you note within 24 hours."

Students write a brief thank-you letter to their mock interview partner, completing d(7)(B).

**EXIT TICKET: What are you most proud of from your mock interview? What would you improve? (d(6)(C))**

## Formative Assessment

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Sentence-by-sentence cover letter template with blanks.
- Simplified application with fewer fields.
- Allow written interview answers as reference during mock interview.
- Question cards with suggested answer frameworks on the back.

### Extensions

- Second mock interview with different partner for improvement comparison.
- Write a cover letter for a REAL summer job or volunteer position.
- Research video interview best practices.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Cover Letter = Carta de presentacion, Job Application = Solicitud de empleo, Mock Interview = Entrevista de practica.
- Bilingual cover letter template with Spanish starters.
- Question cards with Spanish translations on back.
- Allow ESL students to mock interview with bilingual partner.
