## Lesson Objective

Students will explore the Manufacturing career cluster through the Hats & Ladders platform, identify career opportunities in robotics and automated manufacturing, and apply introductory programming concepts using the Sphero RVR+ to simulate a factory floor task.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe at least three careers in the Manufacturing cluster, explain the training required for each, and program a Sphero robot to complete a simulated factory floor task."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(1)(B):** Explore and describe the CTE career clusters.
- **d(1)(C):** Identify various career opportunities within one or more career clusters.
- **d(2)(A):** Research and describe applicable academic, technical, certification, and training requirements for one or more careers in an identified career cluster.

## Materials Needed

- Chromebooks with internet access (1 per student)
- Hats & Ladders student accounts
- Sphero RVR+ robots (1 per team of 3-4 students)
- SpheroEDU app (pre-installed on Chromebooks or tablets)
- Painter's tape for factory floor course layout
- Printed "Manufacturing Career Profile" research sheet (1 per student)
- Projector for videos
- BLS Occupational Outlook Handbook - Manufacturing: https://www.bls.gov/ooh/production/home.htm

## Career Connection

Manufacturing is one of the fastest-evolving career fields in the United States. Modern factories look nothing like the assembly lines of 50 years ago -- today's manufacturing professionals work alongside robots, use computer-aided design, and operate multi-million dollar CNC machines. In the DFW area, advanced manufacturing is a major employer. At Singley Academy, Irving ISD offers a Robotics Manufacturing pathway leading to the FANUC Robot Operator certification, which is an industry-recognized credential that qualifies students for jobs straight out of high school.


**Irving ISD Pathway Link: Singley Academy: Robotics Manufacturing (FANUC Robot Operator certification). Students completing this pathway can earn an industry credential recognized by manufacturers worldwide.**

## Vocabulary

- **Manufacturing:** The process of converting raw materials into finished products, often using machinery and technology.
- **Automation:** The use of technology and machines to perform tasks with minimal human intervention.
- **CNC (Computer Numerical Control):** A manufacturing process where pre-programmed computer software controls the movement of factory tools and machinery.
- **Quality Control:** The process of ensuring products meet certain standards of quality through testing and inspection.
- **FANUC:** A leading manufacturer of industrial robots. Irving ISD offers FANUC Robot Operator certification at Singley Academy.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In Hats & Ladders, the Manufacturing cluster introduces students to pathways including Production, Manufacturing Production Process Development, and Quality Assurance. The platform's career profiles show students localized DFW salary and demand data for manufacturing careers, and the From the Field interview videos feature real manufacturing professionals discussing their daily work. Students will connect their RIASEC results from Week 0 to see if Manufacturing aligns with their personality type (typically strong for Realistic and Conventional types).

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Stop and Jot:** While watching the H&L Manufacturing cluster tour video, students pause at two key moments to jot down one new career they learned about and one question they have. Teacher collects these to guide discussion.
- **Active Monitoring:** During the Sphero programming activity on Days 3-4, teacher circulates with a clipboard checklist tracking: (1) all team members participating, (2) students connecting programming to manufacturing careers, (3) teams following the Engineering Design Process steps.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: The Manufacturing Cluster: More Than Assembly Lines

**WARM-UP: Name a product you used this morning (phone, toothbrush, shoes). How do you think it was made?**

#### H&L Manufacturing Cluster Tour (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Last week you set up your Climber Profile and took your assessments. This week, we are diving into our FIRST career cluster: Manufacturing. Open Hats and Ladders and navigate to the Manufacturing cluster."

> **Teacher:** "I want you to watch the cluster tour video first. While you watch, use Stop and Jot -- pause the video twice and write down one new career you learned about and one question you have."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students navigate to the Manufacturing cluster page. The H&L workbook (Ch 14) opens with "Exploring the World of Manufacturing," which describes Manufacturing careers and lists six pathways: Robotics & Automation Technology, Industrial Maintenance, Manufacturing Technology, Electronics Technology, Welding, and Advanced Manufacturing & Industrial Technology. The workbook includes a "Making Connections" pair activity where students brainstorm how an everyday product is manufactured. The workbook directs students to "Go to the Hats & Ladders app and click on the Manufacturing Cluster" to explore pathways for 15 minutes.

#### Class Discussion (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "What did you write down? Let us hear some of the careers you discovered and the questions you have. Who found a career that surprised them?"

**DOK 2: How would you summarize the difference between what you THOUGHT manufacturing was before the video and what you learned it actually involves?**

#### From the Field Interviews (10 min)

Students note what the professional says about their daily tasks, education, and what they wish they had known sooner.

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students watch 1-2 From the Field interview videos featuring real manufacturing professionals. These are short, ESL-friendly videos embedded in the H&L cluster page.

**EXIT TICKET: Name two careers in Manufacturing that you did NOT know existed before today. (Aligned to d(1)(B))**

### Day 2: Exploring Manufacturing Pathways and Hats

**WARM-UP: If you could build anything with unlimited technology and resources, what would you build and why?**

#### H&L Manufacturing Pathway Deep-Dive (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today you are going deeper into the Manufacturing cluster. Hats and Ladders has specific pathways within Manufacturing. I want you to explore at least TWO pathways and then look at the individual careers -- the Hats -- inside each one."

> **Teacher:** "For each Hat you explore, I want you to check three things: What education or training do you need? What is the salary in our area? And rate the perks, quirks, and job gear."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore Manufacturing pathways and select 2-3 individual Hats (careers) to research using the Hat Finder in the app. The six confirmed Manufacturing pathways are: Robotics & Automation Technology, Industrial Maintenance, Manufacturing Technology, Electronics Technology, Welding, and Advanced Manufacturing & Industrial Technology. Students use H&L's localized salary/demand data and rate perks/quirks/job gear for each career. The workbook's "Hat Research" template (Ch 14) provides a structured sheet: Name of Career, What Interests You, Brief Job Description, Education/Training Needed, Average Salary, and Tools/Equipment/Skills Needed.

#### Career Profile Research Sheet (15 min)

Students complete a printed research sheet for their top Manufacturing career, pulling data from both H&L and BLS. Fields: Career name, cluster, pathway, education required, salary range (DFW), job outlook, 3 daily tasks, RIASEC fit.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about the relationship between the amount of education required for a manufacturing career and the salary it pays?**

**EXIT TICKET: Identify one Manufacturing career and describe two training or certification requirements for that career. (Aligned to d(2)(A))**

### Day 3: Factory Floor Challenge: Sphero Introduction

**WARM-UP: Have you ever seen a robot in real life? Where was it and what was it doing?**

#### Connecting Robotics to Manufacturing (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "For the rest of this week, you are going to BE manufacturing engineers. Your team has been hired by a factory to program a robot to move materials from Point A to Point B on the factory floor. But you have to avoid obstacles and be precise -- just like a real FANUC robot on an assembly line."

> **Teacher:** "This is the same kind of work that students in the Robotics Manufacturing pathway at Singley Academy learn to do. They actually earn a FANUC Robot Operator certification that gets them hired at real factories."

Engineering Design Process Step: Define the Problem -- Teams receive their factory floor challenge card describing the course layout and task requirements.


#### SpheroEDU Introduction (30 min)

Distribute Sphero RVR+ robots. Teams log into SpheroEDU and complete the basic driving tutorial. Teacher models the block-based programming interface on the projector. Students practice basic commands: move forward, turn, stop.

**DOK 2: How would you describe the connection between the code you are writing and the movements the robot makes on the factory floor?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one technical skill you used today that a Manufacturing professional would also use. (Aligned to d(1)(C))**

### Day 4: Factory Floor Challenge: Design and Test

**WARM-UP: What went wrong in your first attempts yesterday? What will your team do differently today?**

#### Research and Ideate (10 min)

Teams sketch a plan for their robot's route, accounting for obstacles. They research which Sphero commands will be needed.

Engineering Design Process Step: Research & Ideate -- Teams sketch their robot's path on paper before coding. They measure distances with rulers and plan turns.


#### Prototype and Test (30 min)

Teams code, test, observe, and revise. Teacher uses Active Monitoring checklist. Encourage teams to document what works and what fails.

**DOK 4: Based on your testing results, what improvements would you recommend to your factory floor layout to make the robot more efficient? What evidence supports your recommendation?**

Engineering Design Process Step: Prototype & Test -- Teams build their program in SpheroEDU, run the robot through the course, and iterate based on results.


**EXIT TICKET: Describe one way you applied the Engineering Design Process today, and connect it to how a real manufacturing engineer would use the same process. (Aligned to d(1)(C), d(2)(A))**

### Day 5: Factory Floor Presentations + Cluster Wrap-Up

**WARM-UP: If you were the factory owner watching your robot today, would you hire your team? Why or why not?**

#### Team Presentations (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Each team will now present your factory floor solution. You have 3 minutes. Tell us: What was the problem? What did your robot do? What did you learn from testing? And most importantly -- how does this connect to a real Manufacturing career?"

Teams present. Class provides peer feedback using two stars and a wish.

Engineering Design Process Step: Evaluate & Share -- Teams present their solution, explain their Engineering Design Process, and connect to Manufacturing careers.


#### H&L Manufacturing Wrap-Up (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Before we close out Manufacturing, go back into Hats and Ladders and favorite at least 2 Manufacturing careers that interest you. These will be saved to your Climber Profile and we will come back to them later in the year."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students favorite 2-3 Manufacturing careers in their Climber Profile. They review how their RIASEC type connects to the Manufacturing cluster. The workbook's Hat Research section directs students to "Go to the Hats & Ladders app and click on the Hat Finder" to explore Hats in the Manufacturing cluster. Favorites are saved to the student's Climber Profile for use in the Career Plan later in the year.

**EXIT TICKET: Name the Manufacturing career you favorited in H&L and explain in one sentence why it interests you. (Aligned to d(1)(C))**

## Formative Assessment

- Stop and Jot Review: Collect and scan Day 1 notes for evidence of new career knowledge. (Aligned to d(1)(B))
- Career Profile Research Sheet: Check for accuracy of education/training data on Day 2. (Aligned to d(2)(A))
- Active Monitoring Checklist: Track team participation and EDP usage on Days 3-4. (Aligned to d(1)(C))
- Peer Presentation Feedback: Two Stars and a Wish on Day 5. (Aligned to d(1)(C))

## Summative Assessment

Factory Floor Challenge Presentation (Day 5): Teams present their Sphero solution and must explain: (1) the manufacturing problem they solved, (2) the Engineering Design Process steps they followed, (3) how the task connects to a real Manufacturing career, and (4) one career from H&L they would want to pursue in this field. Scored using a rubric assessing career knowledge (d(1)(B), d(1)(C)), technical skill description (d(2)(A)), and presentation quality.

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide a simplified career research sheet with pre-filled categories and sentence starters for students who need support.
- During Sphero programming, offer a step-by-step visual guide showing the block commands with screenshots.
- Allow struggling teams to complete a simplified factory floor course with fewer obstacles.

### Extensions

- Advanced teams can add sensors or conditional logic to their Sphero program (e.g., if obstacle detected, turn right).
- Students research the FANUC Robot Operator certification pathway at Singley Academy and create a 4-year plan to earn it.
- Compare Manufacturing salary data between DFW and two other metro areas using BLS data.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach vocabulary using visual cards with Spanish translations: Manufacturing = Manufactura, Automation = Automatizacion, Quality Control = Control de Calidad.
- Provide bilingual sentence stems for the career research sheet: "This career requires _____ (Esta carrera requiere _____)."
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers during team activities.
- H&L tour videos include visual context that supports comprehension even without full English fluency. Encourage ESL students to use browser translation.
