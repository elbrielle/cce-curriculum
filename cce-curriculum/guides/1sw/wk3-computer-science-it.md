## Lesson Objective

Students will continue exploring the IT cluster through Hats & Ladders with a focus on Network Systems and Computer Science pathways, research emerging occupations in AI, machine learning, and cloud computing, and play the H&L IT scenario-based game to practice workplace decision-making.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can explain the difference between at least two IT pathways, research an emerging technology career, and make workplace decisions in a simulated IT environment."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(1)(C):** Identify various career opportunities within one or more career clusters.
- **d(1)(D):** Research and evaluate emerging occupations related to career interest areas.
- **d(4)(B):** Identify skills that can be transferable among a variety of careers.

## Materials Needed

- Chromebooks with internet access
- Hats & Ladders student accounts
- Sphero RVR+ robots and SpheroEDU app
- CS First by Google: https://csfirst.withgoogle.com
- BLS - Computer Network Architects: https://www.bls.gov/ooh/computer-and-information-technology/computer-network-architects.htm
- Printed Emerging Tech Research Template (1 per student)

## Career Connection

Computer Science is the backbone of every technology we use. Network administrators keep the internet running. Database managers protect our data. AI engineers are building the technologies of tomorrow. These careers are evolving so fast that jobs that exist today did not exist 10 years ago -- and jobs that will exist in 10 years have not been invented yet. That is what makes d(1)(D) so important: students need to research EMERGING occupations, not just current ones.


**Irving ISD Pathway: Computer Science (District Wide), Technology Support Services (Singley), and Cybersecurity (Singley) all branch from the IT cluster students are exploring this week.**

## Vocabulary

- **Network Systems:** The infrastructure of connected computers and devices that share information and resources.
- **Cloud Computing:** Delivery of computing services (servers, storage, databases, software) over the internet instead of on a local computer.
- **Artificial Intelligence (AI):** Computer systems that can perform tasks that normally require human intelligence, like recognizing speech or making decisions.
- **Transferable Skills:** Abilities that are useful in multiple career fields, such as communication, problem-solving, and teamwork.
- **Emerging Occupation:** A new or rapidly growing career that did not exist or was uncommon in the recent past.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In H&L, the IT cluster branches into multiple pathways. This week students explore Network Systems and begin connecting their exploration to emerging fields. The H&L scenario-based game for IT immerses students in an authentic workplace where they make real-world decisions as an IT professional. The platform also helps students identify transferable skills across IT pathways.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Active Monitoring:** During the H&L game and Sphero activities, circulate with a checklist tracking student engagement, career vocabulary usage, and ability to connect game scenarios to real IT careers.
- **Stop and Jot:** During the emerging tech research, students pause every 5 minutes to jot down one new technology and one career associated with it.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: Network Systems Pathway

**WARM-UP: If the internet went down in our school right now, whose job would it be to fix it? What do you think they would do?**

#### H&L Network Systems Exploration (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "We have been looking at programming careers in IT. Today we are branching into a different IT pathway: Network Systems. These are the people who build and maintain the actual infrastructure that makes the internet, Wi-Fi, and company networks work. Open H&L and navigate to the Network Systems pathway."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore the Networking Systems pathway in H&L (confirmed pathway name from the H&L workbook Ch 12). The workbook describes this pathway as: "Design, set up, and maintain the systems that connect computers and devices to the internet and each other." Students use the Hat Finder in the app to explore Hats within this pathway, such as Network Administrator, Network Architect, Database Administrator, and Systems Analyst.

#### Transferable Skills Discussion (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Here is something interesting: Pull up a Programming career and a Network Systems career side by side. What SKILLS do they share? Problem-solving? Communication? Attention to detail? These are called transferable skills -- they work across many careers."


**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about which skills appear across MULTIPLE IT pathways? Why do you think employers value these transferable skills?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one transferable skill that applies to both Programming and Network Systems careers. (d(4)(B))**

### Day 2: Emerging Tech Research

**WARM-UP: Name one technology that exists today that did NOT exist when your parents were in middle school.**

#### Emerging Occupations Research (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "The TEKS say you need to research and evaluate EMERGING occupations. That means careers that are brand new or growing super fast. Today you are going to research ONE emerging technology career. Your options: AI Engineer, Machine Learning Specialist, Cloud Architect, Cybersecurity Analyst, Data Scientist, UX Designer, or Drone Software Developer."

Students use BLS Career Outlook, H&L career profiles, and Google to research their chosen emerging career. They complete the Emerging Tech Research Template: career name, what the job involves, education needed, salary, why it is growing, and one company hiring for this role in DFW.

**DOK 4: Based on your research, what argument would you make for or against a student choosing this emerging career over a more traditional IT career like Network Administrator? Use salary, growth, and education data to support your argument.**

**EXIT TICKET: Name the emerging career you researched and explain in one sentence why it is considered 'emerging.' (d(1)(D))**

### Day 3: H&L IT Scenario Game

**WARM-UP: Imagine you are an IT professional and your company's server just crashed. What is the first thing you would do?**

#### H&L Scenario-Based Game (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today is game day. Hats and Ladders has an interactive game where you get to BE an IT professional. You will face real workplace scenarios and make decisions. There are no wrong answers, but your choices lead to different outcomes -- just like in a real job."

> [H&L PLATFORM] The H&L workbook's IT chapter (Ch 12) includes the "Cybersecurity in Action" activity, which is a project-based activity (not a scenario game) where students create a cyber safety tool to teach kids about safe online purchasing. For this week's IT scenario experience, use the "Website Design" project from the H&L workbook (Ch 12, p. 200) instead -- students take on the role of a Web Developer and design a website with wireframing and layout planning. Alternatively, use the workbook's "Job Applications" activity (Ch 12, p. 196) where students practice filling out a standard job application for "Future Work, Inc." Note: If the H&L app has additional scenario-based games beyond what is in the workbook, explore those as well.

#### Debrief Discussion (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "What decisions did you have to make? Were any of them hard? What happened when you chose differently than you expected? This is what real IT professionals deal with every day -- making decisions under pressure with incomplete information."


**DOK 2: How would you summarize the most important decision you had to make in the H&L game and what the consequences were?**

**EXIT TICKET: Describe one workplace decision you made in the H&L game and explain what you learned from the outcome. (d(1)(C))**

### Day 4: Sphero Sensor Challenge

**WARM-UP: How do self-driving cars know when to stop? What technology do they use?**

#### Sensor-Based Programming (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today we are adding a new dimension to your Sphero coding: sensors. Real IT systems use sensors everywhere -- security systems, manufacturing robots, self-driving cars. You are going to program your Sphero to respond to sensor input."

Teams program Sphero RVR+ using sensor inputs in SpheroEDU. This connects block-based coding to real-world IoT (Internet of Things) applications.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about how sensor-based programming is different from the simple movement programming you did in Weeks 1-2?**

Engineering Design Process: Define, Ideate, Prototype -- Teams design a Sphero program that uses color or IR sensor to change behavior (e.g., stop at a red line, speed up at green).


**EXIT TICKET: Name one real-world application of sensor-based programming and explain which IT career would build that system. (d(1)(C), d(4)(B))**

### Day 5: IT Pathway Reflection + Favorites

**WARM-UP: You have now spent 3 weeks in the IT cluster. Has your interest in IT gone up, down, or stayed the same? Write one sentence explaining why.**

#### H&L Pathway Fit + Favorites (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "We have explored Programming, Network Systems, and touched on emerging tech. Go into H&L and complete the pathway fit assessment if you haven't already. Then make sure you have favorited at least 2-3 IT careers that interest you."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students complete pathway fit assessment and favorite careers in their Climber Profile. The workbook's "Hat Research" template (Ch 12) provides a structured research sheet for recording career details. Have students complete this worksheet for their top IT career pick before favoriting in the app.

#### Emerging Tech Mini-Presentations (20 min)

Students share their emerging tech research from Day 2 in small groups (3-4 students). Each student has 2 minutes to pitch their emerging career to the group. Groups vote on which emerging career they think will be most in-demand in 10 years.

**DOK 4: If you were advising a current 8th grader on which IT pathway to choose for high school, what evidence from this week would you use to support your recommendation?**

**EXIT TICKET: Complete this sentence: The IT career I am most interested in is _____ because _____, and the Irving ISD pathway that connects to it is _____. (d(1)(C), d(1)(D))**

## Formative Assessment

- Stop and Jot notes from emerging tech research (Day 2). (d(1)(D))
- H&L game completion and debrief participation (Day 3). (d(1)(C))
- Active monitoring checklist during Sphero sensor challenge (Day 4). (d(4)(B))
- Pathway fit assessment completion in H&L (Day 5). (d(1)(C))

## Summative Assessment

Emerging Tech Research Template + Mini-Presentation (Days 2 and 5): Students submit their completed research template and deliver a 2-minute pitch on their emerging career. Scored on accuracy of research data (d(1)(D)), connection to the IT cluster (d(1)(C)), and identification of transferable skills (d(4)(B)).

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide a list of pre-approved emerging careers with starter research links for students who need direction.
- Offer a simplified Sphero sensor challenge with step-by-step visual guide.
- Allow students to use H&L's built-in vocabulary support during the scenario game.

### Extensions

- Students research and present on an IT career that does NOT yet exist but might in 10 years.
- Advanced teams add multiple sensor conditions to their Sphero program.
- Students compare the IT career landscape in DFW vs. Austin (Texas's two biggest tech hubs) using BLS data.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Network = Red (de computadoras), Cloud Computing = Computacion en la nube, Artificial Intelligence = Inteligencia Artificial.
- Provide bilingual research template with Spanish headers and sentence stems.
- H&L scenario game has visual context that supports comprehension. Encourage browser translation.
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers for the mini-presentation activity.
