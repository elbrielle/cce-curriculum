## Lesson Objective

Students will explore the Information Technology career cluster through Hats & Ladders, investigate programming and software development careers, compare salary and demand data for IT careers in the DFW area, and extend their Sphero coding skills to demonstrate how programming connects to real-world IT careers.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe the Information Technology career cluster, identify at least three programming careers with their salary ranges, and write a Sphero program that demonstrates a real-world IT concept."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(1)(B):** Explore and describe the CTE career clusters.
- **d(1)(C):** Identify various career opportunities within one or more career clusters.
- **d(5)(A):** Analyze labor market trends related to a career of interest.
- **d(5)(E):** Use resources to compare salaries of at least three careers in the student's interest area.

## Career Connection

The Information Technology cluster is one of the fastest-growing and highest-paying career fields in America. In the DFW area, companies like Texas Instruments, AT&T, and hundreds of tech startups hire thousands of IT professionals. Irving ISD offers two pathways leading to the Certified Entry Level Python Programmer credential -- one at every high school (District Wide) and a specialized track at Singley Academy. Students who start coding now are building skills that apply to nearly every industry.


**Irving ISD Pathway Link: Computer Science pathway (District Wide - all IISD high schools) and Programming & Digital Tech pathway (Singley Academy), both leading to Certified Entry Level Python Programmer certification.**


## Vocabulary

- **Algorithm:** A step-by-step set of instructions for solving a problem or completing a task.
- **Software Developer:** A professional who designs, builds, and maintains computer programs and applications.
- **Labor Market:** The supply of and demand for workers in a specific field or geographic area.
- **Python:** A popular programming language used in web development, data science, AI, and automation. Irving ISD offers Python certification.
- **Debugging:** The process of finding and fixing errors in computer code.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In Hats & Ladders, the Information Technology cluster contains pathways including Programming & Software Development, Network Systems, and Information Support & Services. The platform's career profiles for IT careers include localized DFW salary data, education requirements (showing both degree and certification pathways), and demand indicators. This week focuses on the Programming & Software Development pathway, connecting directly to Irving ISD's Python certification offerings.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Sentence Stems:** For the salary comparison activity, provide stems: "The highest-paying IT career I researched is _____ because _____. Compared to _____, it pays $_____ more/less per year." This helps all students, especially ELLs, articulate data-driven observations.
- **Think-Pair-Share:** After exploring IT careers in H&L, students think about which IT career surprised them most, pair with a neighbor to discuss, and share with the class.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: Welcome to Information Technology

**WARM-UP: Every app on your phone was built by someone. Pick one app and guess: How many people do you think it took to build it?**

#### H&L IT Cluster Tour (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "This week we are jumping into the biggest career cluster in our modern economy: Information Technology. Open Hats and Ladders and navigate to the IT cluster. Watch the cluster tour video and use Stop and Jot to capture new information."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students navigate to the IT cluster in H&L. The H&L workbook (Ch 12) opens with "Exploring the World of Information Technology," which describes IT careers and lists five pathways: Information Technology Support and Services, Web Development, Networking Systems, Cybersecurity, and Programming and Software Development. The workbook includes a "Making Connections" pair activity where students brainstorm how daily life is impacted by technology. The workbook then directs: "Go to the Hats & Ladders app and click on the Information Technology Cluster" to explore pathways.

#### Programming & Software Development Pathway (20 min)

**DOK 2: How would you compare the education requirements for a Software Developer versus a Web Developer?**

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore the Programming & Software Development pathway specifically. They examine 3-4 career profiles (Hats): Software Developer, Web Developer, App Developer, Game Developer. For each, they review education requirements, salary, and demand. The workbook (Ch 12) lists Programming and Software Development as one of the five IT pathways and directs students to explore careers within each pathway using the app.

**EXIT TICKET: Name the IT pathway you explored today and two careers within it. (Aligned to d(1)(B), d(1)(C))**

### Day 2: IT Salary Showdown

**WARM-UP: Do you think all IT jobs pay the same? Make a prediction: Which IT career do you think pays the MOST?**

#### H&L Salary and Demand Data (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today is about the money. I want you to go into Hats and Ladders and look at the salary and job demand data for at least three IT careers. Write down the career name, the DFW salary, and whether demand is growing, steady, or declining."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students pull salary and demand data from H&L career profiles for 3+ IT careers. The workbook's "Hat Research" template (Ch 12) provides a structured sheet for recording salary data, education/training requirements, and tools/equipment/skills for each career researched.

#### BLS Cross-Reference + Salary Comparison (20 min)

Students use the BLS Occupational Outlook Handbook to cross-reference H&L data. They complete the IT Salary Comparison worksheet comparing 3 careers on salary, education, growth rate, and daily tasks. Use sentence stems.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about the relationship between education level and salary for IT careers in the DFW area? Is more education always worth it?**

#### Class Discussion (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "Who found the highest-paying IT career? Who found the fastest-growing? Are they the same career? What does that tell us about how to choose a career -- should we only look at salary?"


**EXIT TICKET: Compare the salaries of three IT careers you researched. Which one has the best combination of salary AND job growth? (Aligned to d(5)(A), d(5)(E))**

### Day 3: Code Challenge: Sphero Delivery Bot

**WARM-UP: What is one thing that went well with Sphero last week in Manufacturing? What coding skill do you want to get better at?**

#### The Challenge (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "You are now an IT company. A client has asked you to program a delivery robot that navigates a warehouse, picks up a package, and delivers it to the shipping dock. Your Sphero is the robot. Your code is the software. This is what Software Developers do every day -- solve problems with code."

Engineering Design Process Step: Define the Problem -- Teams receive warehouse layout maps and delivery requirements.


#### Code and Test (30 min)

Teams design, code, test, and iterate. Introduce the concept of debugging -- finding and fixing errors. Encourage students to keep a bug log.


**DOK 2: How would you describe the debugging process you used when your code did not work as expected?**

Engineering Design Process Step: Research, Ideate, and Prototype -- Teams plan their code on paper first, then build and test in SpheroEDU.


**EXIT TICKET: Define 'debugging' in your own words and give one example from today. (Aligned to d(1)(C))**

### Day 4: Code Challenge: Iterate and Optimize

**WARM-UP: Look at your bug log from yesterday. What was the most common error your team made?**

#### Optimize (35 min)

Teams iterate on their solutions. Challenge: Can you reduce the number of code blocks while maintaining accuracy? This mirrors real software optimization.

**DOK 4: If you were a Software Development Manager, what criteria would you use to evaluate whether your team's code is ready to ship to a client? Use evidence from your testing.**

Engineering Design Process Step: Test and Evaluate -- Teams run final tests, optimize their code for speed and accuracy, and prepare for presentations.


**EXIT TICKET: Explain one way you optimized your code today and why optimization matters in professional software development. (Aligned to d(1)(C))**

### Day 5: IT Presentations + Pathway Fit Assessment

**WARM-UP: On a scale of 1-10, how much did you enjoy coding this week? What does that tell you about whether IT might be a good fit for you?**

#### Team Presentations (20 min)

Teams present: The problem, their code solution, bugs they fixed, and how this connects to a real IT career. 3 minutes per team.

#### H&L Pathway Fit Assessment + Favorites (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Go back into Hats and Ladders and complete the pathway fit assessment for IT. Then favorite your top 2-3 IT careers. Remember, these favorites are building your Career Plan for later this year."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students use the Hat Finder in the H&L app to explore IT careers and favorite 2-3 in their Climber Profile. The workbook's "Hat Research" template (Ch 12) provides a structured research sheet: Name of Career, What Interests You, Brief Job Description, Education/Training Needed, Average Salary, and Tools/Equipment/Skills Needed. Have students complete this worksheet for their top IT career pick.

**EXIT TICKET: Name the IT career you favorited in H&L and connect it to your RIASEC personality type. Is it a good fit? Why or why not? (Aligned to d(1)(C))**

## Formative Assessment

- Stop and Jot: Review Day 1 notes for evidence of IT cluster knowledge. (Aligned to d(1)(B))
- IT Salary Comparison Worksheet: Check for accurate data and complete comparisons on Day 2. (Aligned to d(5)(A), d(5)(E))
- Active Monitoring: Track coding progress and career connections on Days 3-4. (Aligned to d(1)(C))
- Pathway Fit Assessment Completion: Verify all students completed the H&L assessment on Day 5. (Aligned to d(1)(C))

## Summative Assessment

IT Delivery Bot Presentation (Day 5): Teams present their Sphero solution connecting programming concepts to real IT careers. Scored on career knowledge (d(1)(B), d(1)(C)), salary/demand analysis (d(5)(A), d(5)(E)), and coding demonstration.

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide pre-built code blocks that students can modify rather than building from scratch.
- Offer a BLS data extraction guide with highlighted fields so students know exactly where to find salary and growth data.
- Allow teams to use the simplified warehouse course layout.

### Extensions

- Challenge students to add conditional logic (if/then) to their Sphero program.
- Students research the Python certification pathway and create a 4-year HS plan to earn it.
- Compare DFW IT salaries to Silicon Valley salaries using BLS data and discuss cost of living.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach vocabulary with visual cards: Algorithm = Algoritmo, Debugging = Depuracion.
- Provide bilingual salary comparison worksheet with Spanish headers.
- Use visual step-by-step coding guides with screenshots alongside text instructions.
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers during team coding activities.
