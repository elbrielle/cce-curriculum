## Lesson Objective

Students will establish classroom expectations for the CCE lab environment, complete career self-assessments on the Hats & Ladders platform (RIASEC Core Personality and Work Values surveys), and begin building their personalized Climber Profile that will guide their career exploration throughout the year.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe what a CTE career cluster is, complete my RIASEC personality and Work Values assessments, and explain how my results connect to possible career pathways."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(1)(A):** Analyze and discuss the initial results of the assessments.
- **d(1)(B):** Explore and describe the CTE career clusters.

## Materials Needed

- Chromebooks with internet access (1 per student)
- Hats & Ladders student accounts (pre-rostered via Clever/ClassLink)
- Projector for teacher modeling
- Printed Lab Safety Contract (1 per student)
- Printed "My Career Journey" reflection handout (1 per student)
- Xello student accounts
- CareerOneStop Interest Assessment (backup): https://www.careeronestop.org/ExploreCareers/Assessments/interests.aspx

## Career Connection

Every career starts with self-awareness. Before students can explore specific career clusters, they need to understand their own personality traits, interests, and values. The RIASEC model (Realistic, Investigative, Artistic, Social, Enterprising, Conventional) is the same framework used by professional career counselors and platforms like O*NET. In Hats & Ladders, these results power every recommendation students receive for the rest of the year.

**Irving ISD Pathway:** All District Wide, MacArthur, Nimitz, Irving High, Singley, and Cardwell/Ratteree pathways are introduced at the overview level this week.

## Vocabulary

- **Career Cluster:** A grouping of occupations and industries based on shared knowledge, skills, and interests. Texas CTE recognizes 16 clusters.
- **RIASEC:** A personality model with six types: Realistic, Investigative, Artistic, Social, Enterprising, Conventional. Used to match people with compatible careers.
- **Work Values:** The qualities that are most important to a person in their career, such as independence, recognition, relationships, or achievement.
- **Climber Profile:** In Hats & Ladders, a personalized snapshot of a student's career interests, experiences, and goals that evolves as they explore.
- **Pathway:** A specific sequence of courses and experiences within a career cluster that prepares students for a group of related careers.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

Hats & Ladders calls students "Climbers" and frames career exploration as an adventure. The RIASEC Core Personality Assessment and Work Values Survey are the foundation of every student's experience on the platform. Once completed, H&L uses these results to recommend specific career clusters, pathways, and individual careers (called "Hats") that align with each student's unique profile. This week's assessment data will be referenced and revisited throughout all six grading periods.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Think-Pair-Share:** After completing the RIASEC assessment, students think about their top personality type, pair with a neighbor to compare results, then share one surprise finding with the class. This builds engagement and normalizes the idea that everyone has different strengths.
- **Chunking:** Break the H&L account setup and assessments into manageable segments: Day 1 = account setup + lab safety, Day 2 = RIASEC, Day 3 = Work Values, Day 4 = Xello quizzes, Day 5 = reflection and sharing. This prevents cognitive overload for ESL students especially.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: Lab Expectations and Account Setup

**WARM-UP:** Write or draw: What do you think you want to be when you grow up? It is 100% okay if you do not know yet.

#### Classroom Expectations (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Welcome to Career and College Explorations. This is not a regular class -- this is YOUR class to figure out what you are good at and what careers might be a fit for you. Let me walk you through how our lab works and what I expect from you."

> **Teacher:** "In this room, we use technology as a tool, not a toy. We will be using computers, 3D printers, robots, and more throughout the year. Safety and respect for equipment comes first."

Distribute and review the Lab Safety Contract. Students sign and return. Walk through Chromebook checkout procedures, charging expectations, and behavior guidelines.

#### Account Setup: Hats & Ladders (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Now we are going to set up your account on a platform called Hats and Ladders. This is going to be our main tool for exploring careers all year long. On this platform, YOU are called a Climber, and every career is called a Hat. Your job is to try on as many hats as possible and figure out which ones fit."

Model the login process on the projector. Students log in via Clever/ClassLink SSO. Walk them through creating their Climber Profile: name, grade, avatar/photo.

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students navigate to their Climber Profile page. They should see an empty profile that will fill in over time as they complete assessments and explore clusters. The H&L workbook (Ch 1: My Career Journey) pairs with the app for onboarding. Note: Login flow is district-specific via Clever/ClassLink SSO at app.hatsandladders.com -- confirm your campus SSO integration before this lesson.

**EXIT TICKET:** Name one thing you learned about lab safety today AND one thing you are curious to explore in Hats & Ladders.

### Day 2: RIASEC Core Personality Assessment

**WARM-UP:** Look at these six words: Realistic, Investigative, Artistic, Social, Enterprising, Conventional. Without looking anything up, which ONE word do you think describes you best? Write it down.

#### Introduction to RIASEC (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "Yesterday you set up your Climber account. Today, you are going to take your first career assessment. This is called the RIASEC assessment, and it helps figure out what TYPE of person you are when it comes to work. There are no right or wrong answers -- just honest ones."

> **Teacher:** "Let me show you what each letter stands for. R is for Realistic -- you like working with your hands, tools, or machines. I is for Investigative -- you like solving puzzles and figuring things out. A is for Artistic -- you are creative and like expressing yourself. S is for Social -- you love helping people. E is for Enterprising -- you like leading and persuading. C is for Conventional -- you are organized and like following a clear system."

Display the six RIASEC types with simple icons or examples. Give students 2 minutes to predict their top type before taking the assessment.

#### Take the Assessment (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Open Hats and Ladders. You should see the assessment waiting for you. Take your time and answer honestly. This is not a test -- there is no grade on your answers. The more honest you are, the better the career recommendations will be."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students complete the RIASEC Core Personality Assessment within H&L. The H&L workbook calls this "Learning My Core Personality Type" (Ch 1, p. 12). The platform presents illustrated, age-appropriate survey questions. Results show their top personality types. Students can also use the workbook page to reflect on and record their results.

#### Think-Pair-Share: Results Discussion (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "Okay, you have your results. Take a moment to read through what your top personality type means. Now turn to your partner and share: What is your top type? Were you surprised? Do you agree with it?"

**DOK 2:** How would you summarize the difference between your top RIASEC personality type and the one you predicted before taking the assessment?

**EXIT TICKET:** What is your top RIASEC personality type? Name one career you think might match that type.

### Day 3: Work Values Survey + Building Blocks

**WARM-UP:** Would you rather have a job that pays a lot of money but you work alone all day, or a job that pays less but you work with a fun team? Write a sentence explaining your choice.

#### Work Values Survey (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Yesterday was about your personality. Today is about your values -- what matters most to you in a job. Is it money? Helping people? Being creative? Having freedom? Hats and Ladders is going to ask you questions to figure this out."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students complete the Work Values Survey in H&L. The H&L workbook calls this "Discovering My Work Values" (Ch 1, p. 14). Students rank what qualities matter most to them in a career and workplace. The workbook provides space to record and reflect on results.

#### Building Blocks Activity (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Now that H&L knows your personality and your values, let us add one more piece: your Building Blocks. These are activities you already do -- in school and outside of school -- that connect to career paths. Things like sports, gaming, art, cooking, fixing things, coding -- all of these are building blocks."

**DOK 3:** What conclusions can you draw about how your RIASEC personality type, work values, AND building blocks all connect to suggest certain career clusters for you?

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students complete the Building Blocks activity identifying their likes, dislikes, and connections to related pathways.

#### Explore Initial Recommendations (10 min)

After completing all three inputs, H&L generates initial cluster recommendations. Students browse their recommended clusters and note which ones surprise them.

**EXIT TICKET:** Name your top Work Value and explain why it matters to you in one complete sentence.

### Day 4: Xello Assessments + Career Cluster Overview

**WARM-UP:** How many career clusters do you think exist? Take a guess and write it down.

#### Xello Quizzes (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today we are going to use a second platform called Xello to take three more quizzes: the Matchmaker Quiz, the Personality Quiz, and the Learning Style Quiz. Think of this as a second opinion. The more data points you have about yourself, the better your career plan will be."

Students log into Xello and complete the Matchmaker Quiz, Personality Quiz, and Learning Style Quiz. Teacher circulates and assists with login issues.

#### The 16 Career Clusters (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Now let us zoom out. Texas CTE recognizes 16 career clusters. Over the next 36 weeks, we are going to explore many of them. Let me give you a quick tour of what is coming."

Quick visual overview of the 16 CTE clusters. Show the Irving ISD CTE Pathways poster and let students see which high schools offer which pathways. Ask: Which ones catch your eye?

**DOK 2:** How would you classify the career clusters into groups based on whether they involve working with people, data, things, or ideas?

**EXIT TICKET:** Compare your Xello results to your H&L results. Do they agree? Write one similarity and one difference.

### Day 5: Reflection and Career Journey Launch

**WARM-UP:** Look at your H&L Climber Profile. What has changed since Day 1?

#### My Career Journey Reflection (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "You have done a LOT of self-discovery this week. Today you are going to pull it all together. I am handing out a reflection sheet called My Career Journey. You will fill in your RIASEC type, your top work values, your building blocks, and the clusters you are most curious about."

Students complete the printed "My Career Journey" reflection handout, synthesizing data from both H&L and Xello. This document will be revisited at mid-year (4th Six Weeks) and end-of-year (6th Six Weeks).

#### Gallery Walk: Career Cluster Curiosity (15 min)

Post the 14 H&L cluster names around the room. Students place a sticky dot under the 2-3 clusters they are most curious about. Class discusses which clusters are most popular and least popular -- and why that does not mean the unpopular ones are less valuable.

**DOK 4:** Based on what you know about yourself from this week's assessments, what criteria would you use to decide whether a career cluster is worth exploring, even if it was not in your top recommendations?

**EXIT TICKET:** Write one complete sentence: The career cluster I am most excited to explore is _____ because _____.

## Formative Assessment

- Observation: During Day 2 RIASEC assessment, monitor for students rushing through without reading. (d(1)(A))
- Peer Discussion: During Think-Pair-Share on Day 2, listen for students accurately describing their RIASEC type. (d(1)(A))
- Building Blocks Completion: Check that all students have entered at least 3 Building Blocks in H&L by end of Day 3. (d(1)(B))
- Gallery Walk Participation: Observe student engagement and cluster selections on Day 5. (d(1)(B))

## Summative Assessment

My Career Journey Reflection Handout (collected at end of Day 5): Students synthesize their RIASEC type, work values, building blocks, and top cluster interests into a single written reflection. Scored on completeness, self-awareness, and ability to connect assessment data to career curiosity. (Aligned to d(1)(A) and d(1)(B))

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide pre-filled sentence stems for the reflection handout: "My top RIASEC type is _____, which means I like to _____."
- For students who struggle with the assessments, allow them to work with a partner who reads the questions aloud.
- Offer a simplified visual version of the 16 clusters with icons and 1-sentence descriptions.

### Extensions

- Students who finish early can explore 2-3 career profiles (Hats) within their recommended clusters and rate the perks/quirks/job gear.
- Challenge students to find a career that bridges two of their top RIASEC types (e.g., Artistic + Social = Art Teacher).
- Students compare their H&L recommendations to the CareerOneStop Interest Assessment results.

### ELL Language Support

- H&L is compatible with browser-based language translation tools (Google Translate). Show ESL students how to enable this.
- Pre-teach key vocabulary (RIASEC, cluster, pathway, values) using visual vocabulary cards with Spanish translations.
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers for the Think-Pair-Share discussion.
- Use the bilingual "At a Glance" format for the My Career Journey handout (English instructions with Spanish sentence stems).
