## Lesson Objective

Students will explore the Information Support & Services pathway in Hats & Ladders, research certification vs. degree pathways for tech support careers, and build a micro:bit help desk simulation using Microsoft MakeCode to demonstrate troubleshooting and customer service skills.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe the education and certification options for IT support careers, explain why certifications like CompTIA matter in the IT industry, and build a micro:bit program that simulates a tech support scenario."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(2)(A):** Research and describe applicable academic, technical, certification, and training requirements for one or more careers.
- **d(2)(B):** Use available resources to research and evaluate educational and training options for one or more careers.
- **d(4)(B):** Identify skills that can be transferable among a variety of careers.

## Materials Needed

- Chromebooks with internet access
- Hats & Ladders student accounts
- micro:bit devices (1 per team of 2-3 students)
- Microsoft MakeCode: https://makecode.microbit.org

- Printed Education Pathway Comparison worksheet

## Career Connection

Tech Support is the front line of every IT organization. Help Desk Technicians, IT Support Specialists, and Systems Administrators keep businesses running by solving technology problems. What makes this career cluster special is that you do NOT always need a 4-year college degree. Many IT support professionals enter the field with industry certifications like CompTIA A+, CompTIA Network+, or the Computer Repair Technology certification offered at Singley Academy. This is a great week to show students that college is not the only path to a well-paying career.

**Irving ISD Pathway: Technology Support Services (Singley Academy) leads to the Computer Repair Technology - Job Ready certification. This pathway is a direct entry point into IT support careers.**


## Vocabulary

- **Help Desk:** A centralized resource for providing technical assistance and troubleshooting to users with IT problems.
- **Certification:** A credential earned by passing an exam that proves you have specific knowledge or skills. In IT, certifications like CompTIA A+ are often valued as much as degrees.
- **CompTIA:** A nonprofit trade association that issues industry-recognized IT certifications used worldwide.
- **Troubleshooting:** The systematic process of diagnosing and resolving a problem, especially in technology.
- **micro:** bit: A small, programmable computer designed for education. Used in this lesson to simulate IT troubleshooting scenarios.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In H&L, the Information Support & Services pathway shows students careers like Help Desk Technician, IT Support Specialist, and Systems Administrator. The platform's education pathway indicators are especially valuable this week because they show students the difference between certification-only careers and degree-required careers. Students can see in H&L that some IT support careers are accessible with just a high school diploma plus certifications.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Chunking:** Break the certification vs. degree comparison into manageable pieces: Day 1 = explore the pathway, Day 2 = deep-dive into certification options, Day 3-4 = hands-on MakeCode, Day 5 = synthesis. This prevents information overload.
- **Sentence Stems:** For the education pathway comparison: 'A _____ certification takes _____ to earn and qualifies you for _____. A _____ degree takes _____ and qualifies you for _____.  The main difference is _____.''

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: Information Support & Services Pathway

**WARM-UP: Think about the last time something went wrong with your technology (phone, computer, game console). Who fixed it? How?**

#### H&L Pathway Exploration (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "We have looked at Programming, Network Systems, and emerging tech. Today we are exploring a pathway that is the backbone of every IT department: Information Support and Services. These are the people you call when your computer breaks. Open H&L and find this pathway."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore the Information Technology Support and Services pathway (confirmed name from H&L workbook Ch 12). The workbook describes this pathway as: "Help people and businesses troubleshoot and fix computer, software, and network issues." Students use the Hat Finder in the app to explore Hats such as Help Desk Technician, IT Support Specialist, Desktop Support Technician, and Systems Administrator. Students check education indicators and rate perks/quirks/job gear.

#### Not All IT Careers Need a Degree (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Look at the education requirements for Help Desk Technician versus Software Developer. Notice anything? Help Desk can be entered with certifications and no 4-year degree. This is one of the most important things about the IT field -- there are MULTIPLE entry points. Let us talk about what that means."


**DOK 2: How would you compare the education pathways for a Help Desk Technician versus a Software Developer? Which one can you start earning money in sooner?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one IT support career and describe whether it requires a degree, a certification, or both. (d(2)(A))**

### Day 2: Certification Deep-Dive

**WARM-UP: What is a certification? How is it different from a diploma or a degree?**

#### CompTIA Career Roadmap Exploration (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today I am going to show you the CompTIA Career Roadmap. This is a visual map that shows you every major IT certification and which career it leads to. Pull up this link on your Chromebook: comptia.org/content/it-careers-path-roadmap."

Students explore the interactive CompTIA roadmap. They identify which certifications connect to the IT support careers they explored in H&L yesterday. They note: certification name, time to earn, cost, and what career it unlocks.

#### Education Pathway Comparison (20 min)

Students complete the printed Education Pathway Comparison worksheet. They compare 3 IT careers side-by-side: one requiring only certifications, one requiring an associate degree + cert, and one requiring a bachelor's degree. They calculate the time and estimated cost for each pathway.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about whether a 4-year degree is always the best choice for someone who wants to work in IT support? Use the cost and time data from your comparison to support your answer.**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one IT certification and explain what career it prepares you for. (d(2)(A), d(2)(B))**

### Day 3: Help Desk Simulator: MakeCode Intro

**WARM-UP: If someone brought you a computer that would not turn on, what are the first 3 things you would check?**

#### The Challenge (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "You are now the IT Help Desk. Your company has given you micro:bits to build a troubleshooting tool. When a user reports a problem, your micro:bit will display a diagnostic checklist. When the tech presses Button A, it shows the next troubleshooting step. When they press Button B, it logs that the problem was solved. This is how real help desk ticketing systems work -- just much more complex."

Engineering Design Process: Define the Problem -- Teams receive a help desk scenario card describing a common IT problem (printer not working, Wi-Fi disconnected, software crashing).


#### MakeCode Programming (30 min)

Students open Microsoft MakeCode (makecode.microbit.org) and build a program that cycles through troubleshooting steps on the micro:bit display when Button A is pressed. Teacher models the first 3 blocks, then students extend independently.

**DOK 2: How would you describe the logical order of troubleshooting steps and why that order matters for IT support professionals?**

**EXIT TICKET: List the troubleshooting steps your micro:bit program displays, in order. (d(2)(A))**

### Day 4: Help Desk Simulator: Test and Iterate

**WARM-UP: What is the hardest part about helping someone fix a technology problem? Is it the technology or the communication?**

#### Refine and Test (20 min)

Partner teams swap micro:bits and try to follow each other's troubleshooting programs. They provide feedback: Was the sequence logical? Were the steps clear?

Engineering Design Process: Prototype & Test -- Teams refine their MakeCode programs, add features (LED animations for each step, sound for 'problem solved'), and test with partner teams.


#### Customer Service Role-Play (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Being a great IT support professional is not just about knowing technology. It is about COMMUNICATION. You have to explain complex problems in simple language to people who are frustrated. Let us practice. One partner is the user with the problem. The other is the Help Desk tech. Use your micro:bit as your guide."

Students role-play a help desk call using their micro:bit troubleshooting tool. The 'user' describes a problem; the 'tech' walks them through the steps displayed on the micro:bit.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about why communication skills are considered a transferable skill that applies to BOTH IT support AND other careers like nursing or teaching?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name two transferable skills you practiced today that would be valuable in ANY career, not just IT. (d(4)(B))**

### Day 5: Help Desk Presentations + Reflection

**WARM-UP: After 4 weeks in the IT cluster, rank your interest in these IT pathways from most to least: Programming, Network Systems, Tech Support, Cybersecurity (next week!).**

#### Presentations (20 min)

Teams demo their micro:bit help desk tool and explain: the IT problem they solved, why they ordered the troubleshooting steps the way they did, and how this simulates a real help desk career. 3 minutes per team.

#### H&L Favorites Update (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Update your favorites in Hats and Ladders. You should now have favorites from Manufacturing AND IT. Look at your Climber Profile -- is it starting to paint a picture of who you are as a future professional?"

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students update their Climber Profile with IT support career favorites and review their Building Blocks connections to IT.

**EXIT TICKET: Explain one thing that surprised you about IT support careers this week, and whether the Singley Academy Technology Support Services pathway interests you. (d(2)(A), d(2)(B))**

## Formative Assessment

- H&L pathway exploration completion (Day 1). (d(2)(A))
- Education Pathway Comparison worksheet accuracy (Day 2). (d(2)(A), d(2)(B))
- Active monitoring of MakeCode progress (Days 3-4). (d(4)(B))
- Role-play observation for communication skills (Day 4). (d(4)(B))

## Summative Assessment

Help Desk Simulator Presentation (Day 5): Teams present their micro:bit troubleshooting tool. Scored on: logical troubleshooting sequence (d(2)(A)), ability to explain certification/education requirements for IT support (d(2)(A), d(2)(B)), and identification of transferable skills used (d(4)(B)).

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide pre-built MakeCode starter code that students can modify rather than building from scratch.
- Offer a simplified Education Pathway worksheet with pre-filled data for one of the three careers.
- Provide troubleshooting step cards that students can arrange in order before programming.

### Extensions

- Students research the CompTIA A+ certification exam topics and create a study plan.
- Advanced teams add a 'timer' to their micro:bit that tracks how long each troubleshooting step takes.
- Students compare IT support salaries in DFW vs. national average using BLS data.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Certification = Certificacion, Troubleshooting = Solucion de problemas, Help Desk = Mesa de ayuda.
- Provide bilingual troubleshooting step cards with English and Spanish labels.
- Role-play scripts with sentence stems in both languages: 'I am going to try _____ to fix your problem.'
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers during role-play activities.
