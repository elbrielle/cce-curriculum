## Lesson Objective

Students will explore cybersecurity careers through the Hats & Ladders 'Cybersecurity in Action' interactive game, research emerging cybersecurity occupations, complete the IT cluster wrap-up, and create a personalized college awareness project using the Glowforge laser cutter.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can explain why cybersecurity is one of the fastest-growing career fields, describe the education pathways for cybersecurity careers, and design and laser-cut a college/career logo that represents my future goals."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(1)(D):** Research and evaluate emerging occupations related to career interest areas.
- **d(4)(F):** Define and identify examples in the workplace of characteristics required for personal and professional success such as work ethic, integrity, dedication, and perseverance.
- **d(3)(A):** Describe academic requirements for transitioning from middle school to high school and from high school to career or postsecondary education.

## Career Connection

Cybersecurity is one of the most critical and fastest-growing career fields in the world. Every company, hospital, school, and government agency needs cybersecurity professionals. In the U.S., there are currently hundreds of thousands of unfilled cybersecurity jobs because there are not enough trained professionals. At Singley Academy, Irving ISD offers a Cybersecurity pathway leading to the Cybersecurity Fundamentals certification. This is a career where integrity and work ethic are not just nice-to-haves -- they are essential, because cybersecurity professionals are trusted with protecting sensitive data.


**Irving ISD Pathway: Cybersecurity (Singley Academy) leads to Cybersecurity Fundamentals certification. This is a high-demand, high-wage pathway with strong job security.**

## Vocabulary

- **Cybersecurity:** The practice of protecting computer systems, networks, and data from digital attacks, theft, and damage.
- **Phishing:** A type of cyber attack that uses fake emails or websites to trick people into revealing personal information.
- **Ethical Hacking:** Authorized testing of computer systems to find security vulnerabilities before malicious hackers can exploit them.
- **Integrity:** The quality of being honest and having strong moral principles. In cybersecurity, integrity means protecting data and following ethical standards.
- **Postsecondary Education:** Education after high school, including college, trade school, certifications, military training, and apprenticeships.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

Hats & Ladders features a specific interactive experience called 'Cybersecurity in Action' where students explore cybersecurity workplace scenarios. This is the capstone of the IT cluster exploration. After this week, students will have explored all major IT pathways (Programming, Network Systems, Tech Support, and Cybersecurity) and should have a strong set of favorited careers in their Climber Profile. The Glowforge project provides a tangible connection to college and career awareness (d(3)(A)) as students design and cut a logo representing a college or career program they aspire to.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Think-Pair-Share:** After playing the H&L Cybersecurity in Action game, students think about the ethical dilemma they faced, pair to discuss, and share their reasoning with the class. This connects to d(4)(F) -- integrity in the workplace.
- **Active Monitoring:** During Glowforge design and cutting, circulate to ensure all students are connecting their logo choice to a college or career goal, not just picking a random image.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: Cybersecurity: The Digital Battlefield

**WARM-UP: Have you or someone you know ever been hacked, scammed, or had a password stolen? What happened?**

#### H&L Cybersecurity Exploration (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "This is our LAST IT pathway this six weeks, and it might be the most important one: Cybersecurity. These are the people who protect everything digital -- your bank accounts, medical records, school data, everything. Open H&L and explore cybersecurity careers."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore cybersecurity-related Hats in H&L using the Hat Finder within the Cybersecurity pathway (confirmed pathway from H&L workbook Ch 12). The workbook describes this pathway as: "Protect computers, networks, and data from hackers, viruses, and cyber threats." Students use the Hat Finder to explore careers such as Information Security Analyst, Cybersecurity Specialist, Penetration Tester, and Security Consultant, reviewing salary, education, and demand data.

#### CyberSeek Career Pathway Map (15 min)

Students explore the interactive CyberSeek.org pathway map, which shows the progression from entry-level to advanced cybersecurity careers. They trace a path from their current position (middle school) to a cybersecurity career, noting certifications and degrees needed at each step.

**DOK 2: How would you describe the steps someone would need to take to go from where you are now to becoming a Cybersecurity Analyst?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one cybersecurity career and explain why it is considered a high-demand occupation. (d(1)(D))**

### Day 2: Cybersecurity in Action: H&L Game Day

**WARM-UP: Would you rather be the person who builds the security system or the person who tries to break into it? Why?**

#### H&L Cybersecurity in Action Project (30 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today you are taking on a special role from the Hats and Ladders workbook: you are a Cyber Safety Creator! Your job is to design a tool that helps kids understand the difference between a safe website and an unsafe website for making online purchases. You will choose one of three options: a Kid-Friendly Cyber Security Guide, a Cyber Safety App or System, or a Public Service Announcement. You will brainstorm, design your tool, and then get peer feedback. Pay attention to HOW you protect others -- that is the integrity side of cybersecurity."

> [H&L PLATFORM] "Cybersecurity in Action" is confirmed as a named activity in the H&L workbook (Ch 12, p. 193). CORRECTION: This is a project-based activity, NOT a scenario-based decision game. Students become "Cyber Safety Creators" and design a tool to help kids distinguish safe websites from unsafe ones for online purchases. Students choose one of three tools to create: (1) A Kid-Friendly Cyber Security Guide (colorful brochure with tips), (2) A Cyber Safety App or System (invent an app/software concept with a creative name), or (3) A Public Service Announcement (video script, audio script, or poster). The activity includes brainstorming, designing, getting peer feedback using a rubric, and class discussion. This pairs well with the integrity discussion since students must think critically about protecting vulnerable users.

#### Integrity in the Workplace Discussion (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "While designing your Cyber Safety tool, you had to think about protecting kids who might not know the difference between a safe and unsafe website. That responsibility -- protecting people who trust you with their safety -- is what integrity means in cybersecurity. In this field, integrity is not optional. If you do not have it, you literally cannot be trusted with access to sensitive systems."


**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about why integrity and work ethic are especially important in cybersecurity compared to other careers? What could happen if a cybersecurity professional lacked integrity?**

**EXIT TICKET: Define integrity in your own words and give one example of how it applies specifically to a cybersecurity career. (d(4)(F))**

### Day 3: College Awareness: Designing Your Future Logo

**WARM-UP: Name one college, trade school, military branch, or career program you have heard of. What do you know about it?**

#### MS to HS Transition Discussion (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "We have been exploring IT careers for 5 weeks now. Next year or the year after, you will be in HIGH SCHOOL. The choices you make about which courses to take in high school directly affect which careers you can pursue. Let me show you the Irving ISD CTE pathways poster -- these are the ACTUAL programs you can enter when you get to high school."

Display the Irving ISD CTE Pathways poster. Walk through the IT-related pathways: Computer Science (District Wide), Programming & Digital Tech (Singley), Cybersecurity (Singley), Technology Support Services (Singley). Discuss what courses students would take in 9th grade to start each pathway.

**DOK 2: How would you describe the academic requirements for getting into one of these IT pathways at your future high school?**

#### Glowforge Design: My Future Logo (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Now for something hands-on. You are going to design and laser-cut a logo that represents YOUR future. It can be a college logo, a career program logo, a certification logo, or a custom design that represents a career goal. This is going on the Glowforge, so it needs to be an SVG or a clean image."

Students use Canva, Adobe Express, or Inkscape to design their logo. The design must connect to a specific postsecondary goal (college, trade school, military, certification program). Teacher circulates to ensure meaningful connections.

Engineering Design Process: Define & Ideate -- Students choose their postsecondary goal and design a logo that represents it.


**EXIT TICKET: Name the college, trade school, or career program your logo represents and explain why you chose it. (d(3)(A))**

### Day 4: Glowforge Cutting + IT Cluster Wrap-Up

**WARM-UP: Look at your H&L Climber Profile. How many careers have you favorited so far? Which cluster has the most favorites?**

#### Glowforge Cutting (25 min)

First batch of logos goes to the Glowforge. Students not currently cutting work on the IT wrap-up activities below.

Engineering Design Process: Prototype -- Students finalize designs, convert to SVG, and send to the Glowforge. Teacher manages the cutting queue. While waiting, students complete the IT Cluster Wrap-Up.


#### IT Cluster Wrap-Up in H&L (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "While logos are printing, I need everyone to go into Hats and Ladders and do three things: First, make sure you have favorited at least 2-3 IT careers. Second, check your Building Blocks -- do any of them connect to IT? Third, look at your recommended clusters. Has anything changed since Week 0?"

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students finalize their IT cluster exploration: favorite remaining careers using the Hat Finder, review Building Blocks, and check updated cluster recommendations based on all exploration data. The workbook's Hat Research template (Ch 12) provides a final structured research sheet for students to complete for one IT career. Favorites and Building Blocks are saved in the Climber Profile and accumulate across weeks -- students should now have favorites from both Manufacturing (Wk 1) and IT (Wks 2-5).

**EXIT TICKET: Name the IT pathway that interests you most and the Irving ISD high school that offers it. (d(1)(D), d(3)(A))**

### Day 5: 1st Six Weeks Capstone: My IT Identity

**WARM-UP: Complete this sentence: Five weeks ago, I thought IT was _____. Now I know IT is _____.**

#### Remaining Glowforge Cuts + Logo Gallery (15 min)

Finish cutting remaining logos. Display all completed logos in a gallery. Students do a brief gallery walk to see each other's postsecondary goals.

#### 1st Six Weeks Reflection (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "This is our last day with the IT and Manufacturing clusters. Over the next five six-week periods, we will explore Law, Health Science, Agriculture, Trades, Business, and more. But right now, I want you to reflect on what you have learned about YOURSELF. Open your My Career Journey reflection from Week 0. Has anything changed?"

Students revisit their Week 0 My Career Journey reflection and add an update section: What clusters have I explored? What careers did I favorite? What surprised me? What do I want to explore next? This becomes a living document they update every six weeks.

**DOK 4: Based on everything you have learned about yourself and about IT/Manufacturing careers over the past 5 weeks, what criteria would you use to decide which career cluster to explore next? How would you rank those criteria?**

**EXIT TICKET: Write one complete sentence: The most important thing I learned about myself during the 1st Six Weeks is _____. (d(1)(D), d(4)(F))**

Scored on: career knowledge growth (d(1)(D)), integrity/work ethic reflection (d(4)(F)), and MS-to-HS transition understanding (d(3)(A)).

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide pre-made SVG templates for the Glowforge project so students focus on career meaning, not design frustration.
- Offer a simplified reflection template with fill-in-the-blank prompts for the 1st Six Weeks reflection.
- Provide a CyberSeek pathway printout with annotations for students who need a paper-based version.

### Extensions

- Students complete the NOVA Cybersecurity Lab (PBS) interactive game for extra engagement with cybersecurity concepts.
- Advanced students research a real-world cyber attack (appropriate for middle school) and present what went wrong.
- Students create a 4-year HS plan mapping courses from 9th grade through the Singley Cybersecurity pathway to certification.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Cybersecurity = Ciberseguridad, Integrity = Integridad, Phishing = Suplantacion de identidad.
- Bilingual reflection template with Spanish sentence stems for the 1st Six Weeks reflection.
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers during the H&L game and class discussions.
- Logo design activity is inherently visual and accessible for all language levels.
