## Lesson Objective

Students will complete the H&L PowerSkills Problem Solving module, identify transferable skills that apply across trades and professional careers, explore professional associations like SkillsUSA and ASE, update their Career Plan with 4th Six Weeks data, and complete a mid-year reflection demonstrating growth in career self-awareness.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can identify transferable skills that work across multiple careers, explain the value of professional associations like SkillsUSA, and demonstrate problem-solving strategies using the H&L PowerSkills module."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(4)(B):** Identify skills that can be transferable among a variety of careers.
- **d(4)(F):** Define and identify examples of work ethic, integrity, dedication, and perseverance in the workplace.
- **d(3)(H):** Identify professional associations and the value of membership.

## Career Connection

This capstone week ties together everything from the 4th Six Weeks: career planning, aviation, drones, and automotive. The focus shifts to the SKILLS that transfer across all of these careers. Problem-solving, communication, attention to detail, teamwork, and work ethic are valued in EVERY career field. Professional associations like SkillsUSA, ASE, FAA, and others provide networking, competitions, scholarships, and credentialing that support career growth. Students who join TSA are already experiencing the power of a professional student organization.

**Irving ISD Pathway: This week connects to all pathways explored in the 4th Six Weeks. Professional associations referenced: SkillsUSA (trades), ASE (automotive), FAA (aviation), and TSA (technology student association -- students are already members).**


## Vocabulary

- **Transferable Skills:** Skills that apply across multiple career fields, such as problem-solving, communication, teamwork, time management, and critical thinking.
- **Professional Association:** An organization for people working in a specific field that provides networking, training, certification, and advocacy.
- **SkillsUSA:** A national student organization for CTE students that hosts competitions in trades, technology, and leadership.
- **Work Ethic:** A set of values centered on the importance of doing work well, including reliability, initiative, and dedication.
- **PowerSkills:** In H&L, a set of modules that teach essential workplace skills like communication, problem-solving, and collaboration through interactive scenarios.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

The H&L PowerSkills Problem Solving module is the centerpiece of this week. PowerSkills are H&L's version of 'soft skills' or 'employability skills' -- the competencies that employers consistently rank as most important regardless of industry. The Problem Solving module uses workplace scenarios (some set in trades and logistics settings) where students must analyze a problem, consider multiple solutions, and choose the best course of action. This is the second PowerSkills module students have completed (the first was Communication in the 2nd Six Weeks).


## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Jigsaw:** For the professional association research on Day 3, assign each team a different association (SkillsUSA, ASE, FAA, AMA, NSPE). Teams research membership benefits, competitions, and career connections, then teach other groups.
- **Think-Pair-Share:** After the PowerSkills module, students think about the hardest problem they solved in the scenarios, pair to discuss their approach, and share strategies with the class.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: PowerSkills: Problem Solving

**WARM-UP: Describe a time you had to solve a problem at school or at home. What steps did you take?**

#### H&L PowerSkills Problem Solving Module (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "Remember the PowerSkills Communication module we did in the 2nd Six Weeks? Today we are tackling the next one: Problem Solving. This module puts you in real workplace situations where things go wrong and YOU have to figure out the solution. Some scenarios are set in shops, warehouses, and job sites -- connecting to the trades and transportation careers we explored this month."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Note: The H&L Powerskills workbook does NOT have a standalone "Problem Solving" module. However, problem-solving is embedded throughout many workbook activities: "Machine Breakdown Mystery" (Ch 14, troubleshooting a factory label machine), "Task Bot in Action" (Ch 14, fixing a crayon factory robot), "Hotel Rescue" (Ch 10, hotel management crises), and "Local Risk Response" (Ch 13, emergency response planning). Use any of these previously-introduced activities as a problem-solving revisit, or use the Powerskills workbook's "Design Thinking" module (p. 3-4, worst-idea brainstorming) as a creative problem-solving approach. If the H&L app has an additional Problem Solving module beyond the workbook, explore that as well.

#### Think-Pair-Share Debrief (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "What was the hardest scenario? What strategy did you use to solve it? Turn to your partner and share. Then we will hear from a few of you."

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about how the problem-solving strategies you used in the H&L module apply to careers in BOTH aviation and automotive?**

**EXIT TICKET: Describe one problem-solving strategy you learned from the H&L module and explain how it applies to a real workplace situation. (d(4)(B))**

### Day 2: Transferable Skills Matrix

**WARM-UP: Name a skill that a pilot, an auto mechanic, AND a nurse ALL need. Hint: it is not a technical skill.**

#### Transferable Skills Activity (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today we are building a Transferable Skills Matrix. A transferable skill is a skill that works in MANY careers, not just one. Think about it: problem-solving, communication, attention to detail, teamwork, time management -- every career we have explored this year values these skills. I want you to fill in this matrix showing which transferable skills apply to which careers."

Students complete the printed Transferable Skills Matrix. The matrix has careers across the top (Pilot, Auto Technician, Nurse, Software Developer, Lawyer, Teacher) and transferable skills down the side (Problem Solving, Communication, Attention to Detail, Teamwork, Time Management, Work Ethic, Critical Thinking, Leadership). Students check which skills apply to each career and discover that most skills apply to ALL careers.

**DOK 4: Based on your completed matrix, what argument would you make that transferable skills are MORE important than technical skills when choosing a career? Or would you argue the opposite? Use evidence from your matrix.**

**EXIT TICKET: Name the three transferable skills that appeared most frequently across all careers in your matrix. (d(4)(B))**

### Day 3: Professional Associations Jigsaw

**WARM-UP: Are you a member of any organization or club? What benefits do you get from being a member?**

#### Professional Association Research (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "You are all members of TSA -- the Technology Student Association. But TSA is just one of many professional associations that support career growth. Today each team will research a different professional association and teach the class about it."

Jigsaw activity. Teams research their assigned association: Team 1 = SkillsUSA, Team 2 = ASE, Team 3 = FAA, Team 4 = NSPE (National Society of Professional Engineers), Team 5 = TSA. They research: who can join, membership benefits, competitions, scholarships, and career connections.

#### Jigsaw Share-Out (15 min)

Teams share their research in a round-robin format. Each team has 2 minutes to present their association. Class discusses: What do all these associations have in common? Why do employers value membership?

**DOK 2: How would you describe the value of joining a professional association while you are still in school? What specific benefits would help you the most?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one professional association related to your career interest and explain one benefit of joining. (d(3)(H))**

### Day 4: Work Ethic and Integrity in the Trades

**WARM-UP: What does 'work ethic' mean to you? Describe someone you know who has a strong work ethic.**

#### Work Ethic Discussion (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "In every career we have explored -- IT, law, health science, agriculture, aviation, automotive -- employers say the same thing: they can teach technical skills, but they CANNOT teach work ethic. Work ethic means showing up on time, doing your best, being honest, and not giving up when things get hard. In the trades especially, integrity is everything -- when you repair a car's brakes, someone's LIFE depends on your honesty about whether you did the job right."


**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about why work ethic and integrity are especially critical in trades careers where safety is involved? Give at least two specific examples.**

#### Career Plan Update (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Open your H&L Career Plan and update it with everything you learned in the 4th Six Weeks. Add any new favorites from aviation, drones, or automotive. Review your course mapping from Week 2. Make sure your plan still reflects your interests."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students update their H&L Career Plan with new favorites, Building Blocks, and pathway preferences from the 4th Six Weeks. The workbook (Ch 16: My Next Steps) provides "My Career and Course Plan" and "Lifestyle Snapshot" templates for written planning alongside the app. Students can add new favorites and Building Blocks at any time in the app without starting over -- the Career Plan accumulates data across the year.

**EXIT TICKET: Define work ethic in your own words and give one example of how it applies to a career you are interested in. (d(4)(F))**

### Day 5: 4th Six Weeks Capstone: Mid-Year Growth Reflection

**WARM-UP: You are halfway through the year. If you could go back to Week 0 and tell yourself one thing about career planning, what would it be?**

#### Mid-Year Growth Reflection (30 min)

> **Teacher:** "This is the end of the 4th Six Weeks and the MID-POINT of the year. You have explored: Manufacturing, IT, Law, Health Science, Agriculture, Cosmetology, Entrepreneurship, Aviation, Drones, and Automotive. You have built a Career Plan, mapped your HS courses, and identified transferable skills. Today you are writing your mid-year growth reflection."

Students complete their mid-year reflection updating the My Career Journey sheet. They address: How have my career interests evolved since Week 0? What was the most surprising thing I learned this semester? What is my current top career pathway and why? What transferable skills do I have? What are my goals for the second half of the year?

**DOK 4: Create a set of criteria for evaluating your own career growth over the first half of the year. Then use those criteria to assess your progress honestly. What areas still need development?**

#### Sharing Circle (10 min)

Volunteers share one highlight from their reflection with the class. Teacher celebrates growth and previews the 5th Six Weeks: Architecture, Construction, HVAC/Electrical/Plumbing, Welding, and Personal Finance.

**EXIT TICKET: Complete this sentence: The most important thing I learned about myself during the first half of this course is _____. (d(4)(B), d(4)(F))**

## Formative Assessment

- PowerSkills module completion and scenario performance (Day 1). (d(4)(B))
- Transferable Skills Matrix completeness and accuracy (Day 2). (d(4)(B))
- Professional association Jigsaw research quality (Day 3). (d(3)(H))
- Career Plan update in H&L (Day 4). (d(4)(F))

## Summative Assessment

Mid-Year Growth Reflection (Day 5): Students submit their comprehensive My Career Journey reflection showing growth from Week 0 through the mid-year point. Scored on: evidence of transferable skill identification (d(4)(B)), work ethic and integrity understanding (d(4)(F)), and professional association knowledge (d(3)(H)).

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide a simplified Transferable Skills Matrix with some cells pre-filled as examples.
- Offer sentence starters for the mid-year reflection: 'At the beginning of the year I thought ___. Now I know ___.
- Allow students to complete the reflection in bullet-point or graphic organizer format rather than full paragraphs.

### Extensions

- Students create a visual 'Skills Resume' showing all transferable skills they have demonstrated this year, with evidence from class activities.
- Research and present on a SkillsUSA competition category that connects to their career interest.
- Students write a letter to their future 9th-grade self with career planning advice based on what they learned this semester.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Transferable = Transferible, Work Ethic = Etica de trabajo, Professional Association = Asociacion profesional.
- Bilingual mid-year reflection template with Spanish sentence stems.
- PowerSkills module uses visual scenarios that support comprehension across language levels.
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers during Jigsaw presentations.
