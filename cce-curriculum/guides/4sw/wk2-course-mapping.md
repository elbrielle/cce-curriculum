## Lesson Objective

Students will use the Hats & Ladders District Course Planner to map their high school courses to their chosen career pathway, create a written career plan, download their plan to share with family, and explore experience-building strategies through eDynamic Learning Unit 6.2.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can map my high school courses to a career pathway using the H&L District Course Planner, write a career plan that includes my pathway, courses, and postsecondary goals, and explain how effective planning impacts my future."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(8)(B):** Document high school courses, postsecondary education, and training requirements for a chosen pathway.
- **d(8)(C):** Write an individual plan to start a career.
- **d(3)(A):** Describe academic requirements for transitioning from middle school to high school and from high school to postsecondary education.
- **d(3)(B):** Identify college credit opportunities such as Advanced Placement, International Baccalaureate, and dual credit.
- **d(3)(D):** Discuss the impact of effective planning on career and educational goals.
- **d(3)(E):** Analyze the impact of standardized assessments such as PSAT, SAT, ACT, ASVAB, and TSI on postsecondary opportunities.

## Career Connection

This week bridges the gap between career dreams and concrete action. Students translate their career interests into a real course plan that maps from 9th grade through graduation. The H&L District Course Planner is aligned to local school districts, so students can see exactly which courses are available at their future Irving ISD high school. This plan becomes a conversation starter with families and counselors during the 8th-to-9th grade transition.

**Irving ISD Pathway: Students use the H&L District Course Planner to map courses specific to their chosen Irving ISD pathway, whether that is Computer Science (District Wide), Robotics Manufacturing (Singley), Nursing Science (Singley), Architecture (MacArthur), or any other available pathway.**

## Vocabulary

- **District Course Planner:** In H&L, a tool that shows the specific courses available in a student's school district, mapped to career pathways and CTE programs of study.
- **Graduation Plan:** In Texas, the Foundation High School Program with optional endorsements that determines which courses a student takes across 4 years.
- **Postsecondary:** Education or training that happens after high school, including 4-year university, community college, trade school, military, certification programs, or apprenticeships.
- **Career Plan:** A written document that outlines a student's career goals, the education and training needed to achieve them, and a timeline for completion.
- **Family Engagement:** Involving parents, guardians, and family members in a student's career planning process so they can provide support and informed guidance.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

The H&L District Course Planner is the key tool this week. Because Hats & Ladders partners with school districts, the Course Planner shows Irving ISD-specific course offerings. Students can see exactly which CTE courses are available at each high school and how they sequence across 4 years. The Coach Dashboard allows you to review and provide feedback on student plans. Students can also download their completed plan as a PDF or printout to share with their families.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Chunking:** Break course mapping into manageable steps: Day 1 = understand the HS transition, Day 2 = map courses in H&L, Day 3 = family letter and download, Day 4 = eDynamic experience-building, Day 5 = final plan writing. This prevents students from feeling overwhelmed by the complexity of HS planning.
- **Modeling:** Teacher models the entire course mapping process on the projector before students begin independently. Walk through one complete pathway from 9th to 12th grade, showing how CTE courses, core courses, and endorsements fit together.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: Understanding the MS-to-HS Transition

**WARM-UP: What is ONE thing you know about high school that you did not know when you started middle school?**

#### MS to HS Academic Requirements (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "In just a few months -- or next year for 7th graders -- you will be choosing your high school courses. This is a BIG decision because the courses you pick in 9th grade determine which CTE pathway you enter. Today I am going to walk you through how the Texas high school graduation plan works."

Teacher presents the Texas Foundation High School Program structure: core courses, endorsement options (STEM, Business & Industry, Arts & Humanities, Public Services, Multidisciplinary), and how CTE pathways connect to endorsements. Show the Irving ISD CTE Pathways poster and connect each pathway to an endorsement.

**DOK 2: How would you describe the relationship between a CTE pathway and a Texas high school endorsement?**

#### Texas OnCourse Exploration (20 min)

Students explore Texas OnCourse (texasoncourse.org), a free tool designed specifically for Texas students planning their HS coursework. They identify which endorsement aligns with their chosen career pathway from last week.

#### Standardized Testing: What You Need to Know (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "One more thing about high school planning: standardized tests. You will hear about the PSAT, SAT, ACT, ASVAB, and TSI. Let me break it down: the PSAT is practice for the SAT and can qualify you for scholarships. The SAT and ACT are used for college admissions. The ASVAB is for military careers. The TSI determines college course placement in Texas. Which tests matter to YOU depends on your career plan."

Class discussion: Students identify which tests connect to their pathway -- college-bound students focus on SAT/ACT, military pathways connect to ASVAB, and all Texas college students need the TSI. Teacher emphasizes that PSAT in 10th-11th grade can lead to National Merit Scholarships.

**DOK 2: How would you explain the difference between the PSAT, SAT, and TSI to a student who has never heard of any of them? (d(3)(E))**

**EXIT TICKET: Name the Texas HS endorsement that aligns with your career pathway and identify which standardized test is most relevant to your postsecondary plan. (d(3)(A), d(3)(E))**

### Day 2: H&L District Course Planner

**WARM-UP: If you could take ANY class in high school, what would it be? (It does not have to be a real class.)**

#### Teacher Modeling (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Watch my screen. I am going to walk through the H&L District Course Planner for one complete pathway. I will show you how to select your high school, choose your pathway, and see which courses you would take each year from 9th through 12th grade. Then you will do the same for YOUR pathway."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Teacher models the District Course Planner on the projector, selecting an Irving ISD high school and walking through a full 4-year course sequence for one pathway. Note: The workbook (Ch 16) includes "My Career and Course Plan" where students map out their plans. Confirm with your H&L district admin that Irving ISD course data is loaded in the app's Course Planner. Students may need to select a specific high school campus.

#### Student Course Mapping (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Your turn. Open the District Course Planner in H&L. Select the high school you plan to attend -- or the one that has your preferred pathway. Then map out your 4-year course plan. Make sure you include your CTE pathway courses AND your core academic courses."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students use the District Course Planner to build their personalized 4-year course plan, selecting CTE pathway courses, core courses, and endorsement-aligned electives.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about how your 9th grade course selections affect what is available to you in 11th and 12th grade? Why does the sequence matter?**

**EXIT TICKET: List the CTE courses you would take in 9th and 10th grade for your chosen pathway, and name the high school where they are offered. (d(8)(B))**

### Day 3: Family Engagement: Sharing My Plan

**WARM-UP: Have you talked to your family about what career you are interested in? What do they think?**

#### Download Career Plan (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today is about sharing your plan with the most important people in your life: your family. Hats and Ladders lets you download your career plan so you can show it to your parents or guardians. I also have a letter for your family that explains what we have been doing in this class and why their input matters."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students download or print their career plan from H&L. This includes their pathway selection, course sequence, and career interests. Note: The workbook's "My Career and Course Plan" (Ch 16) also provides a written template students can complete as a backup. Confirm the app's download/export feature with your H&L district admin -- it may export as PDF or provide a shareable link.

#### Family Career Plan Letter (20 min)

Distribute the printed bilingual (English/Spanish) Family Career Plan Letter. Students personalize the letter by filling in their name, career pathway, and top career interests. The letter invites families to discuss the plan at home and asks for a family member signature acknowledging they reviewed it.

**DOK 2: How would you explain your career pathway choice to a family member who has never heard of that career? What information would be most important to include?**

#### College Credit Opportunities + College For All Texans (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "Before you share your plan with your family, let me tell you about ways to earn COLLEGE CREDIT while still in high school. AP classes -- Advanced Placement -- let you take college-level courses and earn credit by passing an exam. Dual credit means you are enrolled in an actual college course AND a high school course at the same time. Some Irving ISD pathways include dual credit options. These can save your family thousands of dollars in college tuition."

Students briefly explore collegeforalltexans.com to see Texas college options, financial aid information, and program offerings. Teacher highlights the AP and dual credit search features. Students identify whether their chosen pathway offers any college credit opportunities.

**DOK 2: How would you compare AP courses and dual credit courses? What are the advantages of each? (d(3)(B))**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one thing you will tell your family about your career plan tonight, and identify one college credit opportunity (AP or dual credit) available in your pathway. (d(3)(D), d(3)(B))**

### Day 4: eDynamic 6.2: Gaining Experience

**WARM-UP: Besides school, what experiences have you had that might help you in a future career? (Think: sports, clubs, volunteering, hobbies, jobs)**

#### eDynamic Unit 6.2 (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "We have been focused on planning. Now let us talk about GAINING EXPERIENCE. eDynamic Unit 6.2 is all about how you build experience for your career before you even graduate. This includes job shadowing, internships, volunteering, student organizations like TSA, and part-time jobs."

Students work through eDynamic Learning Unit 6.2: Gaining Experience. The unit covers: types of work experience, job shadowing, internships, co-curricular activities, and building a portfolio. Teacher connects the content to TSA and other Irving ISD student organizations.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about how participating in TSA, Student Council, or other school activities builds experience that employers value?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one way you can start building career experience THIS YEAR, before high school. (d(3)(A))**

### Day 5: Writing My Career Plan

**WARM-UP: If someone gave you 5 minutes to explain your entire career plan from now through age 25, what would you say?**

#### Individual Career Plan Writing (30 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today you are writing your official Career Plan. This is the big d(8)(C) artifact for this course. Your plan needs to include: your career goal, your RIASEC type, the pathway you selected, the high school courses you will take, any certifications or degrees you will need after high school, and three action steps you can take right now."

Students write their individual career plan using a structured template. The plan synthesizes their H&L Career Planner data, District Course Planner mapping, Xello Quick Sim insights, and eDynamic experience-building strategies.

**DOK 4: Based on everything you have learned this semester about career planning, what criteria would you use to evaluate whether your plan is strong enough to guide your decisions over the next 4-6 years?**

#### H&L Career Plan Finalization (10 min)

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students finalize their H&L Career Plan in the platform, ensuring all sections are complete. Note: The Coach messaging feature allows students to submit their plan to their teacher for review. Confirm the exact workflow with your H&L district admin -- this is an app-specific feature not documented in the student workbook.

**EXIT TICKET: In one sentence, describe your career goal and the first step you will take toward it. (d(8)(C))**

## Formative Assessment

- MS-to-HS transition knowledge check (Day 1). (d(3)(A))
- Course Planner completion in H&L (Day 2). (d(8)(B))
- Family letter personalization quality (Day 3). (d(3)(D))
- eDynamic 6.2 progress and experience identification (Day 4). (d(3)(A))

## Summative Assessment

Individual Career Plan (Day 5): Students submit a written career plan including: career goal, RIASEC alignment, pathway selection, 4-year course map, postsecondary requirements, and 3 action steps. Scored on: completeness (d(8)(B)), quality of reasoning (d(8)(C)), MS-to-HS transition understanding (d(3)(A)), and evidence of planning impact (d(3)(D)).

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide a career plan template with fill-in-the-blank sections and sentence starters for students who need structure.
- Offer a pre-printed list of Irving ISD CTE pathways with course sequences for students who struggle with the digital Course Planner.
- Allow students to complete their career plan in bullet-point format rather than full paragraphs if needed.

### Extensions

- Students research dual-credit and articulated college credit options for their chosen pathway.
- Challenge students to create a visual career roadmap (poster or digital) showing their path from 8th grade to age 30.
- Students interview a family member about their career path and compare it to their own plan.

### ELL Language Support

- Bilingual Family Career Plan Letter provided in English and Spanish.
- Pre-teach: Graduation Plan = Plan de graduacion, Postsecondary = Postsecundaria, Course Mapping = Mapa de cursos.
- Career plan template available with bilingual headers and sentence stems.
- H&L District Course Planner interface uses visual layouts that support ESL navigation.
