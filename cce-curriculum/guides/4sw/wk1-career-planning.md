## Lesson Objective

Students will revisit their RIASEC assessment results, review all careers favorited across the first semester in Hats & Ladders, use the Career Planner tool to begin selecting a preferred pathway, and complete Xello Quick Sims to experience real-world career decision-making.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can compare my current career interests to my RIASEC results from the beginning of the year, select a preferred career pathway using H&L Career Planner, and explain how my interests have evolved over the first semester."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(8)(A):** Select a career pathway related to personal interests.
- **d(8)(B):** Document high school courses, postsecondary, and training requirements.
- **d(1)(A):** Re-analyze and discuss the results of career assessments at mid-year.

## Materials Needed

- Chromebooks with internet access
- Hats & Ladders student accounts
- Xello student accounts (Quick Sims: The Real Game)
- My Career Journey reflection sheet (from Week 0, updated)
- CareerOneStop Work Values Matcher: https://www.careeronestop.org/Toolkit/Careers/work-values-matcher.aspx
- MyNextMove Interest Profiler: https://www.mynextmove.org/explore/ip
- Irving ISD CTE Pathways poster (displayed in room)
- eDynamic Learning Unit 8.1: Your Turn! Choosing a Career Path

## Career Connection

This is the most important planning week of the year. Students have now explored Manufacturing, IT, Law, Health Science, Agriculture, Human Services, and Business clusters. The H&L Career Planner aggregates all of their assessment data, favorites, and exploration history into a personalized career roadmap. This is where the platform truly pays off -- students are not starting from scratch; they are building on 18 weeks of data.


**Irving ISD Pathway: All pathways across District Wide, Singley Academy, MacArthur, Nimitz, Irving High, and Cardwell/Ratteree. Students narrow to their top 1-2 pathways this week.**

## Vocabulary

- **Career Planner:** In H&L, a tool that compiles a student's RIASEC type, Work Values, Building Blocks, and favorited careers into a personalized career roadmap with recommended next steps.
- **Pathway Selection:** The process of choosing a specific sequence of courses and experiences that aligns with a student's career interests and goals.
- **Mid-Year Reflection:** A structured pause to evaluate progress, reassess goals, and adjust plans based on new information and experiences.
- **Quick Sims:** In Xello, short interactive simulations that let students experience real-world career and life decisions (budget, housing, lifestyle choices).
- **Endorsement:** In Texas high schools, a specialized track (STEM, Business, Arts, Public Services, Multidisciplinary) that students select alongside their graduation plan.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

The H&L Career Planner is the central planning tool for this week. It pulls together everything students have built since Week 0: their RIASEC Core Personality results, Work Values, Building Blocks, and every career they have favorited across all clusters explored in the first three six-week periods. The Planner generates a personalized career roadmap showing recommended pathways, education requirements, and next steps. Students can also use the Coach Dashboard messaging feature to communicate their plans with you.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Gallery Walk:** Post the Irving ISD CTE Pathways poster and printed career cluster summaries around the room. Students walk through and place colored dots on their top 2-3 pathways. This creates a visual snapshot of the class's career interests and sparks discussion about popular and underrepresented pathways.
- **Think-Pair-Share:** After revisiting RIASEC results, students think about how their interests have changed, pair to discuss, and share one surprise with the class.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: RIASEC Revisit: Have I Changed?

**WARM-UP: Look at your original RIASEC results from Week 0. Do you think they would be different if you took the assessment again today? Why or why not?**

#### RIASEC Comparison (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Welcome to the 4th Six Weeks. For the next two weeks, we are shifting from exploring careers to PLANNING for your future. First, I want you to go back into Hats and Ladders and look at your RIASEC Core Personality results from the beginning of the year. Think about everything you have explored since then -- Manufacturing, IT, Law, Health Science, Agriculture, Cosmetology, Entrepreneurship. Have your interests changed?"

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students review their original RIASEC results in H&L. The workbook (Ch 1) calls this "Learning My Core Personality Type" and names the types: Doer, Analyzer, Creator, Helper, Persuader, Organizer. The app's "Discover My Core" section stores results. Students compare their initial personality type to their current career interests by looking at which clusters they favorited the most.

#### Think-Pair-Share: Growth Discussion (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Turn to your partner. Share: What is your RIASEC type? Looking at the careers you favorited this semester, do they match your type? Or did you discover interests that surprised you? That is TOTALLY normal -- career exploration is about trying on different hats."


**DOK 2: How would you summarize the ways your career interests have changed since the beginning of the year, and what experiences caused those changes?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name your RIASEC type and list the 3 career clusters where you have the most favorites. Do they align? (d(1)(A))**

### Day 2: H&L Career Planner Deep-Dive

**WARM-UP: If you had to choose ONE career pathway to study in high school, which one would you choose right now? Write it down.**

#### Career Planner Introduction (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today we are using the most powerful tool in Hats and Ladders: the Career Planner. This tool looks at EVERYTHING you have done on the platform -- your assessments, your favorites, your Building Blocks -- and helps you build a personalized career plan. This is YOUR plan. Nobody else's will look like yours."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students navigate to the H&L Career Planner. The workbook (Ch 16: My Next Steps) includes "My Career and Course Plan" and "Lifestyle Snapshot" activities for career planning. The app's Career Planner displays aggregated data: personality type, Work Values, Building Blocks, favorited careers across all clusters, and pathway recommendations. The workbook also includes "Thinking About My Career Journey" and "Being a Career Thinker" reflections to support this process.

#### Pathway Selection Activity (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "I want you to spend time in the Career Planner narrowing down your options. Look at the pathways that H&L recommends for you. Then look at the Irving ISD pathways poster on the wall. Find the overlap -- which recommended pathways are actually AVAILABLE at your future high school?"

Students use the Career Planner to identify their top 2-3 pathway recommendations, then cross-reference with the Irving ISD CTE Pathways poster to find available local options. They begin documenting their preferred pathway.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about the overlap between H&L's recommended pathways and the actual pathways available at Irving ISD high schools? What would you do if your top recommendation is not offered locally?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name the career pathway you are most interested in and the Irving ISD high school that offers it. (d(8)(A))**

### Day 3: Xello Quick Sims: The Real Game

**WARM-UP: If you earned $40,000 per year, how would you spend your money? List your top 3 expenses.**

#### Xello Quick Sims (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today we are switching to Xello for an activity called Quick Sims: The Real Game. This simulation puts you in real-life scenarios where you have to make career and life decisions -- budgeting, housing, transportation, lifestyle. The choices you make show you what life actually costs and how your career choice affects everything."

Students log into Xello and complete The Real Game Quick Sim. They make decisions about career, housing, transportation, food, and entertainment based on a realistic salary. The simulation shows the financial impact of career choices.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about the relationship between the career you want and the lifestyle you can afford? Did anything surprise you about the cost of living?**

#### Debrief: Career vs. Lifestyle (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "How did that feel? Did anyone run out of money? Did anyone have to change their career choice to afford the lifestyle they wanted? This is exactly why career planning matters -- you need to understand what careers pay and what life costs BEFORE you make big decisions."


**EXIT TICKET: Describe one financial decision you made in the Quick Sim that surprised you and explain how it connects to career planning. (d(8)(A))**

### Day 4: eDynamic 8.1: Choosing a Career Path

**WARM-UP: What is the difference between a career and a job? Write your answer in one sentence.**

#### eDynamic Unit 8.1 (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today we are working through eDynamic Learning Unit 8.1: Your Turn! Choosing a Career Path. This unit walks you through the process of matching your assessment results to career choices, understanding job search steps, and identifying professional organizations related to your interests."

Students work through eDynamic Unit 8.1 at their own pace. The unit covers: personal assessment review, career matching strategies, job search introduction, and professional organization research. Teacher circulates to check for understanding and connect eDynamic content to students' H&L Career Planner results.

**DOK 2: How would you compare the career matching process in eDynamic to the recommendations you received from Hats and Ladders? Do they point to similar careers?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one professional organization related to your career interest and explain what it does. (d(8)(A))**

### Day 5: My Career Journey Update + Gallery Walk

**WARM-UP: Pull out your My Career Journey reflection from Week 0. Read what you wrote about your career interests at the beginning of the year. How does it compare to what you know now?**

#### My Career Journey Mid-Year Update (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Remember this reflection sheet? You filled it out during the first week of school. Today you are going to add a MID-YEAR UPDATE section. Write about: What clusters have you explored? What careers did you favorite? What pathway are you considering? What has changed since Week 0?"

Students update their My Career Journey reflection with a mid-year section. They reference their H&L Climber Profile, Career Planner recommendations, and Xello Quick Sim results.

#### Gallery Walk: Pathway Predictions (20 min)

Post the Irving ISD CTE Pathways poster and cluster summaries around the room. Students place colored dots on their top 1-2 preferred pathways. Class discusses the distribution -- which pathways are most popular, which are underrepresented, and why that information matters for job market demand.


**DOK 4: If you were advising a friend who could not decide between two career pathways, what criteria would you recommend they use to make their decision? Use evidence from this week.**

**EXIT TICKET: Complete this sentence: At the beginning of the year, I was interested in _____. Now I am most interested in _____ because _____. (d(1)(A), d(8)(A))**

## Formative Assessment

- RIASEC revisit discussion participation (Day 1). (d(1)(A))
- Career Planner navigation and pathway identification (Day 2). (d(8)(A))
- Xello Quick Sim completion (Day 3). (d(8)(A))
- eDynamic 8.1 progress check (Day 4). (d(8)(A))

## Summative Assessment

My Career Journey Mid-Year Update (Day 5): Students submit their updated reflection showing growth from Week 0 to mid-year. Scored on: evidence of self-awareness growth (d(1)(A)), pathway selection with rationale (d(8)(A)), and ability to connect assessment data to career choices (d(8)(B)).

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide pre-printed sentence stems for the mid-year reflection: 'My RIASEC type is _____. The clusters I explored this semester include _____. My top pathway is _____ because _____.
- Offer a simplified Career Planner guide with screenshots showing where to find key data in H&L.
- Allow students to use the Irving ISD pathways poster as a visual reference during pathway selection.

### Extensions

- Students create a 4-year high school course plan that maps to their selected pathway using Texas OnCourse.
- Challenge students to research whether their preferred pathway offers dual-credit or articulated college credit opportunities.
- Students compare their H&L Career Planner recommendations to MyNextMove Interest Profiler results.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Career Planner = Planificador de carrera, Pathway Selection = Seleccion de camino profesional, Endorsement = Especializacion.
- Provide bilingual mid-year reflection template with Spanish sentence stems.
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers during the Gallery Walk discussion.
- Xello Quick Sims include visual decision-making interfaces that support comprehension across language levels.
