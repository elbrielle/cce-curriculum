## Lesson Objective

Students will explore the Transportation, Distribution & Logistics career cluster through Hats & Ladders, investigate aviation and air traffic control careers, compare military and civilian pathways into aviation, and build a LEGO air traffic control simulation to demonstrate spatial reasoning and communication skills.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe at least three aviation careers with their education requirements, compare military and civilian pathways into aviation, and demonstrate air traffic control decision-making through a LEGO simulation."

## Career Connection

Aviation is one of the most exciting and well-paying career fields in Transportation. Pilots, air traffic controllers, aviation mechanics, and drone operators are all in high demand. What makes aviation unique is the variety of entry pathways: students can enter through a 4-year college aviation program, through the military (Air Force, Army, Navy, Coast Guard all train pilots and ATC specialists), or through FAA certification programs. Irving ISD is developing an Aviation/Air Traffic Control pathway at Irving High School (coming 2027) and already offers Drone Engineering with FAA Part 107 certification.

**Irving ISD Pathway: Aviation / Air Traffic Control (Irving High - coming 2027) and Drone Engineering (Irving High - FAA Part 107). Students can also connect to the military aviation pathway through JROTC programs.**


## Vocabulary

- **Air Traffic Controller:** A professional who directs the movement of aircraft on the ground and in the air to ensure safe distances between planes.
- **FAA:** Federal Aviation Administration, the government agency that regulates aviation in the United States and issues pilot and ATC certifications.
- **JROTC:** Junior Reserve Officers' Training Corps, a military-affiliated program in high schools that introduces students to military careers and leadership.
- **Military Service Pathway:** A career entry route through military service, which provides training, experience, and certifications that transfer to civilian careers.
- **Part 107:** The FAA certification required to operate drones commercially in the United States. Available through Irving ISD Drone Engineering pathway at Irving High.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In Hats & Ladders, the Transportation, Distribution & Logistics cluster includes aviation-related pathways. Students will explore careers such as Pilot, Air Traffic Controller, Aviation Maintenance Technician, and Drone Operator. The platform's career profiles show the unique education indicators for aviation -- including military pathways, which are a major entry point. This week pairs with eDynamic Unit 2.2 on goal-setting, helping students understand the long-term planning required for aviation careers (many require years of training).


## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Active Monitoring:** During the LEGO ATC simulation, circulate with a checklist tracking: (1) clear communication between 'controller' and 'pilot,' (2) spatial reasoning in runway/taxiway layout, (3) connection between simulation and real ATC careers.
- **Gallery Walk:** Post printed profiles of 4 aviation careers (Pilot, ATC, Mechanic, Drone Operator) around the room with education pathways (military vs. civilian) highlighted. Students rotate through stations and add sticky notes with questions or observations.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: Transportation Cluster: Taking Flight

**WARM-UP: How many people do you think are involved in getting a single airplane from Dallas to New York? Take a guess.**

#### H&L Transportation Cluster Tour (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "We are entering a brand new cluster this week: Transportation, Distribution, and Logistics. This is the cluster that keeps the world moving -- planes, trains, trucks, ships, and drones. Open Hats and Ladders and navigate to the Transportation cluster. Watch the cluster tour video and use Stop and Jot."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore the Transportation, Distribution, and Logistics cluster in H&L. The workbook (Ch 15) lists seven pathways: Diesel and Heavy Equipment Maintenance and Commercial Drivers, Automotive and Collision Repair, Distribution/Logistics/Warehousing, Aviation (Pilots), Aviation Maintenance, Renewable Energy (note: this may overlap with Energy cluster), and Maritime. Key workbook activities: "Transportation Troubles" (survey design for community transit needs), Powerskill: Creativity (8 ideas in 8 minutes brainstorming), and "Delivery Connection App" (warehouse communication app design using focus groups).

#### Aviation Career Profiles (20 min)

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students dive into aviation-specific Hats: Pilot, Air Traffic Controller, Aviation Mechanic, Flight Dispatcher, Drone Operator. They check education requirements, salary data, and rate perks/quirks/job gear.

**DOK 2: How would you compare the daily responsibilities of a pilot versus an air traffic controller? Which role appeals to you more and why?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name two aviation careers and one surprising fact about each. (d(1)(B), d(1)(C))**

### Day 2: Military vs. Civilian Pathways

**WARM-UP: Name one branch of the U.S. military. Do you know anyone who has served?**

#### Military Pathway Exploration (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "One of the most important things about aviation is that there are MULTIPLE ways in. You can go to a 4-year college with an aviation program. You can attend an FAA-approved flight school. Or you can join the military -- the Air Force, Navy, Army, and Coast Guard all train pilots and air traffic controllers. Today we are comparing these pathways side by side."

Students complete the printed Military vs. Civilian Pathways comparison worksheet. They research: (1) Military pathway -- branch, training program, time commitment, benefits, post-service credentials; (2) College pathway -- degree required, flight hours needed, cost, timeline; (3) FAA certification pathway -- requirements, cost, timeline. Students use H&L career profiles, BLS data, and FAA.gov.


**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about the advantages and disadvantages of entering aviation through the military versus through a civilian college program? What factors would influence your decision?**

#### JROTC Connection (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "If you are interested in the military pathway, one thing you can do in HIGH SCHOOL is join JROTC. This is a program that introduces you to military life, leadership, and discipline. It does not commit you to joining the military, but it gives you a head start if you decide to go that direction."

**EXIT TICKET: Compare the military and civilian pathways for becoming a pilot in 2-3 sentences. Which would you choose and why? (d(3)(G))**

### Day 3: LEGO ATC Simulation: Build Phase

**WARM-UP: Air traffic controllers manage dozens of planes at once, making split-second decisions that affect hundreds of lives. What skills do you think that requires?**

#### The Challenge (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "You are now air traffic controllers. Your team has been assigned to manage a small airport. Using LEGOs, you will build your airport runway layout and then run a simulation where you have to safely guide multiple planes in and out. You will need communication, spatial reasoning, and the ability to stay calm under pressure -- the same skills real ATCs use every day."

Engineering Design Process: Define the Problem -- Teams receive scenario cards describing their airport's traffic pattern and must plan a runway layout.


#### Build the Airport (30 min)

Teams build LEGO airports using baseplates and bricks. Each team must include: at least 2 runways, taxiways connecting to a terminal, a control tower, and designated parking areas. Teacher circulates ensuring spatial reasoning and career connections.

**DOK 2: How would you describe the design decisions you made about runway placement and why spacing matters for safety?**

Engineering Design Process: Ideate and Prototype -- Teams design and build their LEGO airport with runways, taxiways, terminal gates, and a control tower. They must account for spacing and traffic flow.


**EXIT TICKET: Name two skills you used during the LEGO build today that an air traffic controller uses in real life. (d(1)(C))**

### Day 4: LEGO ATC Simulation: Run and Iterate

**WARM-UP: What is the most stressful decision you have ever had to make quickly? How did you handle the pressure?**

#### ATC Simulation Run (30 min)

Teams run the ATC simulation. One team member acts as the controller, giving verbal directions. Other members move LEGO planes along the runways and taxiways following the controller's commands. Teacher distributes scenario cards adding complexity: weather delays, emergency landings, simultaneous arrivals. Teams must communicate clearly and make real-time decisions.

**DOK 4: Based on your simulation experience, what recommendations would you make to improve the communication procedures between the controller and the pilots? What evidence from your test runs supports these recommendations?**

Engineering Design Process: Test and Evaluate -- Teams run the simulation, identify communication breakdowns, and iterate on their procedures.


#### eDynamic 2.2: Setting Goals (10 min)

Students begin eDynamic Unit 2.2: Setting Goals. They apply goal-setting strategies specifically to aviation careers -- what steps would they need to take over the next 10 years to become a pilot or ATC?


**EXIT TICKET: Describe one communication challenge you faced during the ATC simulation and how you solved it. (d(4)(A))**

### Day 5: Aviation Presentations + Pathway Favorites

**WARM-UP: On a scale of 1-10, how interested are you in an aviation career after this week?**

#### Team Presentations (20 min)

Teams present their LEGO airport and ATC simulation results. Each team explains: their airport layout design, the communication procedures they developed, challenges they overcame, and how the simulation connects to real ATC careers. 3 minutes per team.

#### H&L Favorites + eDynamic 2.2 Completion (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Go into Hats and Ladders and favorite any aviation or transportation careers that interest you. Then finish up eDynamic Unit 2.2 on setting goals. Think about how the goal-setting strategies apply to YOUR career pathway from last week."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students favorite aviation and transportation careers in H&L and complete goal-setting activities in eDynamic 2.2.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about why goal-setting is especially important for careers like pilot or air traffic controller that require years of training and certification?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name the transportation career you are most interested in and write a 3-step goal to work toward it. (d(4)(A), d(1)(C))**

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide pre-built LEGO airport base designs for teams that need a starting point.
- Offer simplified ATC scenario cards with fewer simultaneous planes for struggling teams.
- Provide a graphic organizer for the military vs. civilian comparison with pre-filled categories.

### Extensions

- Students research the specific Air Force or Navy aviation training pipeline and create a timeline from enlistment to pilot certification.
- Advanced teams add weather or emergency scenarios to their ATC simulation.
- Students investigate the Irving High Drone Engineering pathway and map the courses from 9th to 12th grade.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Air Traffic Controller = Controlador de trafico aereo, Military Service = Servicio militar, Aviation = Aviacion.
- ATC simulation uses verbal commands that practice academic English in context -- provide a command vocabulary list (cleared for takeoff, hold position, taxi to gate).
- Bilingual military vs. civilian comparison worksheet with Spanish headers.
- LEGO activity is hands-on and visually accessible across language levels.
