## Lesson Objective

Students will explore automotive, diesel, and collision repair careers through the H&L Facility & Mobile Equipment Maintenance pathway, research ASE certification and apprenticeship requirements, compare salary data across automotive careers in the DFW area, and investigate career preparation through trade school and apprenticeship pathways.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe at least three automotive careers with their certification requirements, explain the difference between ASE certification levels, and compare salary and education pathways for automotive versus other careers I have explored."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(2)(A):** Research and describe applicable academic, technical, certification, and training requirements.
- **d(2)(B):** Use available resources to research and evaluate educational and training options for one or more careers.
- **d(3)(G):** Investigate opportunities for career preparation through apprenticeships and community/technical colleges.
- **d(5)(E):** Use resources to compare salaries of at least three careers in the student's interest area.

## Career Connection

The automotive industry is one of the largest employers in America, and the DFW metroplex is a major hub for dealerships, repair shops, fleet maintenance, and collision centers. What makes automotive careers special is the apprenticeship model -- many technicians enter the field through paid on-the-job training combined with technical school, earning money while they learn. ASE (Automotive Service Excellence) certification is the industry standard, and Irving ISD offers automotive pathways at Cardwell/Ratteree that lead directly to ASE certification. Students can also earn diesel and collision repair credentials.


**Irving ISD Pathway: Automotive (Cardwell/Ratteree) leads to ASE certification. Diesel & Heavy Equipment and Collision Repair are also available at Cardwell/Ratteree.**

## Vocabulary

- **ASE (Automotive Service Excellence):** The nationally recognized certification for automotive professionals. ASE tests cover areas like engine repair, brakes, electrical systems, and HVAC.
- **Apprenticeship:** A training model where a student or worker learns a trade by working alongside experienced professionals while also completing classroom instruction. Apprentices are usually paid.
- **Diesel Technician:** A professional who maintains and repairs diesel engines used in trucks, buses, construction equipment, and generators.
- **Collision Repair:** The process of restoring vehicles that have been damaged in accidents, including bodywork, painting, and frame straightening.
- **Trade School:** A postsecondary institution focused on hands-on technical training for specific careers, typically taking 6 months to 2 years to complete.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In H&L, the Facility & Mobile Equipment Maintenance pathway within the Transportation cluster contains automotive-related Hats. Students will explore careers like Auto Mechanic, Diesel Technician, Collision Repair Technician, and Automotive Service Manager. The H&L education pathway indicators are especially valuable this week because they clearly show the apprenticeship and certification pathways that differ from the traditional degree model students have seen in previous clusters.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Sentence Stems:** For the salary comparison: 'An automotive technician with ASE certification earns approximately $_____ per year in DFW. Compared to _____, the salary is higher/lower by $_____. The education required takes _____ years and costs approximately $_____.  This helps ESL students articulate data-driven comparisons.
- **Think-Pair-Share:** After exploring the apprenticeship model, students think about the pros and cons of apprenticeship vs. college, pair to discuss, and share with the class.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: Facility & Mobile Equipment Maintenance

**WARM-UP: When was the last time you were in a car that needed repair? What was wrong with it? Who fixed it?**

#### H&L Automotive Pathway Exploration (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "This week we are looking at careers that keep vehicles on the road. Open H&L and find the Facility and Mobile Equipment Maintenance pathway within the Transportation cluster. Explore careers like Auto Mechanic, Diesel Technician, and Collision Repair Technician. Pay close attention to the EDUCATION PATHWAY -- these careers use a different model than the ones we have seen before."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore automotive-related Hats in the Automotive and Collision Repair pathway (confirmed from H&L workbook Ch 15). The workbook describes this pathway as: "Fix, service, and repaint different types of vehicles; diagnose problems, repair damage, and use the latest tools and technologies to keep cars running and looking great." Also explore the Diesel and Heavy Equipment Maintenance pathway. NEISD offers Automotive & Collision Repair and Diesel/Heavy Equipment pathways at the high school level. Students use the Hat Finder to check education indicators (apprenticeship and certification options), DFW salary data, and demand levels.

#### Discussion: Not Every Career Needs College (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "Look at the education requirements for these automotive careers. Notice anything different from the engineering careers we explored last week? Many of these careers do NOT require a 4-year college degree. Instead, they use certifications and apprenticeships. Let us talk about what that means for you."

**DOK 2: How would you compare the education pathway for an Automotive Technician versus a Mechanical Engineer? Which one gets you working and earning money faster?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name two automotive careers and describe the education pathway for each (degree, certification, or apprenticeship). (d(2)(A))**

### Day 2: ASE Certification and Apprenticeships

**WARM-UP: Would you rather get paid while you learn a job, or pay to go to college first and THEN start working? Write one sentence explaining your choice.**

#### ASE Certification Deep-Dive (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "ASE stands for Automotive Service Excellence. It is THE certification that automotive professionals need. There are different ASE tests for different specialties -- engine repair, brakes, electrical, HVAC, and more. The more ASE certifications you earn, the more you can do and the more you earn. Irving ISD offers ASE certification pathways at Cardwell and Ratteree."

Students research ASE certification using ase.com: what areas are tested, how many certifications exist, how to become ASE Master Certified, and how certification affects salary.

#### Apprenticeship vs. College Comparison (20 min)

Students complete the printed Apprenticeship vs. College comparison chart. They compare: time to career entry, cost, income during training, credential earned, and long-term earning potential. They use data from BLS, ASE, and H&L.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about whether the apprenticeship model or the college model is a better fit for someone interested in automotive careers? What factors would change your answer?**

**EXIT TICKET: Explain the apprenticeship model in your own words and name one advantage it has over the traditional college model. (d(3)(G))**

### Day 3: Automotive Salary Showdown

**WARM-UP: Guess: How much do you think an experienced diesel mechanic earns per year in Dallas?**

#### Salary Comparison Activity (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today is all about the numbers. I want you to compare salaries for at least 3 automotive careers using H&L, BLS, and CareerOneStop. But here is the twist -- I also want you to compare ONE automotive career to ONE career from a DIFFERENT cluster you explored earlier this year. How do they stack up?"

Students complete the Automotive Career Salary Comparison worksheet. They compare: Auto Technician, Diesel Technician, Collision Repair Technician, and Automotive Service Manager on salary (DFW), education time, education cost, and job demand. Then they add one career from a different cluster for cross-cluster comparison.

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students pull DFW-specific salary and demand data from H&L career profiles.

**DOK 4: Based on your salary comparison data, if you were advising a student who cared most about earning potential with the shortest education time, which automotive career would you recommend and why? Use specific numbers from your research.**

**EXIT TICKET: Compare the salaries of 3 automotive careers and identify which one has the best ratio of salary to education time. (d(5)(E))**

### Day 4: Cardwell/Ratteree Pathways Deep-Dive

**WARM-UP: Have you heard of Cardwell or Ratteree? What do you know about the programs they offer?**

#### Irving ISD Automotive Pathways (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Irving ISD offers three automotive-related pathways at Cardwell and Ratteree: Automotive, Diesel and Heavy Equipment, and Collision Repair. These are hands-on programs where students spend part of their school day in a real automotive shop, working on real vehicles. By the time you graduate, you can have your ASE certification and go straight to work."

Teacher presents the Cardwell/Ratteree automotive pathways: courses offered, schedule format (AM/PM sessions), certification outcomes, and industry partnerships. Students ask questions and take notes.

#### Trade School and Community College Research (15 min)

Students research additional post-high school automotive training options: UTI (Universal Technical Institute), Lincoln Tech, Tarrant County College automotive programs. They compare these to the Cardwell/Ratteree pathway on time, cost, and outcomes.

**DOK 2: How would you describe the difference between starting your automotive training in HIGH SCHOOL at Cardwell versus waiting until after graduation to attend a trade school?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one advantage of starting the Cardwell/Ratteree automotive pathway in high school instead of waiting until after graduation. (d(2)(B), d(3)(G))**

### Day 5: Automotive Wrap-Up + H&L Favorites

**WARM-UP: After this week, has your opinion of automotive careers changed? Write one sentence explaining how.**

#### H&L Favorites Update (15 min)

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students favorite automotive and transportation careers in their Climber Profile. They review how these connect to their RIASEC type and career plan from Weeks 1-2.

#### Cross-Cluster Salary Presentation (25 min)

Students present their cross-cluster salary comparison (automotive career vs. another cluster career). Class discusses: Is college always worth it? When is a certification pathway the better choice? What factors beyond salary should influence career decisions?

**DOK 4: Based on everything you have learned about automotive careers this week and other careers this semester, create a set of criteria for evaluating whether a career is a good fit. Your criteria should go beyond just salary.**

**EXIT TICKET: Complete this sentence: The most surprising thing I learned about automotive careers is _____ because _____. (d(2)(A))**

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide pre-filled salary data for one of the three automotive careers on the comparison worksheet.
- Offer a simplified apprenticeship vs. college chart with visual timeline graphics.
- Allow students to use CareerOneStop's Compare Occupations tool for side-by-side data rather than manual research.

### Extensions

- Students create a detailed cost analysis comparing the Cardwell/Ratteree pathway (free in HS) to attending UTI after graduation (tuition costs).
- Research the transition from gas vehicles to electric vehicles and how it is creating new automotive careers.
- Students interview a local automotive professional (in person or via video) about their career path.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Apprenticeship = Aprendizaje, Certification = Certificacion, Collision Repair = Reparacion de colisiones, Trade School = Escuela tecnica.
- Bilingual salary comparison worksheet with Spanish headers and sentence stems.
- Use CareerOneStop's Spanish-language career videos for automotive careers.
- Visual timeline comparing apprenticeship vs. college pathways is accessible across language levels.
