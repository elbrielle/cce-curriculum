## Lesson Objective

Students will explore the Health Science career cluster through H&L, investigate the nursing career ladder from CNA to Nurse Practitioner, use micro:bits to simulate vital sign monitoring, and compare education pathways and salaries across nursing levels.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe the nursing career ladder, explain the education required at each level, compare salaries from CNA to Nurse Practitioner, and build a micro:bit vital signs monitor prototype."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(1)(B):** Explore and describe the CTE career clusters.
- **d(1)(C):** Identify various career opportunities within one or more career clusters.
- **d(2)(A):** Research and describe applicable academic, technical, certification, and training requirements.
- **d(2)(B):** Use available resources to research and evaluate educational and training options.
- **d(5)(E):** Use resources to compare salaries of at least three careers in the student's interest area.

## Career Connection

Nursing is one of the most in-demand and rewarding career fields in America. The DFW metroplex has a significant nursing shortage, meaning job opportunities are abundant. What makes nursing unique is the career LADDER -- students can enter as a CNA with just a few weeks of training, then climb to LVN, RN, and eventually Nurse Practitioner, each step increasing their education, responsibility, and salary. At Singley Academy, Irving ISD offers the Nursing Science pathway leading to Patient Care Technician and Phlebotomy certifications.

**Irving ISD Pathway: Nursing Science (Singley Academy) leads to Patient Care Technician and Phlebotomy certifications.**

## Vocabulary

- **CNA (Certified Nursing Assistant):** An entry-level healthcare worker who assists patients with daily activities. Requires a short certification program (4-12 weeks).
- **LVN (Licensed Vocational Nurse):** A nurse who provides basic nursing care under the supervision of an RN or doctor. Requires about 1 year of training.
- **RN (Registered Nurse):** A nurse who provides and coordinates patient care. Requires an associate or bachelor's degree in nursing.
- **Nurse Practitioner (NP):** An advanced practice nurse who can diagnose conditions, prescribe medications, and manage patient care independently. Requires a master's or doctoral degree.
- **Career Ladder:** A progression of jobs within a field, where each step requires more education and offers higher pay and responsibility.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In H&L, the Health Science cluster contains Therapeutic Services, Health Informatics, Diagnostic Services, and Biotechnology pathways. The nursing career profiles in H&L visually show the career ladder concept -- students can see how education level directly correlates with salary. The localized DFW salary and demand data for nursing careers is especially powerful because nursing is a high-demand field locally.


## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Chunking:** Break the nursing career ladder into digestible levels: Day 1 = overview + CNA, Day 2 = LVN + RN, Day 3 = NP + salary comparison, Day 4-5 = micro:bit project. Each day builds on the previous.
- **Stop and Jot:** During the H&L Health Science tour video, students pause twice to write down one thing that surprised them about healthcare careers and one question they have.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: The Health Science Cluster

**WARM-UP: Name every type of healthcare worker you can think of in 60 seconds. How many did you get?**

#### H&L Health Science Cluster Tour (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "We are entering one of the biggest and most important career clusters: Health Science. These are the people who keep us alive and healthy. Open H&L and watch the Health Science cluster tour video. Use Stop and Jot -- pause twice and write down surprises and questions."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore the Health Science cluster in H&L. The workbook (Ch 9) opens with "Exploring the World of Health Science" and lists five pathways: Exercise Science, Wellness and Restoration; Nursing Science; Health Informatics; Diagnostic & Therapeutic Services; and Biomedical Science. The workbook includes a "Making Connections" pair activity where students discuss a healthcare tool or technology they've seen (thermometer, X-ray, blood pressure monitor) and why it's important. The workbook directs: "Go to the Hats & Ladders app and click on the Health Science Cluster" to explore pathways for 15 minutes.

#### Nursing Pathway Introduction (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today we are focusing on one specific part of Health Science: Nursing. But here is what makes nursing special -- it is not just ONE career. It is a LADDER. You can start at the bottom with a CNA certification and climb all the way to a Nurse Practitioner with a doctoral degree. Each step up means more education, more responsibility, and more money."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore nursing-related Hats in H&L: CNA, LVN, RN, NP (Ch 9). Ch 9's Hat Research template directs students to use the Hat Finder to explore Health Science careers and record education, salary, and job description data. They begin the Nursing Career Ladder worksheet by filling in the CNA level.

**DOK 2: How would you describe the difference between a CNA and an RN in terms of what they do each day?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name two levels of the nursing career ladder and one key difference between them. (d(1)(B), d(1)(C))**

### Day 2: Climbing the Nursing Ladder

**WARM-UP: If you could earn money faster with less school (CNA) or earn more money with more school (NP), which would you choose? Why?**

#### Career Ladder Deep-Dive (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today you are completing the full nursing career ladder. For each level -- CNA, LVN, RN, NP -- I want you to find: education required, time to complete, salary in DFW, and one daily task. Use both H&L and the BLS website."

Students complete the Nursing Career Ladder worksheet using H&L salary data and BLS Occupational Outlook Handbook. They track education time, cost estimates, and salary at each level.

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students pull localized DFW salary and demand data from H&L for each nursing level (Ch 9). Ch 9's Hat Research template includes salary recording fields where students document average salary and compare across career levels.

#### Salary Comparison Analysis (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Now let us do the math. Look at your worksheet. A CNA can start working in about 2 months. An NP takes about 8 years of school after high school. But the salary difference is enormous. Is the extra education worth it? Let us analyze."

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about the relationship between education level and salary in nursing? Is more education ALWAYS worth the investment? Use specific salary numbers from your research to support your answer.**

**EXIT TICKET: Compare the salaries of three nursing careers (CNA, RN, NP) and explain which offers the best balance of education time and salary. (d(5)(E))**

### Day 3: Vital Signs Monitor: micro:bit Design

**WARM-UP: What are vital signs? Name as many as you can. (Hint: Think about what a nurse checks when you visit the doctor.)**

#### The Challenge (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "Nurses monitor vital signs constantly -- heart rate, temperature, blood pressure, oxygen levels. Modern hospitals use electronic monitoring systems to do this. Today you are going to BUILD a simplified vital signs monitor using your micro:bit. When you press Button A, it will display a simulated heart rate. When you press Button B, it will display a simulated temperature. This is how real medical technology works at a basic level."

Engineering Design Process: Define the Problem -- Teams receive vital signs monitor specifications: must display at least 2 simulated vital signs with LED animations.


#### MakeCode Programming (30 min)

Students build their vital signs monitor in MakeCode. Button A = heart rate display with pulsing LED animation. Button B = temperature display. Teacher models the first variable and LED pattern, then students extend.

**DOK 2: How would you describe the connection between the programming you are doing today and the technology that real nurses use in hospitals?**

**EXIT TICKET: List the two vital signs your micro:bit monitors and explain why each one is important for patient care. (d(1)(C))**

### Day 4: Vital Signs Monitor: Test and Present

**WARM-UP: If your vital signs monitor showed an abnormal reading, what would a nurse do next?**

#### Refine and Test (15 min)

Engineering Design Process: Prototype & Test -- Teams refine their programs, add features (alarm sound for abnormal readings, shake to reset), and test with partner teams.


#### LEGO Health Tracker Prototype (optional) (10 min)

If LEGOs are available, teams build a physical housing for their micro:bit monitor. This adds an Engineering Design Process layer and connects to manufacturing of medical devices.

#### Team Presentations (15 min)

Teams present: What vital signs does your monitor track? How does it work? How does this connect to what a real nurse or patient care technician does? Connect to Singley Academy's Nursing Science pathway.

**DOK 4: If you were designing a training program for new nursing students, how would you incorporate technology like the micro:bit monitors you built? What would students learn from building before they start working with real patients?**

**EXIT TICKET: Explain one way your micro:bit project simulates real medical technology and connect it to a specific nursing career. (d(1)(C), d(2)(A))**

### Day 5: Health Science Reflection + eDynamic

**WARM-UP: How has your understanding of nursing changed this week compared to what you thought before?**

#### eDynamic Unit 4.2: Experimenting with Careers Part 2 (20 min)

Students complete the eDynamic Learning unit on Health Science career experimentation. This provides a structured curriculum complement to the H&L exploration.

#### H&L Favorites + Climber Profile Update (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Favorite your top 2-3 Health Science careers in H&L. Next week we continue with Dental and Medical Billing -- different corners of the same Health Science cluster. Your career ladder understanding will help you compare."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students favorite nursing careers and check their Building Blocks connections to Health Science (Ch 9). The workbook's Hat Research template includes fields for recording career interests and connecting them to personal strengths.

**EXIT TICKET: Name the nursing career you favorited in H&L and explain whether your RIASEC type is a good fit for healthcare. (d(1)(C))**

## Formative Assessment

- Stop and Jot notes from cluster tour (Day 1). (d(1)(B))
- Nursing Career Ladder worksheet accuracy (Day 2). (d(2)(A), d(5)(E))
- MakeCode program completion and functionality (Days 3-4). (d(1)(C))
- eDynamic unit completion (Day 5). (d(1)(C))

## Summative Assessment

Nursing Career Ladder Analysis + Vital Signs Monitor Presentation: Students submit their completed career ladder worksheet (showing education, time, salary for 4 nursing levels) AND present their micro:bit vital signs monitor. Scored on: salary comparison accuracy (d(5)(E)), education pathway knowledge (d(2)(A), d(2)(B)), and career connection (d(1)(C)).

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide pre-filled career ladder worksheet with CNA level completed as a model.
- Offer simplified MakeCode starter code with one vital sign pre-built.
- Provide a BLS data guide highlighting exactly where to find salary information.

### Extensions

- Students research specialized nursing careers (flight nurse, nurse anesthetist, forensic nurse) and add them to the career ladder.
- Advanced teams add a third vital sign to their micro:bit monitor.
- Students calculate the total cost of education for each nursing level and determine the 'break-even point' where extra education pays off.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Nurse = Enfermera/o, Heart Rate = Frecuencia Cardiaca, Temperature = Temperatura, Patient = Paciente.
- Bilingual career ladder worksheet with Spanish column for each nursing level.
- Visual MakeCode guide with screenshots alongside text instructions.
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers during team activities.
