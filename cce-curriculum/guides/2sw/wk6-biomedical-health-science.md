## Lesson Objective

Students will explore biomedical and biotechnology careers through H&L, research emerging occupations in biomedical science, investigate the heavy postsecondary education requirements, and complete the Health Science cluster wrap-up with finalized career favorites.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe at least three biomedical careers, explain why these careers require extensive postsecondary education, research one emerging biomedical occupation, and finalize my Health Science career favorites."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(1)(D):** Research and evaluate emerging occupations related to career interest areas.
- **d(2)(A):** Research and describe applicable academic, technical, certification, and training requirements.
- **d(5)(A):** Analyze labor market trends related to a career of interest.

## Career Connection

Biomedical science sits at the intersection of healthcare and cutting-edge technology. Biomedical engineers design artificial organs and prosthetics. Lab technicians develop new drugs and vaccines. Research scientists study diseases at the molecular level. These careers are among the most intellectually demanding -- and most rewarding -- in the Health Science cluster. At Irving High School, Irving ISD offers the Biomedical Science pathway leading to the BACE (Biotechnology Office and Competitive Exam) certification.

**Irving ISD Pathway: Biomedical Science (Irving High School) leads to the BACE (Biotechnology Office and Competitive Exam) certification.**

## Vocabulary

- **Biomedical Engineering:** The application of engineering principles to medicine and biology, including designing medical devices, prosthetics, and drug delivery systems.
- **Biotechnology:** The use of living organisms and biological systems to develop products and technologies for healthcare, agriculture, and industry.
- **Clinical Trial:** A research study that tests new medical treatments or drugs on human volunteers to determine safety and effectiveness.
- **Research Scientist:** A professional who conducts experiments and investigations to advance knowledge in a specific field like medicine, biology, or chemistry.
- **Emerging Occupation:** A new or rapidly growing career that reflects current trends in technology, science, or society.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In H&L, the Biotechnology Research & Development pathway introduces students to careers that require some of the most extensive postsecondary education in any field. The career profiles show students that biomedical careers typically require graduate degrees (master's or doctoral), which is an important contrast to the certification-based careers explored earlier in this six weeks. The localized DFW data shows strong growth in biotech due to the region's medical research institutions.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Stop and Jot:** During the NIH career videos, students pause three times to jot down: one technology they saw, one career associated with it, and one question they want to investigate further.
- **Chunking:** Break the biomedical research into manageable pieces: Day 1 = explore, Day 2 = emerging careers research, Day 3-4 = eDynamic + poster, Day 5 = cluster wrap-up.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: The Biomedical Frontier

**WARM-UP: Name one medical technology that did not exist when your grandparents were young. How do you think it was invented?**

#### H&L Biotechnology R&D Pathway (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "We are finishing the Health Science cluster with the most advanced careers in the field: Biomedical Science. These are the careers that INVENT new medical technologies, develop new drugs, and push the boundaries of what is possible in healthcare. Open H&L and explore the Biotechnology Research and Development pathway."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore the Biomedical Science pathway (confirmed from H&L workbook Ch 9). The workbook describes this pathway as: "Combines biology and medicine to research and diagnose diseases, learn about health conditions, patient care, and effective communication in medical settings." Students use the Hat Finder to explore Hats such as Biomedical Engineer, Lab Technician, Research Scientist, Pharmacist, and Genetic Counselor, noting that most require graduate-level education.

#### NIH Career Videos (15 min)

Students watch 2-3 short NIH STEM career videos featuring real biomedical scientists. They use Stop and Jot to capture technologies, careers, and questions.

**DOK 2: How would you describe the education pathway for a Biomedical Engineer compared to a Medical Billing Specialist?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one biomedical career and explain why it requires more education than the healthcare careers we explored earlier. (d(2)(A))**

### Day 2: Emerging Biomedical Occupations

**WARM-UP: COVID-19 vaccines were developed in record time. What types of scientists made that possible?**

#### Emerging Biomedical Career Research (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "Biomedical science is one of the fastest-evolving fields. Careers that exist today did not exist 10 years ago: genomics counselors, AI drug discovery specialists, 3D bioprinting engineers. You are going to research ONE emerging biomedical career and become the class expert on it."

Students select an emerging biomedical career and complete the research template using BLS Career Outlook, H&L, NIH resources, and Google. Fields: career name, what it involves, education needed, salary, why it is emerging, one DFW employer in this field, and labor market trends.

**DOK 4: Based on your research, what argument would you make to convince a school district that biomedical science should be a required course for all high school students? Use labor market data and emerging career trends to support your argument.**

**EXIT TICKET: Name the emerging biomedical career you researched and explain one reason it is growing. (d(1)(D), d(5)(A))**

### Day 3: Biomedical Career Poster + eDynamic

**WARM-UP: If you could invent one medical technology to help people, what would it be?**

#### Career Poster Design (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today you are turning your research into a visual career poster. Think of this as an advertisement for your emerging career. Your poster needs six things: the career name, what the job involves, the education pathway, the salary, a demand trend showing WHY this career is growing, and one image that represents the work. You can use Canva or draw it by hand -- either way, it needs to be clear enough that someone walking by can learn about this career in 30 seconds."

Students create a visual career poster for their emerging biomedical career using Canva or paper. Poster must include: career name, description, education pathway, salary, demand trend, and one image representing the career. Teacher circulates to check that posters include data-driven content from the research template, not just opinions.

#### eDynamic Review (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "While you finish your posters, pull up eDynamic and review the Health Science content. Over the past 4 weeks you have covered nursing, dental, medical billing, and now biomedical. Use this time to connect the dots between what eDynamic taught you and what you explored in H&L."

Students review relevant eDynamic content on Health Science careers to reinforce knowledge from the past 4 weeks of cluster exploration.

**EXIT TICKET: Describe one labor market trend that makes your emerging biomedical career a good choice for the future. (d(5)(A))**

### Day 4: Emerging Career Mini-Presentations

**WARM-UP: If you were an investor, which emerging biomedical career would you invest in? Why?**

#### Mini-Presentations (30 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today each of you presents your emerging biomedical career to your group. You have 3 minutes. Tell your group: what the career does, what education you need, and why it is growing. After everyone presents, your group will vote on which career is most likely to be in high demand in 10 years. Be ready to defend your choice with data."

Students present their emerging biomedical career research in small groups (4-5 students per group). Each student has 3 minutes. Groups discuss: Which career is most likely to grow? Which one would you choose? Why?

**DOK 4: If you were creating a new Biomedical Science pathway for Irving ISD high schools, which 3 emerging careers would you build the pathway around, and what certifications would students earn?**

#### H&L Pathway Fit Assessment (10 min)

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students complete the Hat Research template from the H&L workbook (Ch 9). The template directs students to "Go to the Hats & Ladders app and click on the Hat Finder. Explore Hats in the Health Science career cluster. Choose one Hat and fill out the information: Name of Career, What Interests You, Brief Job Description, Education/Training Needed, Average Salary, and Tools/Equipment/Skills Needed."

**EXIT TICKET: Name the emerging biomedical career from another student's presentation that interested you most and explain why. (d(1)(D))**

### Day 5: Health Science Cluster Wrap-Up

**WARM-UP: We have spent 4 weeks in Health Science. Rate your interest level 1-10 and explain your rating.**

#### H&L Health Science Cluster Wrap-Up (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "This is our last day with Health Science. Over the past 4 weeks you explored nursing, dental, medical billing, PowerSkills, and biomedical careers. Go into H&L and finalize ALL your Health Science favorites. Make sure your Climber Profile reflects what you learned."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students finalize all Health Science favorites and review their complete Climber Profile across Manufacturing, IT, Law, and Health Science clusters.

#### 2nd Six Weeks Reflection (20 min)

Students update their My Career Journey reflection document (originally created in Week 0 of the 1st Six Weeks). They add: clusters explored this six weeks, new favorites, SMART goal progress update, and one PowerSkill they improved. This living document will be reviewed again at mid-year.

**DOK 4: Based on everything you have explored so far -- Manufacturing, IT, Law, and Health Science -- what pattern do you notice about the types of careers that attract you? What does that pattern tell you about what you should explore next?**


**EXIT TICKET: Write: The most important thing I learned about Health Science careers is _____, and the most important thing I learned about MYSELF is _____. (d(1)(D))**

## Formative Assessment

- H&L Biotech pathway exploration (Day 1). (d(2)(A))
- Emerging career research template completion (Day 2). (d(1)(D), d(5)(A))
- Career poster quality and accuracy (Day 3). (d(5)(A))
- Mini-presentation delivery and content (Day 4). (d(1)(D))

## Summative Assessment

2nd Six Weeks Portfolio Update: Students submit their updated My Career Journey reflection (now covering 2 full six-week periods), their completed Emerging Biomedical Career Research Template, and their career poster. Scored on: emerging career research quality (d(1)(D)), labor market analysis (d(5)(A)), education pathway knowledge (d(2)(A)), and self-reflection growth.

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide a list of 6-8 pre-approved emerging biomedical careers with starter research links.
- Offer a career poster template with pre-labeled sections in Canva.
- Provide a simplified reflection template with fill-in-the-blank prompts.

### Extensions

- Students research a specific DFW biotech company (e.g., McKesson, Kimberly-Clark Health) and present what careers they hire for.
- Advanced students calculate the total education cost for a Biomedical Engineer and determine when they break even on salary.
- Students explore the Irving High Biomedical Science pathway and create a full 4-year plan.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Biomedical = Biomedico, Research = Investigacion, Laboratory = Laboratorio, Emerging = Emergente.
- Bilingual research template with Spanish field labels.
- Career poster can include vocabulary in both languages.
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers for group presentations.
