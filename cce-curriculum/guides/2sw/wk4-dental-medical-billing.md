## Lesson Objective

Students will explore Health Informatics (medical billing/coding) and dental science careers through H&L, compare short-term certification pathways with longer degree programs, and connect the Xello Education Experience activity to their career exploration.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe careers in medical billing and dental science, compare the time and cost of different health science certification programs, and explain how school subjects connect to healthcare careers."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(2)(A):** Research and describe applicable academic, technical, certification, and training requirements.
- **d(5)(B):** Classify evidence of high-skill, high-wage, or high-demand occupations based on analysis of labor market information.
- **d(3)(B):** Explore and list opportunities for earning college credit in high school.

## Career Connection

Not everyone in healthcare works directly with patients. Medical billing and coding specialists ensure that healthcare providers get paid correctly -- they translate medical procedures into billing codes. Dental assistants work directly alongside dentists. Both are high-demand careers with relatively short training timelines. At Singley Academy, Irving ISD offers both the Medical Billing (Certified Medical Billing and Coding Specialist) and Dental (Registered Dental Assistant) pathways.

**Irving ISD Pathway: Medical Billing (Singley Academy -- Certified Medical Billing and Coding Specialist) and Dental (Singley Academy -- Registered Dental Assistant).**

## Vocabulary

- **Medical Billing and Coding:** The process of translating healthcare services into standardized codes for insurance billing. Requires specialized certification.
- **Dental Assistant:** A healthcare professional who works alongside dentists, preparing patients, sterilizing instruments, and assisting during procedures.
- **High-Demand Occupation:** A career with many job openings relative to the number of qualified workers. Often indicated by low unemployment and strong job growth.
- **College Credit in High School:** Opportunities like dual credit, AP exams, or articulated credit that allow students to earn college credits before graduating high school.
- **Health Informatics:** The intersection of healthcare and information technology, including medical records management, billing systems, and health data analysis.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In H&L, the Health Informatics pathway shows students the technology side of healthcare. Medical Billing and Coding careers appear here with localized salary and demand data showing strong growth in the DFW area. The Dental Science Hats show the range from Dental Assistant to Dental Hygienist to Dentist. Students can compare certification timelines in H&L.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Sentence Stems:** For the career comparison activity: 'Career A requires _____ education and pays _____, while Career B requires _____ and pays _____. Based on this data, I would classify Career A/B as a high-demand occupation because _____.''
- **Think-Pair-Share:** After completing the Xello Education Experience, students think about which school subject surprised them as relevant to healthcare, pair to discuss, and share.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: Medical Billing and Health Informatics

**WARM-UP: When you go to the doctor, someone has to make sure the bill gets paid correctly. How do you think that process works?**

#### H&L Health Informatics Pathway (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today we are exploring a part of healthcare that most people never think about: the business side. Open H&L and explore the Health Informatics pathway. These careers involve managing health data, billing insurance companies, and keeping medical records accurate."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore the Health Informatics pathway (confirmed from H&L workbook Ch 9). The workbook describes this pathway as: "Focus on managing patient information in healthcare; learn about computerized healthcare systems, maintaining medical records, and understanding how to write and interpret medical reports while following healthcare regulations." Students use the Hat Finder to explore Hats: Medical Biller, Medical Coder, Health Information Technician, Health Informatics Specialist, checking education and certification requirements.

#### Singley Academy Connection (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "Singley Academy offers the Medical Billing and Coding pathway right here in Irving ISD. Students who complete it earn the Certified Medical Billing and Coding Specialist certification, which qualifies them for jobs at hospitals, clinics, and insurance companies."

**DOK 2: How would you describe what a Medical Billing and Coding Specialist does to someone who has never heard of this career?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one Health Informatics career and describe what certification it requires. (d(2)(A))**

### Day 2: Dental Science Careers

**WARM-UP: How often do you go to the dentist? How many different people help you during a dental visit?**

#### H&L Dental Pathways (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Dental careers have a ladder just like nursing. A Dental Assistant needs certification, a Dental Hygienist needs an associate degree, and a Dentist needs 8+ years of education. Let us explore each level."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore dental-related Hats in H&L: Dental Assistant, Dental Hygienist, Dentist, Orthodontist (Ch 9). Ch 9's Hat Research template directs students to use the Hat Finder to explore and record career data including education, salary, and tools/equipment. They compare education requirements and salary for each level.

#### Health Science Career Comparison (20 min)

Students complete the Health Science Career Comparison worksheet, comparing a Medical Biller, Dental Hygienist, and RN on: education time, certification required, salary, job growth, and high-demand classification.

**DOK 3: Based on your comparison data, which of these three health science careers would you classify as the BEST option for someone who wants to start working quickly in a high-demand field? Support your answer with specific data.**

**EXIT TICKET: Classify one health science career as high-skill, high-wage, or high-demand (or a combination) and explain your classification using data. (d(5)(B))**

### Day 3: Xello: Education Experience + School Subjects at Work

**WARM-UP: Which school subject do you think is MOST important for healthcare careers? Math? Science? English? Take a guess.**

#### Xello Education Experience (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today we are using Xello to explore how your current school subjects connect to real careers. You might be surprised to find out that English class matters for medical billing (documentation), math matters for dental hygienists (measurements), and science obviously matters for everyone in healthcare."

Students complete the Xello Education Experience and School Subjects at Work activities. They connect their current classes to healthcare careers they have explored.

#### College Credit in High School Discussion (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Here is something important for your future planning: You can start earning college credit while you are still in high school. Dual credit classes, AP exams, and CTE pathway certifications can all give you a head start. Let us talk about what options Irving ISD offers."

Overview of dual credit, AP, and CTE certification opportunities available at Irving ISD high schools. Students note which options connect to health science pathways.

**DOK 2: How would you describe the advantages of earning college credit while still in high school?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one school subject and explain how it connects to a specific health science career. (d(3)(B))**

### Day 4: Medical Coding Simulation

**WARM-UP: Doctors write medical notes in shorthand. If you had to create a code for 'broken arm,' what code would you invent?**

#### Intro to Medical Coding (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Medical coders translate what doctors do into standardized codes that insurance companies understand. For example, a routine checkup has one code, a broken arm has another, and a surgery has yet another. Getting the code wrong means the hospital does not get paid correctly. Let us try it."

Brief overview of how medical coding works (ICD-10 codes). Show 3-4 examples of procedures and their codes.

#### Coding Challenge Activity (25 min)

Students receive a set of simplified 'patient charts' describing medical visits. They must match each visit to the correct code from a provided code reference sheet. This simulates the core task of a medical billing specialist. Teams compete for accuracy.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about why accuracy is so important in medical coding? What could happen if a code was entered incorrectly?**

Engineering Design Process: Define & Ideate -- Students analyze patient information and match it to standardized codes using a systematic process.


**EXIT TICKET: Explain in one sentence why accuracy matters more in medical coding than in most other jobs. (d(2)(A))**

### Day 5: Health Science Mid-Point + Favorites

**WARM-UP: We are halfway through Health Science. Which health career has surprised you the most so far?**

#### H&L Favorites Update (15 min)

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students favorite dental and medical billing careers in their Climber Profile (Ch 9). They now have Health Science favorites spanning nursing, dental, and health informatics. The workbook's Hat Research template includes fields for recording career interests and connecting them to personal strengths.

#### Career Cluster Reflection (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "You have now explored nursing, dental, and medical billing. Next week we are shifting to PowerSkills -- the soft skills that EVERY healthcare worker needs. And the week after that, we finish Health Science with Biomedical careers. Take a moment to reflect on what you have learned so far."

Students write a brief reflection: Which Health Science pathway fits me best? Which one was I most wrong about before exploring it? How does my RIASEC type connect to healthcare?

**DOK 4: If you were advising a friend who wants to work in healthcare but does not want to go to college for 8 years, what pathway would you recommend? Use specific education and salary data from this week and last week to support your advice.**

**EXIT TICKET: Recommend one Health Science career to a friend and explain your recommendation using education time and salary data. (d(2)(A), d(5)(B))**

## Formative Assessment

- H&L Health Informatics exploration (Day 1). (d(2)(A))
- Career Comparison worksheet accuracy (Day 2). (d(5)(B))
- Xello activity completion (Day 3). (d(3)(B))
- Medical coding simulation accuracy (Day 4). (d(2)(A))

## Summative Assessment

Health Science Career Comparison + Reflection: Students submit their completed comparison worksheet (3 careers, 5 data points each) and written reflection. Scored on: data accuracy (d(5)(B)), classification reasoning (d(5)(B)), education pathway knowledge (d(2)(A)), and college credit awareness (d(3)(B)).

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide a simplified medical coding reference sheet with fewer codes for students who need support.
- Pre-fill one row of the career comparison worksheet as a model.
- Offer sentence stems for the reflection: 'The health science career that fits me best is _____ because my RIASEC type is _____ and this career requires _____.''

### Extensions

- Students research the difference between ICD-10 and CPT coding systems and explain when each is used.
- Advanced students explore the Dental pathway at Singley and create a 4-year high school plan.
- Students calculate the return on investment for each health science certification (cost of training vs. first-year salary).

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Dental = Dental, Medical Billing = Facturacion Medica, Certification = Certificacion, College Credit = Credito Universitario.
- Bilingual medical coding reference sheet with Spanish descriptions of procedures.
- Sentence stems for career comparison in both English and Spanish.
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers for the coding simulation.
