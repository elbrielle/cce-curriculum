## Lesson Objective

Students will explore law enforcement and emergency services careers through H&L, play the scenario-based game for first responders, research training requirements including military pathways, and participate in a first responder simulation activity.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe the training pathways for law enforcement and EMT careers, explain why work ethic and integrity are critical for first responders, and make decisions in a simulated emergency response scenario."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(1)(B):** Explore and describe the CTE career clusters.
- **d(1)(C):** Identify various career opportunities within one or more career clusters.
- **d(2)(A):** Research and describe applicable academic, technical, certification, and training requirements.
- **d(4)(F):** Define and identify examples of work ethic, integrity, dedication, and perseverance.

## Career Connection

First responders are the backbone of public safety. Police officers, EMTs, paramedics, and firefighters put their lives on the line to protect our communities. These careers require a unique combination of physical fitness, quick thinking, emotional resilience, and absolute integrity. Many first responders enter through military service, police academies, or EMT certification programs -- not necessarily through traditional college. At Singley Academy, the Law Enforcement pathway leads to the Non-Commissioned Security Officer Level II certification.

**Irving ISD Pathway: Law Enforcement (Singley Academy) leads to Non-Commissioned Security Officer Level II certification.**

## Vocabulary

- **EMT (Emergency Medical Technician):** A healthcare professional trained to provide emergency medical care and transport patients.
- **Police Academy:** An intensive training program (typically 6-8 months) that prepares recruits to become law enforcement officers.
- **First Responder:** The first trained professional to arrive at the scene of an emergency, including police, firefighters, and EMTs.
- **Integrity:** Doing the right thing even when no one is watching. Critical for first responders who are trusted with public safety.
- **Perseverance:** Continuing to work toward a goal despite challenges and setbacks. Essential for the physical and emotional demands of emergency services.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In H&L, the Law Enforcement Services and Emergency & Fire Management pathways show students the breadth of first responder careers. The scenario-based game for this cluster puts students in the position of making real-time decisions as a first responder. The platform highlights both civilian and military pathways for entering law enforcement.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Active Monitoring:** During the emergency simulation, circulate to observe student decision-making and ensure all team members participate. Note students who demonstrate leadership qualities.
- **Chunking:** Break the first responder training comparison into segments: Day 1 = explore careers, Day 2 = training requirements, Day 3 = scenario game, Day 4 = simulation, Day 5 = reflection.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: Law Enforcement & EMT Careers

**WARM-UP: If you called 911 right now, who would show up? How many different types of first responders can you name?**

#### H&L First Responder Pathways (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today we continue in the Law cluster, but we are shifting from the courtroom to the streets. Open H&L and explore the Law Enforcement Services and Emergency & Fire Management pathways."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore Hats within the Law Enforcement and Fire Science pathways (confirmed from H&L workbook Ch 13). Law Enforcement covers constitutional law, the U.S. legal system, criminal law, and law enforcement terminology. Fire Science covers preparedness, fire suppression, arson investigation, hazardous material management, and public fire safety education. Students use the Hat Finder to explore careers: Police Officer, Detective, EMT, Paramedic, Firefighter, 911 Dispatcher, and rate perks/quirks/job gear for each.

#### From the Field Interviews (15 min)

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students watch From the Field interview videos for first responder careers (Ch 13). These videos are embedded in the H&L app cluster pages and feature professionals from Law Enforcement, Fire Science, and Emergency Services pathways.

**DOK 2: How would you compare the daily routine of a Police Officer versus an EMT?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name two first responder careers and one key difference in their daily responsibilities. (d(1)(C))**

### Day 2: Training Pathways: Many Roads In

**WARM-UP: Do you need a college degree to become a police officer? Take a guess.**

#### Training Comparison Research (30 min)

> **Teacher:** "First responder careers have some of the most diverse entry pathways of any field. You can enter through military service, a police academy, an EMT certification program, a fire academy, or a criminal justice degree. Today you are mapping out these different roads."

Students complete the First Responder Training Comparison worksheet using H&L education indicators and BLS data. They compare: Police Officer (academy), EMT (certification), Paramedic (associate degree), Detective (degree + experience), Firefighter (academy + certification).

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about which first responder career you can enter the FASTEST versus which one requires the most extensive training?**

#### Military Pathway Discussion (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "Did you know that many first responders start their careers in the military? Military Police, Combat Medics, and Military Firefighters gain skills that transfer directly to civilian first responder jobs. This is a pathway many people overlook."

Brief discussion of military-to-civilian career transitions for first responders. Students note this pathway on their worksheet.

**EXIT TICKET: Describe two different training pathways for becoming a first responder and compare the time required for each. (d(2)(A))**

### Day 3: H&L Scenario Game: Emergency Response

**WARM-UP: You arrive at the scene of a car accident. There are two injured people and one bystander. What do you do first?**

#### H&L Scenario-Based Game (30 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today you are a first responder in the Hats and Ladders scenario game. You will face emergency situations and have to make split-second decisions. Remember -- in real life, these decisions can save lives. Pay attention to how the game rewards integrity and quick thinking."

> [H&L PLATFORM] The H&L workbook (Ch 13) includes the "Local Risk Response" project, a major group activity where students form an Emergency Response Task Force for their town. Teams select an emergency scenario (hurricane, wildfire, flood, major accident, earthquake), assign real public service roles (Emergency Management Director, Firefighter, EMT, Police Officer, Public Info Officer, etc.), and develop both individual role-specific response plans and a combined Citywide Emergency Plan. The workbook includes a detailed City Infrastructure Guide for "Silver Ridge" (population 72,000) with buildings, facilities, and 5 historical case files. The workbook also includes "Emergency Essentials: Kit Design" (students design a 10-item emergency kit for earthquake, fire, or flood) and "Missing Painting" (a detective-style investigation activity). Use these activities in place of or alongside a scenario game.

#### Integrity Debrief (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "In the game, were there moments where you had to choose between the easy option and the RIGHT option? First responders face these choices every day. Integrity is not just a nice word -- it is a requirement for this career."


**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about why integrity and perseverance are non-negotiable qualities for first responders, based on your experience in the game?**

**EXIT TICKET: Define integrity in your own words and give one example from the H&L game where integrity mattered. (d(4)(F))**

### Day 4: Emergency Response Simulation

**WARM-UP: In an emergency, every second counts. How would you stay calm under pressure?**

#### Classroom Emergency Simulation (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "Your team is now a first responder unit. I am going to give you emergency scenario cards. You have 3 minutes to read the scenario, assign roles, and present your response plan to the class. You must identify: Who responds first? What information do you need? What resources do you need? How do you keep everyone safe?"

Teams receive emergency scenario cards (car accident, school injury, natural disaster, etc.). They assign roles (incident commander, medical lead, communications), develop a response plan, and present to class. Teacher evaluates teamwork, decision-making, and leadership.

**DOK 4: If you were designing the training program for new EMTs, what real-world scenarios would you include and why? Use evidence from today's simulation to support your recommendations.**

Engineering Design Process: Define the Problem, Research, Ideate -- Teams analyze the emergency scenario, research appropriate response protocols, and develop an action plan.


**EXIT TICKET: Name one transferable skill you used in the simulation that applies to ANY career, not just emergency services. (d(4)(F))**

### Day 5: First Responder Reflection + Cluster Wrap-Up

**WARM-UP: Would you want to be a first responder? What would be the hardest part of the job for you?**

#### H&L Law Cluster Wrap-Up (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "We have spent two weeks in the Law cluster -- legal careers last week, first responders this week. Go into H&L and finalize your favorites for this cluster. You should now have favorites from three clusters: Manufacturing, IT, and Law."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students favorite 2-3 first responder careers and complete any remaining Law cluster activities (Ch 13). The workbook's Hat Research template directs students to record career data including salary, education, and tools/equipment for their selected Hats.

#### Roadtrip Nation Video + Discussion (15 min)

Watch a short Roadtrip Nation career documentary clip featuring a first responder. Discuss: What surprised you about their career journey? Students reflect on how their own RIASEC type connects or does not connect to first responder careers.

**EXIT TICKET: Complete: The first responder career I respect the most is _____ because it requires _____. (d(1)(C), d(4)(F))**

## Formative Assessment

- H&L pathway exploration completion (Day 1). (d(1)(C))
- Training Comparison worksheet accuracy (Day 2). (d(2)(A))
- H&L game participation and integrity debrief (Day 3). (d(4)(F))
- Emergency simulation teamwork and decision-making (Day 4). (d(4)(F))

## Summative Assessment

Emergency Response Plan Presentation (Day 4): Teams present their emergency response plan. Scored on: role assignment logic, resource identification, communication plan, and connection to first responder careers and training requirements. (d(1)(C), d(2)(A), d(4)(F))

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide simplified emergency scenario cards with guided questions for struggling teams.
- Offer a pre-filled training comparison worksheet with one career completed as a model.
- Provide role cards with specific responsibilities for the emergency simulation.

### Extensions

- Students research the Irving ISD Law Enforcement pathway at Singley and create a 4-year plan.
- Advanced students compare civilian police training with military police training using BLS and military career resources.
- Students interview a real first responder (family member, school resource officer) and present findings.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Police Officer = Oficial de Policia, EMT = Tecnico de Emergencias Medicas, Firefighter = Bombero, Integrity = Integridad.
- Bilingual emergency scenario cards with key terms in both languages.
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers during the simulation activity.
- Visual role cards with icons for each simulation role.
