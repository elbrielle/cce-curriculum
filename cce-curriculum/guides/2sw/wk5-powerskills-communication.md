## Lesson Objective

Students will engage with the H&L PowerSkills communication module, practice active listening and assertive communication through healthcare workplace scenarios, and develop time management and goal-setting strategies.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can demonstrate active listening and assertive communication in a workplace scenario, explain why communication is a transferable skill valued in every career, and set a SMART goal for my career exploration."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(4)(A):** Demonstrate effective time-management and goal-setting strategies.
- **d(4)(B):** Identify skills that can be transferable among a variety of careers.
- **d(4)(F):** Define and identify examples of work ethic, integrity, dedication, and perseverance.

## Career Connection

Communication is consistently ranked as the #1 skill employers look for across ALL industries. In healthcare, poor communication can literally be life-threatening -- a nurse who does not listen carefully to a patient's symptoms could miss a critical diagnosis. A medical biller who does not communicate clearly with insurance companies could cost a hospital thousands of dollars. The H&L PowerSkills module teaches communication through workplace scenarios that are relevant to the Health Science cluster students have been exploring.

**Irving ISD Pathway: All Irving ISD Health Science pathways. Communication is a transferable PowerSkill that applies across every career cluster.**

## Vocabulary

- **Active Listening:** Fully concentrating on what someone is saying, understanding their message, and responding thoughtfully -- not just waiting for your turn to talk.
- **Assertive Communication:** Expressing your thoughts, feelings, and needs clearly and respectfully without being passive or aggressive.
- **SMART Goal:** A goal that is Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound.
- **Transferable Skill:** An ability that is valuable across many different careers and industries. Communication, problem-solving, and teamwork are common examples.
- **Time Management:** The ability to plan and control how you spend your time to effectively accomplish your goals.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

The H&L PowerSkills module is a research-backed, game-based intervention developed with support from the National Science Foundation and the Institute of Education Sciences. The communication module covers active listening, accountable talk, and assertiveness through guided instruction, practice games, and scenario-based performance challenges. Healthcare workplace scenarios are built into the module, making this a natural extension of the Health Science cluster exploration.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Think-Pair-Share:** After the H&L PowerSkills diagnostic, students think about their communication strengths and weaknesses, pair to discuss, and share one area they want to improve. This normalizes growth mindset around soft skills.
- **Active Monitoring:** During role-play activities, circulate with a rubric tracking: eye contact, paraphrasing, asking clarifying questions, respectful tone, and body language.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: What Are PowerSkills?

**WARM-UP: Think of the best communicator you know (teacher, family member, friend). What makes them good at communicating?**

#### H&L PowerSkills Introduction + Diagnostic (30 min)

> **Teacher:** "For the past 4 weeks, we have been exploring specific careers -- legal, nursing, dental, IT. But there is something that connects ALL of these careers: soft skills. Hats and Ladders calls them PowerSkills. This week, we are focusing on communication. Open H&L and go to the PowerSkills section. You are going to take a short diagnostic first."

> [H&L PLATFORM] CORRECTION: The H&L Powerskills workbook does NOT have a single "Communication" module with a diagnostic assessment. Instead, communication skills are spread across multiple standalone modules. The most relevant for this week are: "Written Communication" (p. 25-26, students write a professional notice for a neighborhood Little Library), "Giving and Receiving Feedback" (p. 22-24, students practice giving verbal and written feedback using a structured protocol), and "Conflict Resolution" (p. 19-21, students resolve a group disagreement about a lemonade stand product launch). Use the workbook's "Introduction to Powerskills" (p. 1-2) to start the week, which includes a chart of 10 Powerskills with definitions and a "Making Connections" activity where students match Powerskills to careers (Doctor, Plumber, Chef, Artist, Video Game Designer, Principal, Athletic Coach). Then use specific modules across Days 2-4.

#### Communication Skills Overview (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "Your diagnostic results show where you are strong and where you can grow. Today is about awareness. We are going to learn three specific communication skills this week: active listening, accountable talk, and assertive communication."

**DOK 2: How would you describe the difference between hearing someone and actively LISTENING to someone?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name your strongest communication skill based on the H&L diagnostic and one skill you want to improve. (d(4)(B))**

### Day 2: Active Listening Practice

**WARM-UP: Try this: Your partner will tell you about their weekend for 30 seconds. Then YOU have to repeat back what they said word for word. How hard was it?**

#### H&L Active Listening Module (20 min)

> [H&L PLATFORM] Use the H&L Powerskills workbook "Giving and Receiving Feedback" module (p. 22-24). In this activity, students practice giving verbal feedback in pairs and then write constructive written feedback. The module teaches a structured feedback protocol and includes class discussion questions about whether verbal or written feedback is easier and why. Pair this with the active listening role-play activity below, which is teacher-led (not from the workbook).

#### Role-Play: Patient Communication (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Now let us practice. You are a nurse. A patient is explaining their symptoms but they are nervous and keep jumping around in their story. Your job is to ACTIVELY LISTEN and then paraphrase what they said back to them. Ready?"

Students pair up and practice active listening using healthcare communication scenario cards. One student plays the patient describing symptoms; the other practices active listening techniques (eye contact, paraphrasing, clarifying questions). Then they switch.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about why active listening is especially important in healthcare careers compared to a career like data entry?**

**EXIT TICKET: Describe one active listening technique you practiced today and explain how it would help a nurse or EMT do their job better. (d(4)(B))**

### Day 3: Assertive Communication + Goal Setting

**WARM-UP: What is the difference between being assertive and being aggressive? Can you be assertive AND respectful?**

#### H&L Assertive Communication Module (15 min)

> [H&L PLATFORM] Use the H&L Powerskills workbook "Conflict Resolution" module (p. 19-21). In this activity, student groups run a lemonade stand and face a disagreement about when to launch a new flavor: one person wants to launch immediately, another says to wait, and a third is unsure. Students work through the conflict using resolution strategies. The module includes reflection on how conflicts are resolved in the workplace. This connects assertive communication to practical problem-solving. Additionally, the "Advocacy" module (p. 30-32) teaches students to speak up for themselves and others -- another form of assertive communication.

#### SMART Goal Setting (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "We are halfway through the year's career exploration. It is time to set a goal. Not just any goal -- a SMART goal. This stands for Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. Your goal should be about your career exploration journey. Example: 'By the end of the 4th Six Weeks, I will have explored at least 8 career clusters and favorited at least 15 careers in H&L.'"

Students create a SMART goal for their career exploration using the printed worksheet. Teacher provides examples and non-examples. Students write their goal, identify how they will measure progress, and set a deadline.

**DOK 4: Design a SMART goal for a student who is interested in healthcare careers but is not sure which pathway to pursue. How would you make the goal specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound?**

**EXIT TICKET: Write your SMART career exploration goal in one complete sentence. (d(4)(A))**

### Day 4: Communication in the Workplace Scenarios

**WARM-UP: Think of a time when miscommunication caused a problem. What happened and how could it have been prevented?**

#### H&L Scenario-Based Performance Challenge (20 min)

> [H&L PLATFORM] Use the H&L Powerskills workbook "Written Communication" module (p. 25-26) as the capstone activity for this week. Students imagine they are responsible for a neighborhood Little Library and must write a professional notice about its status. This combines written communication, audience awareness, and professional tone. Pair this with the teacher-led workplace communication role-play below to create a comprehensive communication skills day that covers both written and oral communication in workplace contexts.

#### Workplace Communication Challenge (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Now a bigger challenge. Each team will receive a workplace scenario card. You will role-play the scenario in front of the class. The class will evaluate your communication skills: Did you listen actively? Were you assertive but respectful? Did you solve the problem?"

Teams receive workplace communication challenge cards (e.g., a nurse explaining a difficult diagnosis to a family, a tech support agent helping a frustrated customer, a legal assistant communicating a deadline to a busy attorney). They prepare and perform a 3-minute role-play. Class provides feedback using a communication rubric.

**DOK 3: Based on all the scenarios you saw today, what conclusions can you draw about which communication skill -- active listening, assertiveness, or accountable talk -- is most important in the workplace?**


**EXIT TICKET: Name one communication skill you demonstrated in today's role-play and rate yourself 1-5 on how well you used it. (d(4)(B), d(4)(F))**

### Day 5: Skills Reflection + CareerOneStop Skills Matcher

**WARM-UP: If you had to hire someone for a job, what soft skills would you look for? List your top 3.**

#### CareerOneStop Skills Matcher (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "We have been working on communication skills all week. Now let us see how ALL your skills connect to careers. Go to CareerOneStop Skills Matcher -- this tool asks you to rate yourself on 40 different skills and then shows you careers that match."

Students complete the CareerOneStop Skills Matcher and compare results to their H&L Climber Profile recommendations.

#### PowerSkills Reflection + SMART Goal Check (15 min)

Students revisit their SMART goal from Day 3. Is it still realistic? Do they need to adjust? They write a brief reflection on what they learned about communication this week and how it connects to the careers they have explored.

**DOK 4: If you were training a new employee at a hospital, how would you teach them the communication skills we practiced this week? Design a 30-minute training session.**

**EXIT TICKET: Complete: The transferable skill I improved most this week is _____ and it matters for my career exploration because _____. (d(4)(B))**

SMART Goal + Communication Reflection: Students submit their SMART goal worksheet and a written reflection connecting communication skills to at least two careers they have explored this semester. Scored on: SMART goal quality (d(4)(A)), identification of transferable skills (d(4)(B)), and connection to career exploration (d(4)(F)).

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide SMART goal examples and non-examples to help students understand each component.
- Offer simplified role-play scripts with dialogue suggestions for students who need support.
- Provide sentence stems for the reflection: 'Active listening is important for _____ (career) because _____.''

### Extensions

- Students create a 'Communication Skills Guide' for their chosen career pathway, listing the top 5 communication skills needed.
- Advanced students research emotional intelligence and explain how it connects to PowerSkills.
- Students compare their CareerOneStop Skills Matcher results to their H&L RIASEC results and analyze the overlap.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Active Listening = Escucha Activa, Assertive = Asertivo, Goal = Meta, Transferable = Transferible.
- Bilingual role-play cards with key phrases in both languages.
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers for role-play activities.
- SMART goal worksheet with bilingual prompts and examples.
