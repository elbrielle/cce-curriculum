## Lesson Objective

Students will explore the Law, Public Safety, Corrections & Security career cluster through H&L, investigate legal careers and their education requirements, and engage in an AI ethics debate activity that connects technology to the legal profession.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe at least three careers in the legal field, explain the education pathway from middle school to becoming a lawyer or paralegal, and discuss ethical issues related to AI in the legal system."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(1)(B):** Explore and describe the CTE career clusters.
- **d(1)(C):** Identify various career opportunities within one or more career clusters.
- **d(3)(I):** Define entrepreneurship and identify entrepreneurial opportunities within a field of personal interest.
- **d(3)(H):** Identify professional associations affiliated with a particular career pathway.

## Career Connection

The legal profession is one of the oldest and most respected career fields. From courtroom attorneys to paralegals who prepare critical documents, legal professionals keep our justice system functioning. What many students do not realize is that the legal field also includes entrepreneurial opportunities -- solo practice attorneys, private investigators, and legal consultants all run their own businesses. At Irving ISD, the Law Enforcement pathway at Singley Academy introduces students to the broader Law & Public Safety cluster.

**Irving ISD Pathway: Law Enforcement (Singley Academy). Students explore legal careers that connect to the broader Law, Public Safety, Corrections & Security cluster.**

## Vocabulary

- **Paralegal:** A professional who assists lawyers with legal research, document preparation, and case management. Does not require a law degree.
- **Probable Cause:** A reasonable basis to believe a crime may have been committed, allowing legal action like an arrest or search.
- **Due Process:** The legal requirement that the government must respect all legal rights owed to a person.
- **Bar Association:** A professional association for lawyers. Most states require lawyers to pass the Bar Exam to practice law.
- **Entrepreneur (Legal):** A person who starts their own legal business, such as a solo law practice, mediation firm, or legal consulting company.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In H&L, the Law, Public Safety, Corrections & Security cluster includes pathways like Legal Services, Law Enforcement Services, and Corrections. The platform's career profiles for legal careers show the extensive education pathway (undergraduate degree plus law school for attorneys, or associate degree for paralegals). The vocabulary activities in the Legal Services pathway teach terms like probable cause and due process -- which is exactly what students saw when we accessed the H&L app earlier.


## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Think-Pair-Share:** After exploring legal careers in H&L, students think about whether they would rather argue cases in court or research cases behind the scenes, pair to discuss, then share. This helps students distinguish between different legal career personalities.
- **Sentence Stems:** For the AI ethics debate: 'I believe AI should/should not be used in the legal system because _____. One piece of evidence that supports my position is _____.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: The Law Cluster: Beyond Lawyers

**WARM-UP: If you were accused of something you did not do, who would you want on your side? Describe that person.**

#### H&L Law Cluster Tour (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Welcome to the 2nd Six Weeks. We are leaving IT and Manufacturing behind and entering a completely different world: Law, Public Safety, Corrections, and Security. Open H&L and navigate to this cluster. Watch the tour video and review the Myth Busters."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore the Law and Public Service cluster in H&L. The H&L workbook (Ch 13) opens with "Exploring the World of Law and Public Service" and lists four pathways: Fire Science, Government and Public Administration, Law Enforcement, and Legal Studies. The workbook includes a "Making Connections" pair activity where students discuss how people in this cluster help communities. The workbook directs: "Go to the Hats & Ladders app and click on the Law and Public Service Cluster" to explore pathways for 15 minutes.

#### Legal Services Pathway (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Now explore the Legal Services pathway specifically. Look at careers like Lawyer, Paralegal, Judge, Court Reporter, and Legal Secretary. Check the education requirements for each one -- you will notice a HUGE range."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore Legal Services pathway Hats and compare education requirements across legal careers (Ch 13). Ch 13's Hat Research template directs students to use the Hat Finder to explore and record career data including education, salary, and job descriptions.

**DOK 2: How would you compare the education pathway for a Paralegal versus an Attorney? Which can you enter the workforce sooner?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name two legal careers and describe one key difference in their education requirements. (d(1)(B), d(1)(C))**

### Day 2: Law & Entrepreneurship

**WARM-UP: Can a lawyer be their own boss? How would that work?**

#### Entrepreneurship in Law (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today we are connecting law to entrepreneurship. Many lawyers start their own firms. Private investigators run their own businesses. Mediators work independently. Let us explore what it means to be an entrepreneur in the legal field."

Students research one legal entrepreneurship opportunity using H&L career profiles and BLS data. They identify: startup requirements, typical income, and advantages/disadvantages of self-employment in law.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about the risks and rewards of starting your own law practice versus working for a large law firm?**

#### Professional Associations Research (15 min)

Students research the American Bar Association, National Paralegal Association, or Texas Bar Association. They identify: what the association does, how to join, and why professionals join these organizations.

**DOK 2: How would you describe the purpose of a professional association like the Bar Association?**

**EXIT TICKET: Define entrepreneurship in the legal field and name one professional association a lawyer might join. (d(3)(I), d(3)(H))**

### Day 3: iCivics: Justice in Action

**WARM-UP: What does 'innocent until proven guilty' mean? Why is that important?**

#### iCivics Game Play (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today you are going to experience the legal system through a game. iCivics was created to help students understand how our government and legal system works. You are going to play one of the courtroom games where you act as a lawyer arguing a case."

Students play an iCivics courtroom or government simulation game (teacher selects based on available options). Games are free, browser-based, and highly engaging for middle schoolers.

**DOK 3: Based on your experience in the iCivics game, what conclusions can you draw about why communication skills are essential for legal professionals?**

**EXIT TICKET: Describe one legal concept you learned from the iCivics game today. (d(1)(C))**

### Day 4: AI Ethics in the Legal System

**WARM-UP: Should a computer program be allowed to decide whether someone goes to jail? Write your initial reaction.**

#### AI Ethics Debate (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "This is where law meets technology. Some courts are using AI to help make decisions about bail, sentencing, and evidence analysis. Is that fair? Today you are going to debate this question using evidence. Half the class will argue FOR using AI in courts, half will argue AGAINST."

Structured debate using sentence stems. Students prepare 3-minute arguments using evidence from their career research. Teacher moderates. Connect to d(4)(F) -- integrity and ethical decision-making in legal careers.


**DOK 4: What criteria would you use to evaluate whether AI should be used in a specific part of the legal system, like determining bail amounts? Consider fairness, accuracy, and ethics in your answer.**

**EXIT TICKET: State your position on AI in the legal system in one sentence with one piece of supporting evidence. (d(1)(C))**

### Day 5: Law Cluster Wrap-Up + Favorites

**WARM-UP: After this week, could you see yourself working in the legal field? Why or why not?**

#### H&L Favorites + Career Plan Update (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Go into H&L and favorite your top legal careers. Then check your Climber Profile -- you now have favorites from Manufacturing, IT, AND Law. Your profile is getting richer."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students favorite 2-3 legal careers and review how their RIASEC type connects to the Law cluster (Ch 13). The workbook's Hat Research template includes fields for recording career interests and connecting them to personal strengths.

#### Xello: Life Experience Activity (15 min)

Students complete the Xello Life Experience activity, reflecting on experiences that connect to careers they have explored so far.

**EXIT TICKET: Complete: The legal career that interests me most is _____ because _____. (d(1)(C))**

## Formative Assessment

- H&L cluster tour notes (Day 1). (d(1)(B))
- Entrepreneurship research accuracy (Day 2). (d(3)(I))
- iCivics game engagement and learning (Day 3). (d(1)(C))
- Debate preparation and participation (Day 4). (d(1)(C))

## Summative Assessment

AI Ethics Position Paper (short paragraph): Students write a 5-7 sentence position paper on whether AI should be used in the legal system, citing at least one legal career that would be affected and one professional association that might weigh in on the issue. (d(1)(C), d(3)(I), d(3)(H))

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide pre-filled debate cards with evidence for both sides of the AI ethics debate.
- Offer simplified iCivics game options for students who need support.
- Provide a legal career vocabulary word wall with visual definitions.

### Extensions

- Students research a famous Supreme Court case and present how different legal professionals contributed.
- Advanced students explore the Irving ISD Law Enforcement pathway at Singley and create a 4-year plan.
- Students compare legal career salaries in Texas versus other states using BLS data.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Lawyer = Abogado, Paralegal = Paralegal/Asistente Legal, Judge = Juez, Due Process = Debido Proceso.
- Bilingual debate sentence stems in English and Spanish.
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers during iCivics gameplay.
- iCivics games are highly visual and provide scaffolded instructions.
