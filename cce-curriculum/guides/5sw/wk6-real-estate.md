## Lesson Objective

Students will explore real estate careers through the H&L Finance or Business cluster, research Texas real estate licensing requirements through TREC, compare commission-based income to salary-based income, connect entrepreneurship concepts to real estate, and analyze DFW real estate labor market trends.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe at least three real estate careers with their licensing requirements, explain the difference between commission-based and salary-based income, and analyze why the DFW real estate market creates career opportunities."

- **d(2)(A):** Research and describe applicable licensing requirements for real estate careers.
- **d(3)(I):** Define entrepreneurship and identify entrepreneurial opportunities in real estate.
- **d(5)(A):** Analyze labor market trends in the DFW real estate market.

## Career Connection

Real estate is a career that blends sales skills, market knowledge, and entrepreneurship. In the DFW metroplex -- one of the fastest-growing real estate markets in the country -- agents, brokers, appraisers, and property managers have extraordinary opportunities. What makes real estate unique is the INCOME MODEL: most real estate agents work on commission, meaning they earn a percentage of each sale rather than a fixed salary. This means income is directly tied to effort and skill. Real estate also has a strong entrepreneurship connection -- many agents are essentially running their own business. Texas requires a TREC (Texas Real Estate Commission) license, which students can begin earning right out of high school.


**Irving ISD Pathway: Real Estate (Singley Academy) leads to Real Estate Sales Agent certification. This pathway prepares students for TREC licensing and entry into the real estate profession.**

## Vocabulary

- **Real Estate Agent:** A licensed professional who helps people buy, sell, or rent properties. Agents must pass a state licensing exam.
- **TREC:** Texas Real Estate Commission -- the state agency that regulates real estate licensing and practice in Texas.
- **Commission:** A payment based on a percentage of a sale. Real estate agents typically earn 2.5-3% of a home's sale price per transaction.
- **Appraiser:** A professional who determines the market value of a property. Appraisers must be licensed and follow strict guidelines.
- **Entrepreneurship in Real Estate:** Many real estate professionals operate as independent contractors or business owners, making real estate a natural fit for entrepreneurially-minded students.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In H&L, real estate careers may appear under the Finance cluster or Business Marketing & Finance. Students can explore Hats like Real Estate Agent, Real Estate Broker, Property Manager, Appraiser, and Mortgage Loan Officer. The key feature this week is connecting the entrepreneurship standards (d(3)(I)) to real estate: most agents are independent contractors who essentially run their own business. The TREC licensing research adds a Texas-specific real-world dimension.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Gallery Walk:** Post 4 real estate career profiles (Agent, Broker, Appraiser, Property Manager) around the room with income data. Students rotate and note the difference between commission-based and salary-based roles.
- **Modeling:** Teacher models a commission calculation on the projector: If a home sells for $350,000 and the agent earns 3% commission, how much do they earn from that one sale? How many sales per year would they need to earn $75,000?

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: Real Estate Career Exploration

**WARM-UP: How much do you think a house costs in your neighborhood? Take a guess.**

#### H&L Real Estate Career Profiles (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Our last new cluster exploration of the year is real estate. This is a career field where you can earn great money, be your own boss, and help people find their homes. Open H&L and explore real estate careers."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore real estate Hats in H&L. The workbook (Ch 5: Business, Marketing, and Finance) confirms Real Estate as one of six pathways: "Helping people buy, sell, or rent homes and buildings." Students use the Hat Finder to explore: Real Estate Agent, Broker, Appraiser, Property Manager, Mortgage Loan Officer. They check education/licensing requirements, salary data, and demand.

#### Gallery Walk: Real Estate Roles (15 min)

Students rotate through 4 posted career profiles, noting the difference between commission-based roles (Agent, Broker) and salary-based roles (Appraiser, Property Manager). They identify which roles require TREC licensing.

**DOK 2: How would you describe the key difference between earning a commission and earning a salary? Which model do you think would motivate you more?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name two real estate careers: one that earns commission and one that earns a salary. Explain the difference. (d(2)(A))**

### Day 2: TREC Licensing + Commission Math

**WARM-UP: If you earned 3% of every sale you made, and you sold a $300,000 house, how much would you earn from that one sale?**

#### TREC Licensing Research (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "In Texas, you need a license from TREC -- the Texas Real Estate Commission -- to work as a real estate agent. Today you are going to research exactly what it takes to get that license: How old do you have to be? How many course hours? What exam do you take? How much does it cost?"

Students research TREC licensing requirements at trec.texas.gov: minimum age (18), education requirements (180 hours of coursework), exam details, fingerprinting/background check, and sponsoring broker requirement. They note that Singley Academy offers coursework that counts toward these hours.

#### Commission Calculation Activity (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Let us do the math of real estate. Real estate agents typically earn 2.5-3% commission on each sale. But here is the catch: you do not sell a house every day. Some months you sell 3 houses. Some months you sell zero. That is why real estate income is called variable income. Let us calculate what this looks like over a year."

Students complete the Commission vs. Salary Comparison worksheet. They calculate: commission on homes at various prices ($200K, $350K, $500K), annual income if they sell 6/12/20 homes per year, and compare this to a salaried career like Property Manager ($55K/year fixed). They discuss the risks and rewards of variable income.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about the financial risks and rewards of commission-based income? What kind of person is best suited for this income model?**

**EXIT TICKET: How many homes at $300,000 would you need to sell per year to earn $60,000 in commissions at 3%? Show your math. (d(2)(A))**

### Day 3: Entrepreneurship in Real Estate

**WARM-UP: What does it mean to be your own boss? Would you want that? Why or why not?**

#### Entrepreneurship Connection (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Remember when we studied entrepreneurship back in the 3rd Six Weeks? Real estate is one of the BEST examples of entrepreneurship in action. Most agents are independent contractors -- they are not employees of a company. They set their own schedule, find their own clients, and their income depends entirely on their own effort. Many agents even start their own brokerages."

Teacher connects the d(3)(I) entrepreneurship standards to real estate: agents as independent contractors, creating a personal brand, marketing yourself, building client relationships, and eventually starting a brokerage.

**DOK 4: Based on everything you have learned about entrepreneurship this year -- from the clothing company logo project in 3rd Six Weeks to real estate this week -- create a set of characteristics that define a successful entrepreneur. Which characteristics do you have?**


#### DFW Real Estate Market Analysis (15 min)

Students research why the DFW real estate market is booming: population growth, corporate relocations (Toyota, Charles Schwab, Goldman Sachs moved to DFW), new construction, and rising home values. They connect market trends to career demand.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about why the DFW area is one of the best places in the country to start a real estate career?**

**EXIT TICKET: Define entrepreneurship in your own words and give two examples of how it applies to real estate careers. (d(3)(I))**

### Day 4: Real Estate Market Trends + Career Plan Update

**WARM-UP: Do you think home prices in DFW will go up or down over the next 10 years? What evidence supports your prediction?**

#### Labor Market Trends Analysis (20 min)

Students analyze DFW real estate labor market trends: job growth projections, average number of real estate transactions per year, median home price trends, and factors affecting the market (interest rates, population growth, inventory). They connect this data to career opportunity.

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students use H&L localized data for real estate career demand in the DFW area.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about how factors like interest rates and population growth affect the number of real estate jobs available in DFW?**

#### Career Plan Update: 5th Six Weeks (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Update your H&L Career Plan with everything you learned in the 5th Six Weeks: architecture, engineering, construction, trades, personal finance, and real estate. Make sure your favorites are current and your course plan still reflects your interests."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students update their Career Plan and favorites in H&L with all 5th Six Weeks exploration data.

**EXIT TICKET: Name one labor market trend affecting DFW real estate and explain how it creates career opportunity. (d(5)(A))**

### Day 5: Real Estate Wrap-Up + 5th Six Weeks Reflection

**WARM-UP: You have explored Architecture, Engineering, Construction, Trades, Budgeting, and Real Estate over the past 6 weeks. Which week was your favorite and why?**

#### Real Estate Pitch Activity (20 min)

Fun application: Students create a 2-minute 'elevator pitch' for a DFW real estate career, using data from the week to sell the career to a hypothetical student who is undecided. They include: earning potential, licensing pathway, entrepreneurship opportunities, and market demand. Volunteers present to the class.

#### 5th Six Weeks Reflection (20 min)

Students write a reflection covering the entire 5th Six Weeks: What clusters did you explore? What was the most valuable thing you learned about budgeting? Has your career pathway changed? What do you want to explore in the final six weeks (Education, Arts, Business/Marketing, and Capstone)? Update the My Career Journey document.

**DOK 4: Looking at all the career clusters you have explored this year, create a personal 'Career Fit Ranking' of your top 5 careers. For each, explain what makes it a good fit based on your RIASEC type, salary expectations, education preferences, and personal interests.**

**EXIT TICKET: Name your top career choice as of today and explain how your personal budget work this week influenced (or did not influence) that choice. (d(5)(A), d(5)(D))**

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide commission calculation examples with step-by-step math for students who need support.
- Offer a simplified TREC licensing research guide with the specific website pages to visit.
- Allow students to complete the elevator pitch in written form rather than presenting orally.

### Extensions

- Students research and present on the difference between a real estate agent and a real estate investor.
- Calculate the total commission earned over a 20-year real estate career with increasing sales volume and home prices.
- Students compare TREC licensing requirements to real estate licensing in another state.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Real Estate = Bienes raices, Commission = Comision, License = Licencia, Appraiser = Tasador.
- Commission math worksheets use numbers and formulas that are language-accessible.
- Bilingual TREC research guide with Spanish headers.
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers during the elevator pitch activity.
