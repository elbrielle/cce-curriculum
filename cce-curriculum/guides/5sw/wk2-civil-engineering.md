## Lesson Objective

Students will explore civil and structural engineering careers through H&L, investigate emerging occupations in green building and smart infrastructure, research the impact of standardized tests (PSAT/SAT/ACT) on college admissions for engineering programs, and complete a bridge-building challenge.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe at least three engineering careers, explain how PSAT/SAT/ACT scores affect college admission for engineering programs, and apply engineering design principles to build a weight-bearing bridge."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(1)(D):** Research and evaluate emerging occupations (green building, smart infrastructure).
- **d(2)(A):** Research and describe academic and training requirements for engineering careers.
- **d(3)(E):** Explain the impact of standardized testing (PSAT/SAT/ACT/ASVAB/TSI) on postsecondary opportunities.

## Career Connection

Civil engineers design and maintain infrastructure: roads, bridges, water systems, airports, and buildings. In DFW, massive population growth creates strong demand. MacArthur offers pathways connecting to architecture and construction technology. Most university engineering programs are competitive, and standardized test scores play a significant role in admissions -- making this the ideal week for d(3)(E).


**Irving ISD Pathway: Architecture and Construction Technology (MacArthur). Welding (MacArthur - Structural Steel) connects to structural engineering.**


## Vocabulary

- **Civil Engineer:** An engineer who designs, builds, and maintains infrastructure.
- **PSAT/SAT:** College Board standardized tests for admissions and National Merit Scholarship.
- **ACT:** Alternative college admissions test measuring English, math, reading, science.
- **Smart Infrastructure:** Modern infrastructure using sensors and data to manage systems efficiently.
- **Green Building:** Construction practices reducing environmental impact. LEED certification is the standard.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In H&L, students explore engineering Hats in both A&C and STEM clusters: Civil Engineer, Structural Engineer, Surveyor, Construction Manager. The emerging occupations focus targets green building specialists and smart infrastructure engineers.

- **Active Monitoring:** During bridge challenge, track collaboration, EDP usage, and career connections.
- **Gallery Walk:** Post PSAT/SAT/ACT/ASVAB/TSI cards around room. Students rotate and take notes.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: Civil Engineering in H&L

**WARM-UP: Name a bridge or road in Irving you use regularly. Someone ENGINEERED that.**

#### H&L Engineering Exploration (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Last week was architecture -- design. This week is engineering -- making designs WORK. Open H&L and explore engineering careers."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore engineering Hats across both A&C and Engineering clusters. The Engineering cluster (Ch 8) includes Civil Engineering as a confirmed pathway alongside Mechanical/Aerospace Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Geospatial Engineering, and Drone Engineer. The A&C cluster (Ch 3) includes Construction Management and Inspection. The workbook's "Infrastructure Imagination" activity (Ch 8) is a civil engineering project where students solve traffic and flooding problems for the city of Los Lomas. Students use the Hat Finder to explore: Civil Engineer, Structural Engineer, Surveyor, Environmental Engineer, Construction Manager.

#### Emerging Engineering Occupations (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Green Building Engineer, Smart Infrastructure Specialist -- these are emerging occupations driven by climate change and technology."

Students begin Emerging Engineering Careers research template.

**DOK 2: How does a traditional Civil Engineer differ from a Green Building Engineer?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one established and one emerging engineering career with education requirements. (d(1)(D), d(2)(A))**

### Day 2: PSAT/SAT/ACT: Why They Matter

**WARM-UP: Have you heard of the SAT? Does it affect you right now?**

#### Standardized Test Overview (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "If you want to study engineering at a university, you need to know about the PSAT, SAT, and ACT. These tests are used for admissions and scholarships. For competitive programs like engineering, scores can determine whether you get in."

Teacher presents PSAT, SAT, ACT, ASVAB, TSI overview. Students complete PSAT/SAT Impact worksheet.

#### Gallery Walk: Test Comparison (15 min)

Students rotate through test comparison cards, take notes, discuss which tests apply to their pathway.

**DOK 3: How could standardized test performance impact your ability to pursue engineering?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name the test most relevant to YOUR pathway and explain why. (d(3)(E))**

### Day 3: Bridge Challenge: Plan

**WARM-UP: What makes a bridge strong?**

#### The Challenge (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "You are civil engineers. Build a bridge spanning 12 inches that holds maximum weight with limited materials."

Engineering Design Process: Define the Problem.

#### Research and Design (30 min)

> **Teacher:** "Research bridge types: trusses, arches, beams. Sketch at least 2 designs before picking one."

Teams sketch designs, label structural elements, estimate weak points.

**DOK 2: Why is a truss stronger than a simple beam?**

Engineering Design Process: Research and Ideate -- Teams sketch 2-3 options, select best.


**EXIT TICKET: Sketch your bridge design with labeled structural elements. (d(1)(D))**

### Day 4: Bridge Challenge: Build and Test

**WARM-UP: What could go wrong during testing?**

#### Build Phase (20 min)

Emphasis on precision and following the plan.

Engineering Design Process: Prototype -- Teams build bridges.


#### Testing Phase (15 min)

Teams add weight incrementally. Teacher records max weight before failure.

**DOK 4: What design features correlated with strongest bridges? Evidence?**

**EXIT TICKET: One engineering lesson from testing, connected to real civil engineering. (d(1)(C))**

### Day 5: Engineering Wrap-Up + Favorites

**WARM-UP: If a bridge failed, whose job to investigate?**

#### Results + Presentations (20 min)

Display results. Teams present design rationale, results, and improvements.

#### H&L Favorites + Research Submission (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Submit your Emerging Engineering research. Favorite engineering careers in H&L."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students favorite careers and update Building Blocks. (Ch 8: Engineering and Ch 3: Architecture & Construction provide the career profiles for favoriting; Ch 16: My Next Steps provides the Building Blocks and Career Plan templates.)

**DOK 3: Is engineering a good fit for you based on RIASEC, this week, and education requirements?**

**EXIT TICKET: Most interesting engineering career and why it is 'emerging.' (d(1)(D))**
