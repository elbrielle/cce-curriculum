## Lesson Objective

Students will explore HVAC, electrical, plumbing, and welding careers through Hats & Ladders, compare certification timelines and earning potential across these trades, analyze DFW labor market data to identify high-demand skilled trades, and understand how future Irving ISD pathways will prepare students for these careers.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe the certification and training requirements for at least three skilled trades, compare their DFW salaries and job demand, and explain why skilled trades are classified as high-demand occupations."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(2)(A):** Research and describe applicable academic, technical, certification, and training requirements for skilled trades.
- **d(5)(A):** Analyze labor market information such as job availability, industry trends, and employment projections.
- **d(5)(B):** Classify occupations as high-skill, high-wage, and high-demand.

## Career Connection

Electricians, plumbers, HVAC technicians, and welders are among the most in-demand workers in America. The DFW area's rapid growth means these professionals are needed for every new home, office building, school, and hospital being built. Many experienced tradespeople are retiring, creating a massive skills gap. Irving ISD is responding: HVAC, Electrical, and Plumbing pathways are coming to Cardwell/Ratteree in 2027, and Welding is already available at MacArthur. These trades offer strong salaries, job security, and the ability to start earning quickly through apprenticeships.

**Irving ISD Pathway: HVAC / Electrical / Plumbing (Cardwell/Ratteree - coming 2027). Welding (MacArthur - Structural Steel & Metal Welding certification, available now). Students entering 9th grade in 2027 or later will have access to these new pathways.**


## Vocabulary

- **HVAC:** Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning. HVAC technicians install and repair climate control systems in homes and buildings.
- **Journeyman Electrician:** A fully trained electrician who has completed an apprenticeship and passed a licensing exam. Can work independently.
- **Master Plumber:** The highest level of plumbing licensure. Master plumbers can run their own businesses and pull permits.
- **Welder:** A professional who joins metal parts using heat. Welding is used in construction, manufacturing, shipbuilding, and pipeline work.
- **Skills Gap:** The difference between the skills employers need and the skills available workers have. Construction trades have one of the largest skills gaps in America.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In H&L, the Maintenance/Operations pathway within the A&C cluster contains Hats for Electrician, Plumber, HVAC Technician, and related trades. Students can also find Welder in the Manufacturing cluster. The platform's localized DFW salary and demand data is especially powerful this week because it shows students the real earnings potential and job availability in their own area. The demand indicators help students understand why these are classified as high-demand occupations.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Jigsaw:** Assign each team one trade (Electrician, Plumber, HVAC Tech, Welder). Teams become experts on their trade and present to the class, ensuring every student learns about all four trades.
- **Sentence Stems:** For labor market analysis: 'According to BLS data, the job outlook for _____ is projected to grow _____% over the next 10 years. In the DFW area, the average salary is $_____. This occupation is classified as high-demand because _____.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: The Essential Trades: HVAC, Electrical, Plumbing

**WARM-UP: It is July in Texas. Your air conditioning breaks at 2:00 AM. How much would you pay to have it fixed right now? What does that tell you about the value of HVAC technicians?**

#### H&L Skilled Trades Exploration (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today we are looking at the trades that keep every building functioning: HVAC, electrical, and plumbing. Without these professionals, there is no heat in winter, no AC in summer, no lights, and no running water. Open H&L and explore these careers."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore Electrician, Plumber, and HVAC Technician Hats in H&L. The workbook (Ch 3) confirms Electrical as a pathway: "Install, fix, and maintain electrical wiring, equipment, and fixtures; work with telecommunications cables, including fiber optics." These trades Hats likely appear under the A&C cluster's Electrical and related pathways. Students check education pathways (apprenticeship + licensing), DFW salary data, and demand levels, and rate perks/quirks/job gear.

#### Certification Comparison (15 min)

Students begin the Skilled Trades Comparison Matrix, filling in columns for Electrician, Plumber, HVAC Tech: apprenticeship length, licensing requirements, certification names, starting salary, and experienced salary.

**DOK 2: How would you compare the licensing pathway for an Electrician versus an HVAC Technician? Which takes longer and why?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name the three trades explored today and describe one certification requirement for each. (d(2)(A))**

### Day 2: Welding Careers + Jigsaw Research

**WARM-UP: Welding is used to build bridges, skyscrapers, ships, pipelines, and even spacecraft. Name one thing in this room that was probably welded.**

#### Welding Career Exploration (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Welding is a trade that crosses multiple industries -- construction, manufacturing, oil and gas, even aerospace. MacArthur already offers a Welding pathway with Structural Steel and Metal Welding certification. Let us add welding to our comparison."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore Welder and related Hats in H&L. They add welding data to their Skilled Trades Comparison Matrix. (Welding careers appear under Manufacturing in the app; the workbook's A&C chapter (Ch 3) covers related construction trades.)

#### Jigsaw Deep-Dive Research (25 min)

Each team is assigned one trade (Electrician, Plumber, HVAC Tech, Welder). Teams conduct deep research using BLS, H&L, and industry websites. They prepare to present: what the job involves daily, apprenticeship timeline, licensing requirements, DFW salary range, job outlook, and one surprising fact.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about why there is a 'skills gap' in your assigned trade? What factors are causing more jobs to open than workers to fill them?**

**EXIT TICKET: Describe the skills gap in your assigned trade and explain why it creates opportunity for young people entering the field. (d(5)(A))**

### Day 3: DFW Labor Market Analysis

**WARM-UP: If there are more job openings than workers in a field, what happens to the salary? Why?**

#### Labor Market Analysis Activity (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today you are labor market analysts. You are going to look at real data about job availability, salary trends, and growth projections for skilled trades in the DFW area. This is not guesswork -- this is the same kind of analysis that companies and school districts use to make decisions."

Students complete the Labor Market Analysis worksheet. They research each of the 4 trades: current DFW job openings (Indeed or BLS), 10-year projected growth rate, median salary, salary range (entry to experienced), and factors driving demand (new construction, retiring workers, infrastructure spending). They classify each as high-skill, high-wage, and/or high-demand with evidence.

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students use H&L's localized demand indicators alongside BLS projections to build their analysis. (The workbook's Hat Research template (Ch 3: Architecture & Construction) provides a structured sheet for recording salary and demand data from the app.)

**DOK 4: Based on your labor market analysis, if you were advising the Irving ISD school board on which new CTE pathway to invest in, which trade would you recommend and what data supports your recommendation?**

**EXIT TICKET: Classify one skilled trade as high-skill, high-wage, AND high-demand. Cite two pieces of evidence. (d(5)(A), d(5)(B))**

### Day 4: Irving ISD Future Pathways + Coming 2027

**WARM-UP: What new CTE pathways do you wish your school offered? Why?**

#### Cardwell/Ratteree 2027 Preview (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Here is some exciting news: Irving ISD is launching HVAC, Electrical, and Plumbing pathways at Cardwell and Ratteree starting in 2027. That means if you are in 7th grade right now, these pathways will be available when you are in 10th or 11th grade. And welding is ALREADY available at MacArthur. The district is investing in these programs because of the same labor market data you analyzed yesterday."

Teacher presents the upcoming Cardwell/Ratteree trades pathways: anticipated programs, partnership with industry, expected certifications, and timeline. Connect this to the labor market data students analyzed.

#### Complete Skilled Trades Matrix + Career Plan Update (25 min)

Students finalize their Skilled Trades Comparison Matrix with all 4 trades. Then they update their H&L Career Plan to include any new A&C or trades interests from this six weeks.

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students update favorites and Career Plan in H&L with trades and A&C careers. (Ch 3: Architecture & Construction provides the trades career profiles; Ch 16: My Next Steps provides the Career Plan template.)

**DOK 2: How would you describe the difference between choosing a trade because it pays well versus choosing it because you enjoy the work? Which factor matters more to you?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name the Irving ISD pathway that interests you most from this week and explain whether you would want to start it in high school. (d(2)(A))**

### Day 5: Jigsaw Presentations + A&C Cluster Wrap-Up

**WARM-UP: You have now explored Architecture, Engineering, Construction, and four skilled trades. Which career in this entire cluster surprised you the most?**

#### Jigsaw Presentations (25 min)

Each team presents their assigned trade: daily responsibilities, apprenticeship pathway, licensing, DFW salary data, job outlook, skills gap analysis, and one surprising fact. 4 minutes per team. Class takes notes on all four trades.

#### A&C Cluster Wrap-Up + Reflection (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "We are wrapping up the Architecture and Construction cluster. Over the past 4 weeks you explored architecture, civil engineering, construction trades, and skilled trades. Next week we shift to personal finance -- budgeting on a real salary. Go into H&L and make sure all your A&C favorites are saved."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students finalize A&C favorites in H&L. They review their Career Plan and Building Blocks for connections to construction and trades. (Ch 3: Architecture & Construction provides the A&C career profiles; Ch 16: My Next Steps provides the Career Plan and Building Blocks templates.)

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about the overall health of the Architecture and Construction career field in DFW? Would you recommend this cluster to a friend? Why or why not?**

**EXIT TICKET: Complete this sentence: The most important thing I learned about skilled trades this week is _____. (d(5)(A), d(5)(B))**

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide pre-populated BLS data sheets for each trade so students focus on analysis rather than data retrieval.
- Offer a simplified matrix with fewer comparison categories for students who need support.
- Allow students to focus on just 2 of the 4 trades if the full matrix is overwhelming.

### Extensions

- Students calculate the lifetime earnings difference between entering a trade at 18 versus completing a 4-year degree and starting work at 22.
- Research the growing demand for 'smart HVAC' systems and how IoT technology is changing the trade.
- Students create a recruitment poster for one of the Irving ISD 2027 pathways targeting future 9th graders.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Electrician = Electricista, Plumber = Plomero, Welder = Soldador, HVAC = Climatizacion, Skills Gap = Brecha de habilidades.
- Bilingual Skilled Trades Matrix with Spanish headers.
- Labor market data displays use numbers and charts that are accessible across language levels.
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers during Jigsaw research.
