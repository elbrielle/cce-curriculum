## Lesson Objective

Students will explore construction careers through H&L, investigate trade school and apprenticeship pathways, research trade unions and professional associations, and classify construction occupations as high-skill, high-wage, and high-demand.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe at least three construction careers, explain the apprenticeship model, identify union roles, and classify construction careers using labor market data."

## Career Connection

The construction industry has a massive labor shortage. There are not enough skilled workers to meet demand, meaning construction careers offer strong job security, competitive wages, and rapid advancement. In DFW, carpenters, masons, heavy equipment operators, and construction managers are in extremely high demand. Irving ISD offers Construction Technology at MacArthur (NCCER CORE) and Welding (Structural Steel). The apprenticeship model is the traditional entry point -- workers learn on the job while earning a paycheck.


**Irving ISD Pathway: Construction Technology (MacArthur) leads to NCCER CORE. Welding (MacArthur) leads to Structural Steel & Metal Welding certification.**

## Vocabulary

- **NCCER:** National Center for Construction Education and Research. Develops standardized curriculum and certifications for construction trades.
- **Apprenticeship:** Paid training: on-the-job experience combined with classroom instruction, typically 2-5 years.
- **Trade Union:** An organization of workers that negotiates wages, benefits, and working conditions. Also provides training and apprenticeships.
- **Construction Manager:** Plans, coordinates, and oversees construction projects from start to finish.
- **High-Demand Occupation:** A career with more openings than qualified workers.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In H&L, the Construction pathway includes Construction Manager, Carpenter, Mason, Electrician, Heavy Equipment Operator. Education indicators show apprenticeship model prominently. H&L localized demand data supports career classification activity.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Jigsaw:** Each team researches a different construction trade, then teaches the class.
- **Think-Pair-Share:** After exploring apprenticeships, students discuss personal appeal and pros/cons.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: H&L Construction Pathway

**WARM-UP: What is being built near your neighborhood right now?**

#### H&L Construction Exploration (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Now we look at the people who actually BUILD. Open H&L and find the Construction pathway."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore Construction Hats within the A&C cluster. The workbook (Ch 3) confirms Carpentry as a pathway ("Build, install, and repair wooden structures like walls, stairways, and roofs; work with heavy equipment and follow blueprints"). Students use the Hat Finder to explore: Construction Manager, Carpenter, Mason, Heavy Equipment Operator, Ironworker.

#### H&L Scenario Game (15 min)

> [H&L PLATFORM] The H&L workbook does not include a scenario-based game for the A&C cluster. Instead, use the workbook's "Unexpected Architecture" project (Ch 3) where students design a novelty architectural landmark for Brighton Ridge, or "Trash to Treasure" where students repurpose materials creatively. Alternatively, use Exploring.org career lessons as a supplement.

**EXIT TICKET: Name two construction careers and their education type. (d(3)(G))**

### Day 2: Apprenticeships and Trade Unions

**WARM-UP: Would you rather learn by DOING on a job site while getting paid, or sit in a classroom first?**

#### Apprenticeship Deep-Dive (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "The apprenticeship pipeline: apply, apprentice (earn while learning), journeyman (certified), master (can train others/start a business)."

Teacher presents full apprenticeship pipeline. Students compare to college model.

#### Trade Unions and Associations (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Trade unions negotiate wages, provide benefits, run apprenticeships, and protect workers."

Research: IBEW, UA, Carpenters Union, NCCER, AGC. Note benefits and how to join.

**DOK 3: What is the value of union membership for a new construction apprentice?**

**EXIT TICKET: Explain the apprenticeship pathway and name one trade union. (d(3)(G), d(3)(H))**

### Day 3: Construction Career Classification

**WARM-UP: What makes a career 'high-demand'?**

#### Classification Activity (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "Classify 5 construction careers as high-skill, high-wage, and/or high-demand using H&L and BLS data."

Students complete Classification worksheet for 5 careers with evidence.

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students pull data from H&L.

**DOK 4: Which construction career would you recommend for someone wanting job security with minimal college debt? Use data.**

**EXIT TICKET: Classify one career with evidence. (d(5)(B))**

### Day 4: NCCER and MacArthur Pathways

**WARM-UP: One new thing you learned about construction this week.**

#### NCCER Overview (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "NCCER creates the curriculum and certifications nationally. Irving ISD uses NCCER at MacArthur. CORE is the foundation."

Students explore nccer.org.

#### MacArthur Pathway Mapping (20 min)

Map Construction Technology and Welding pathways: courses, certifications, post-HS connections.

**DOK 2: Advantage of starting NCCER in HIGH SCHOOL versus waiting?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one NCCER certification and what career it prepares you for. (d(2)(A), d(3)(G))**

### Day 5: Jigsaw Presentations + Favorites

**WARM-UP: Would you consider working in the trades?**

#### Jigsaw Presentations (25 min)

Teams present: career overview, education, salary, demand, unions. 3 min each.

#### H&L Favorites (15 min)

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students favorite construction careers and update Career Plan.

**DOK 3: How does the labor shortage create opportunity?**

**EXIT TICKET: Most interesting construction career: high-skill, high-wage, high-demand, or all three? (d(5)(B))**
