## Lesson Objective

Students will explore plant science and natural resources careers through H&L, research emerging careers in precision agriculture and urban farming, and connect environmental science to sustainability careers.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe careers in plant science and natural resources, research one emerging occupation in sustainable agriculture, and explain how environmental changes are creating new career opportunities."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(1)(C):** Identify various career opportunities within one or more career clusters.
- **d(1)(D):** Research and evaluate emerging occupations related to career interest areas.

## Materials Needed

- Chromebooks
- Hats & Ladders accounts
- BLS - Conservation Scientists: https://www.bls.gov/ooh/life-physical-and-social-science/conservation-scientists.htm
- USDA Careers: https://www.usda.gov/our-agency/careers

## Career Connection

Plant science careers are at the forefront of solving some of the world's biggest problems: feeding a growing population, adapting to climate change, and protecting natural resources. At Nimitz HS, the Plant Science pathway leads to the Texas Certified Landscape Associate credential, while the Sustainable Engineering pathway connects to AutoDesk CAD and Inventor certifications.

**Irving ISD Pathway: Plant Science (Nimitz HS -- Texas Certified Landscape Associate) and Sustainable Engineering (Nimitz HS -- AutoDesk CAD and Inventor certifications).**

## Vocabulary

- **Horticulture:** The science of growing plants, including fruits, vegetables, flowers, and ornamental plants.
- **Precision Agriculture:** Using technology like GPS, drones, and sensors to optimize farming practices.
- **Conservation:** The careful management and protection of natural resources like water, soil, and forests.
- **Sustainability:** Meeting present needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.
- **Urban Farming:** Growing food in urban areas using techniques like vertical farming, rooftop gardens, and hydroponics.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In H&L, the Plant Systems and Natural Resources Systems pathways show students careers that combine science, technology, and environmental stewardship. The career profiles highlight both traditional careers (farmer, forester) and emerging ones (precision agriculture technician, urban farming manager).

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Stop and Jot:** During the Ag cluster exploration, students pause to jot emerging careers and sustainability connections.
- **Think-Pair-Share:** After researching emerging ag careers, students share their findings with a partner before class discussion.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: Plant Systems Pathway

**WARM-UP: Where does the food you eat come from? Trace one meal back to the farm.**

#### H&L Plant Systems Exploration (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "We explored animal careers last week. This week we are looking at the other side of agriculture: plants and natural resources. Open H&L and explore the Plant Systems and Natural Resources pathways."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore the Plant Science and Environmental and Natural Resources pathways (confirmed from H&L workbook Ch 2). Plant Science: "Advances crop growth, production, and agricultural research." Environmental and Natural Resources: "Conserves and manages natural resources like water and forests." Students use the Hat Finder to explore Hats: Botanist, Horticulturist, Soil Scientist, Agricultural Engineer, Park Ranger, Forester, Environmental Scientist.

#### Career Research (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Now pick two plant science careers that interest you. For each one, I want you to find three things in H&L: What education do you need? What is the salary in DFW? And what does a typical day look like? Then compare the two side by side. You might be surprised how different a Botanist's day is from a Park Ranger's day."

Students select 2 plant science careers and compare education, salary, and daily tasks using H&L and BLS.

**DOK 2: How would you describe the difference between a Horticulturist and an Environmental Scientist?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name two plant science careers and the education each requires. (d(1)(C))**

### Day 2: Emerging Ag: Tech Meets Farming

**WARM-UP: Can drones help farmers? How?**

#### Emerging Ag Career Research (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "Agriculture is being transformed by technology. Precision agriculture uses GPS, drones, and AI to farm more efficiently. Urban farming is growing food in cities. These are emerging careers that barely existed 10 years ago. Research one and become the expert."

Students research one emerging ag career (precision ag technician, urban farmer, agricultural drone operator, hydroponic specialist) using BLS Career Outlook and supplemental resources. Complete research template.

**DOK 4: Based on labor market data, what argument would you make for why schools should teach agricultural technology alongside traditional farming?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name your emerging ag career and explain why it is growing. (d(1)(D))**

### Day 3: Sustainability and Environmental Careers

**WARM-UP: What does 'sustainability' mean? Can you give an example from your own life?**

#### H&L Environmental Pathways (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today we bridge from plants to the environment. Environmental careers focus on PROTECTING natural resources -- clean water, clean air, healthy forests. These careers are growing fast because every city, company, and government needs people who understand environmental science. Open H&L and explore the Environmental Service Systems pathway."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore Environmental Service Systems pathway (from H&L workbook Ch 2, which covers the full Agriculture, Food & Natural Resources cluster including environmental careers). Hats: Environmental Engineer, Sustainability Consultant, Water Treatment Specialist.

#### Xello Interests Lesson (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Now switch to Xello and complete the Interests Lesson. This activity connects YOUR personal interests to career possibilities. Think about whether anything you discovered in H&L this week matches what Xello recommends."

Students complete the Xello Interests Lesson, connecting their personal interests to career possibilities across all clusters explored so far.

#### eDynamic Unit 7.1 (10 min)

Students begin eDynamic Unit 7.1: Progressive and Adaptable, covering Ag, Energy, and Health Science clusters.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about how climate change is creating new career opportunities in agriculture and environmental science?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one environmental career and explain one societal change driving demand for it. (d(1)(D))**

### Day 4: Emerging Career Presentations

**WARM-UP: Which emerging ag career do you think will be most in-demand in 20 years?**

#### Mini-Presentations (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Presentation time. Each of you has 3 minutes to pitch your emerging ag career to your group. Tell them what the career does, what technology it uses, and why it is growing. After everyone presents, your group votes on which career is most likely to change the world in the next 20 years."

Students present their emerging ag career research in small groups. Each student has 3 minutes. Groups vote on most promising career.

#### Pathway Fit Assessment (10 min)

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students complete the Ag pathway fit assessment in H&L (Ch 2 includes a pathway fit reflection where students evaluate their interest in Agriculture careers and connect them to their RIASEC type).

**DOK 4: If you were designing an Agriculture pathway for Irving ISD middle schools, what emerging careers would you include and what technology would students need to learn?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name the emerging career from another student's presentation that surprised you most. (d(1)(D))**

### Day 5: Ag Cluster Midpoint + Favorites

**WARM-UP: After 2 weeks in Agriculture, has your view of farming careers changed? How?**

#### H&L Favorites + Reflection (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "You have now explored Manufacturing, IT, Law, Health Science, and the first half of Ag. Your Climber Profile is getting rich with data. Next week we continue with Sustainable Engineering and then Culinary Arts."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students favorite Plant Science and Environmental careers (Ch 2 directs students to use the Hat Finder to explore and favorite careers across the Agriculture cluster). They review their Climber Profile with Ag additions.

#### Nimitz Pathway Connections (15 min)

Discuss Nimitz HS Plant Science (Texas Certified Landscape Associate) and Sustainable Engineering (AutoDesk) pathways. Students note which ones align with their interests.

**EXIT TICKET: Name the Ag career you favorited and one reason it fits your RIASEC type. (d(1)(C))**

## Formative Assessment

- H&L pathway exploration (Day 1). (d(1)(C))
- Emerging career research template (Day 2). (d(1)(D))
- Xello + eDynamic completion (Day 3). (d(1)(D))
- Presentation quality (Day 4). (d(1)(D))

## Summative Assessment

Emerging Ag Career Research + Presentation: Students submit research template and present findings. Scored on data accuracy, labor market analysis, and career connection.

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide starter research links for emerging ag careers.
- Simplified research template with fill-in-the-blank fields.
- Visual vocabulary cards for ESL students.

### Extensions

- Research a DFW urban farm and present what careers they employ.
- Compare ag technology careers in Texas vs. California.
- Design a vertical farm layout and explain the careers needed to operate it.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Agriculture = Agricultura, Sustainability = Sostenibilidad, Environment = Medio Ambiente.
- Bilingual research template.
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers for presentations.
