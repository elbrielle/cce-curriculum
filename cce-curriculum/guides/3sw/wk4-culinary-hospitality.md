## Lesson Objective

Students will explore hospitality and tourism careers through H&L, investigate culinary and food service management careers, explore entrepreneurship in the restaurant industry, and design a professional restaurant menu using AI tools and Canva.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe careers in culinary arts and hospitality, explain entrepreneurial opportunities in the restaurant industry, compare salaries across hospitality careers, and design a professional menu using AI and Canva."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(1)(C):** Identify various career opportunities within one or more career clusters.
- **d(3)(I):** Define entrepreneurship and identify entrepreneurial opportunities within a field of personal interest.
- **d(5)(E):** Use resources to compare salaries of at least three careers in the student's interest area.

## Career Connection

The hospitality industry is the world's largest employer. From Michelin-star restaurants to food trucks, from luxury hotels to event planning, this cluster offers careers for every personality type. Entrepreneurship is especially strong in hospitality -- many people dream of opening their own restaurant, catering company, or event business. At Irving ISD, the Culinary Arts pathway at Cardwell/Ratteree leads to ServSafe Manager certification, and Lodging & Resort Management is available district-wide.

**Irving ISD Pathway: Culinary Arts (Cardwell/Ratteree -- ServSafe Manager certification) and Lodging & Resort Management (District Wide).**

## Vocabulary

- **Hospitality:** The business of providing food, drink, lodging, and entertainment to guests and travelers.
- **Entrepreneur:** A person who starts and runs their own business, taking on financial risk in exchange for potential profit.
- **ServSafe:** An industry-recognized food safety certification required for food service managers in most states.
- **Food Cost:** The percentage of menu price that goes toward the raw ingredients. Typically 25-35% in a well-run restaurant.
- **Mise en Place:** A French culinary term meaning 'everything in its place' -- the practice of preparing and organizing ingredients before cooking.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In H&L, the Hospitality & Tourism cluster includes Restaurant & Food/Beverage Services and Lodging pathways. Students can explore Hats for Chef, Restaurant Manager, Food Scientist, Event Planner, and Hotel Manager. The entrepreneurship angle is strong here because H&L's career profiles show how many hospitality careers lead to self-employment.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Chunking:** Days 1-2 = H&L exploration + salary comparison, Days 3-4 = AI menu design + Canva layout, Day 5 = presentation + eDynamic.
- **Active Monitoring:** During AI menu creation, monitor for students understanding the connection between menu design and business skills like marketing, budgeting, and customer psychology.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: The Hospitality Cluster

**WARM-UP: What is the best restaurant meal you have ever had? What made it special -- the food, the service, or the atmosphere?**


#### H&L Hospitality Cluster Tour (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "This week we are exploring careers that most people experience every day: hospitality. Every time you eat at a restaurant, stay at a hotel, or go to an event, you are interacting with professionals from this cluster. Open H&L and explore."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore the Hospitality and Tourism cluster in H&L. The workbook (Ch 10) lists three pathways: Culinary Arts, Lodging and Resort Management, and Travel/Tourism/Attractions. Key workbook activities: "Culinary Twist" (design a creative dish with a surprise ingredient and menu display), "Hotel Rescue" (group problem-solving for hotel crises), and "Pack Your Bags" (tourism campaign for a fictional city called Silverbrook). Students use the Hat Finder to explore Hats: Chef, Restaurant Manager, Food Scientist, Pastry Chef, Event Planner.

#### Entrepreneurship in Hospitality (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Here is something cool about this cluster: it is one of the EASIEST fields to start your own business. Food trucks, catering companies, pop-up restaurants, event planning businesses -- these are all entrepreneurial ventures. Let us look at what it takes."


**DOK 2: How would you describe the difference between working as a chef at someone else's restaurant versus owning your own?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name two hospitality careers and one entrepreneurial opportunity in this cluster. (d(1)(C), d(3)(I))**

### Day 2: Salary Comparison + Menu Budget

**WARM-UP: How much do you think a head chef at a nice restaurant earns per year? Take a guess.**

#### Hospitality Salary Comparison (20 min)

Students use H&L and BLS to compare salaries of 3+ hospitality careers: Line Cook, Head Chef, Restaurant Manager, Event Planner, Hotel Manager. They complete a salary comparison worksheet.

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students pull DFW-specific salary data from H&L career profiles.

#### Food Cost Math (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "If you are going to be a restaurant entrepreneur, you need to understand food cost. If a dish costs you $4 in ingredients and you sell it for $12, your food cost is 33%. Let us practice calculating food costs for a real menu."

Students calculate food cost percentages for 5 menu items. This connects math skills to culinary entrepreneurship.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about why understanding food cost is essential for someone who wants to open their own restaurant?**

**EXIT TICKET: Compare three hospitality salaries and explain which career offers the best balance of pay and lifestyle. (d(5)(E))**

### Day 3: AI Menu Design: The Concept

**WARM-UP: Look at a restaurant menu from your favorite place. What makes it look professional?**

#### AI-Assisted Menu Writing (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today you are restaurant entrepreneurs creating a menu for YOUR restaurant. First, decide your concept: What kind of restaurant is it? Italian? Taco truck? BBQ? Sushi? Then use an AI tool to help you write professional menu item descriptions. The AI is your assistant, but YOU are the creative director."

Students choose a restaurant concept and use an AI text tool to generate menu item descriptions. Teacher models how to write effective prompts and edit AI output for quality. Students select 8-10 items for their menu.

Engineering Design Process: Define & Ideate -- Students define their restaurant concept and use AI to ideate menu content.


#### Canva Layout Introduction (10 min)

Brief tutorial on Canva menu templates. Students begin selecting a template that matches their restaurant concept.

**EXIT TICKET: Describe your restaurant concept in 2 sentences and name your signature dish. (d(3)(I))**

### Day 4: AI Menu Design: The Layout

**WARM-UP: Why does menu DESIGN matter? Could bad design hurt a restaurant's sales?**

#### Canva Menu Design (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today you are finishing your menu in Canva. Remember -- this is not just about food. Menu design is a MARKETING tool. The placement of items, the fonts, the colors, the descriptions -- all of these influence what customers order. You are combining culinary arts with graphic design and business skills."

Students finalize their menu layout in Canva. They must include: restaurant name and logo, 8-10 items with prices, professional descriptions (AI-assisted), and visual design elements.

**DOK 4: Based on what you learned about food cost, menu design, and customer psychology, how would you design a menu that maximizes both customer satisfaction AND profit? What specific design choices would you make?**

Engineering Design Process: Prototype -- Students create their final menu design in Canva.


**EXIT TICKET: Name one design choice you made for your menu and explain how it connects to marketing or business skills. (d(3)(I))**

### Day 5: Menu Showcase + eDynamic

**WARM-UP: If a customer walked into your restaurant right now, what experience would you want them to have?**

#### Menu Gallery Walk + Peer Voting (20 min)

Students display printed menus (or screen share). Class does a gallery walk and votes for: Best Design, Most Creative Concept, and Most Likely to Succeed. Brief presentations from winners.

#### eDynamic Unit 6.1: Entertaining and Exploring (15 min)

Students complete eDynamic Unit 6.1 covering Arts/AV and Hospitality clusters.

#### H&L Favorites (5 min)

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students favorite hospitality careers in their Climber Profile.

**EXIT TICKET: Name the hospitality career you would want as a FIRST job after high school and one you would want as your DREAM career. (d(1)(C), d(3)(I))**

## Formative Assessment

- H&L cluster exploration (Day 1). (d(1)(C))
- Salary comparison worksheet (Day 2). (d(5)(E))
- AI prompt quality and menu content (Day 3). (d(3)(I))
- Canva design quality (Day 4). (d(3)(I))

## Summative Assessment

Restaurant Menu Design Project: Students submit their completed Canva menu including restaurant concept, 8-10 items with prices, AI-assisted descriptions, and professional layout. Scored on: entrepreneurship understanding (d(3)(I)), salary awareness (d(5)(E)), design quality, and career connection (d(1)(C)).

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide pre-selected Canva templates for students who need design support.
- Offer sample AI prompts for menu writing.
- Simplified food cost worksheet with formula provided.

### Extensions

- Students create a full business plan for their restaurant including startup costs, monthly expenses, and break-even analysis.
- Research the Irving ISD Culinary Arts pathway at Cardwell/Ratteree and present findings.
- Design a social media marketing campaign for their restaurant using Canva.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Restaurant = Restaurante, Chef = Chef, Menu = Menu, Entrepreneur = Emprendedor/a.
- Bilingual menu template option (English and Spanish items).
- AI tools can generate descriptions in Spanish for bilingual menus.
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers during design work.
