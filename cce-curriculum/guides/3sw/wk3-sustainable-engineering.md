## Lesson Objective

Students will explore environmental engineering and sustainability careers, research how changing societal needs create new career opportunities, and connect sustainability to the engineering design process.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe sustainable engineering careers, explain how societal changes like climate awareness create new jobs, and apply the engineering design process to a sustainability challenge."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(1)(D):** Research and evaluate emerging occupations related to career interest areas.
- **d(5)(C):** Analyze the effects of changing employment trends, societal needs, and economic conditions on career choices.

## Career Connection

Sustainable engineering is one of the fastest-growing career fields as society responds to climate change, resource scarcity, and environmental regulations. Environmental engineers design systems to clean water, manage waste, and reduce pollution. Sustainability consultants help businesses reduce their environmental impact. At Nimitz HS, the Sustainable Engineering pathway leads to AutoDesk CAD and Inventor certifications.

**Irving ISD Pathway: Sustainable Engineering (Nimitz HS) leads to AutoDesk CAD and Inventor certifications.**

## Vocabulary

- **Environmental Engineer:** A professional who uses engineering principles to solve environmental problems like water pollution, waste management, and air quality.
- **Green Economy:** Economic activity that aims to reduce environmental risks and ecological scarcities while promoting sustainable development.
- **Carbon Footprint:** The total amount of greenhouse gases produced directly and indirectly by a person, organization, or product.
- **Renewable Energy:** Energy from sources that are naturally replenished, such as solar, wind, and hydroelectric power.
- **Societal Needs:** The changing requirements and priorities of society that influence which careers grow or decline.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In H&L, the Environmental Service Systems pathway introduces students to careers at the intersection of science, engineering, and environmental protection. This week connects strongly to TEKS d(5)(C) because students analyze how societal changes (climate awareness, environmental regulations, green energy demand) directly create new career opportunities.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Think-Pair-Share:** After exploring green economy careers, students think about which sustainability problem they care most about, pair to discuss solutions, then share career connections.
- **Sentence Stems:** For the societal needs analysis: 'Because society is changing in the area of _____, new careers like _____ are growing. This affects career choices because _____.''

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: Environmental Engineering Careers

**WARM-UP: What is one environmental problem you care about? Who do you think works to solve it?**

#### H&L Environmental Pathways (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today we are looking at careers that solve environmental problems. These are some of the most important and fastest-growing careers in the world. Open H&L and explore the environmental careers."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore Environmental Service Systems pathway. Hats: Environmental Engineer, Sustainability Consultant, Water Treatment Specialist, Renewable Energy Technician.

#### NASA Climate Kids Exploration (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Now I want you to explore NASA Climate Kids. NASA is one of the biggest employers of environmental scientists and engineers. Their website shows you the SCIENCE behind the environmental problems these careers work on. Spend 15 minutes exploring -- find one topic that connects to a career you just saw in H&L."

Students explore NASA Climate Kids to understand the science behind environmental challenges and the careers working on solutions.

**DOK 2: How would you describe the connection between climate change and the growth of environmental engineering careers?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one environmental career and the problem it helps solve. (d(1)(D))**

### Day 2: Societal Changes = New Careers

**WARM-UP: Name one thing about society that is different now than when your parents were kids. How might that change create new jobs?**

#### Societal Needs Analysis (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "TEKS says you need to analyze how changing societal needs affect career choices. Today that is exactly what we are doing. Society is becoming more environmentally aware. New laws require businesses to reduce pollution. Clean energy is replacing fossil fuels. Each of these changes CREATES new careers and REDUCES demand for others."

Students create a three-column chart: Societal Change | New Careers Created | Careers Declining. They use H&L career data, BLS, and class discussion to populate at least 5 rows.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about how a student choosing between a career in fossil fuels versus renewable energy should use societal trends data to make their decision?**

**EXIT TICKET: Describe one societal change and two careers it is creating or eliminating. (d(5)(C))**

### Day 3: Sustainability Design Challenge

**WARM-UP: If you could redesign one thing in our school to make it more sustainable, what would it be?**

#### Design Challenge Introduction (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "Your team has been hired as sustainability consultants for Bowie Middle School. Your job: identify ONE sustainability problem in our school and design a solution. It could be energy waste, water waste, food waste, or recycling. You will present your solution using the Engineering Design Process."

Engineering Design Process: Define the Problem -- Teams identify a real sustainability issue in the school.


#### Research and Ideate (25 min)

Teams walk the school (or use knowledge of the building) to identify their problem. They research solutions and create a proposal including: problem description, proposed solution, estimated cost, estimated environmental impact.

**DOK 4: What criteria would you use to evaluate whether your sustainability solution is actually worth implementing? Consider cost, impact, and feasibility.**

Engineering Design Process: Research and Ideate -- Teams research solutions, calculate potential impact, and sketch their design.


**EXIT TICKET: Describe the sustainability problem your team identified and your proposed solution in 2-3 sentences. (d(1)(D))**

### Day 4: Design Proposals + eDynamic

**WARM-UP: What is the strongest argument for your sustainability solution? What is the biggest obstacle?**

#### Team Proposals (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Each team has 4 minutes to present your sustainability proposal. Here is the format: state the problem, describe your solution, share the estimated cost and environmental impact, and -- this is the key part -- name the CAREER that would implement your solution in real life. After each presentation, the class gives one 'star' and one 'wish.'"

Each team presents: the problem, their solution, estimated impact, cost analysis, and which sustainability careers would implement the solution in real life. Class provides feedback using one star and one wish.

Engineering Design Process: Prototype & Evaluate -- Teams present their sustainability proposals to the class.


#### eDynamic Unit 7.1 Completion (10 min)

Students complete remaining eDynamic Unit 7.1: Progressive and Adaptable content.

**DOK 3: Based on the proposals you heard today, which sustainability solution would have the biggest positive impact on our school? Support your answer with evidence.**

**EXIT TICKET: Name the sustainability career that would implement your team's solution in real life. (d(1)(D), d(5)(C))**

### Day 5: Xello Interests + Ag Reflection

**WARM-UP: How has your understanding of Agriculture careers changed since we started this cluster 3 weeks ago?**

#### Xello Interests Lesson (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "We are switching platforms today. Xello's Interests Lesson connects YOUR personal interests to career recommendations. As you complete it, pay attention to whether Xello suggests any of the environmental or agricultural careers you explored this week in H&L. If it does, that is a strong signal about your fit."

Students complete the Xello Interests Lesson, connecting their evolving interests to career possibilities.

#### H&L Favorites + Nimitz Connection (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "If sustainability and engineering interest you, the Sustainable Engineering pathway at Nimitz leads to AutoDesk CAD and Inventor certifications -- these are industry-standard tools used by engineers worldwide."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students favorite environmental and sustainability careers. Teacher highlights Nimitz HS Sustainable Engineering pathway (AutoDesk certifications).

**EXIT TICKET: Would you consider the Sustainable Engineering pathway? Why or why not? Connect your answer to your RIASEC type. (d(1)(D))**

## Formative Assessment

- H&L environmental exploration (Day 1). (d(1)(D))
- Societal needs analysis chart completeness (Day 2). (d(5)(C))
- Design challenge research quality (Day 3). (d(1)(D))
- Proposal presentation (Day 4). (d(1)(D))

## Summative Assessment

Sustainability Design Proposal: Teams submit their written proposal and present to class. Scored on: problem identification, solution feasibility, career connection, and societal needs analysis (d(1)(D), d(5)(C)).

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide a pre-made societal needs analysis chart with 2 rows completed as examples.
- Offer simplified sustainability problem options for teams that need direction.
- Sentence stems for proposals.

### Extensions

- Calculate the carbon footprint of our school and propose a reduction plan.
- Research a real company's sustainability report and present findings.
- Design a renewable energy system for the school using SketchUp.
