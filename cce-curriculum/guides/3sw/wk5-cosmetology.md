## Lesson Objective

Students will explore the Human Services cluster through H&L with a focus on cosmetology and personal care careers, research Texas licensing requirements through TDLR, investigate entrepreneurial opportunities in salon ownership, and complete relevant eDynamic content.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe cosmetology and personal care careers, explain the Texas licensing process for cosmetologists, identify entrepreneurial opportunities in the beauty industry, and explain the steps to enroll in a cosmetology program."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(2)(A):** Research and describe applicable academic, technical, certification, and training requirements.
- **d(3)(G):** Investigate and report on steps required to participate or enroll in career opportunities including apprenticeships.
- **d(3)(I):** Define entrepreneurship and identify entrepreneurial opportunities.

## Career Connection

The beauty industry is a $500+ billion global market that offers diverse career paths from hairstylists to estheticians to nail technicians. In Texas, cosmetologists must be licensed through the Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation (TDLR), which requires 1,500 hours of training. This is a strong entrepreneurship cluster -- many cosmetologists eventually open their own salons or work as independent contractors. At Cardwell/Ratteree, Irving ISD offers the Cosmetology pathway leading to the Cosmetology Operator License.

**Irving ISD Pathway: Cosmetology (Cardwell/Ratteree) leads to the Cosmetology Operator License through TDLR.**

## Vocabulary

- **Cosmetologist:** A licensed professional who provides hair, skin, and nail services. Requires state licensing.
- **TDLR:** Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation -- the state agency that issues cosmetology and barber licenses.
- **Esthetician:** A skincare specialist who performs facials, chemical peels, and other skin treatments.
- **Salon Owner:** An entrepreneur who owns and operates a beauty salon, managing both creative work and business operations.
- **Apprenticeship:** A training model where students learn by working alongside a licensed professional while earning hours toward their own license.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In H&L, the Human Services cluster includes the Personal Care Services pathway with Hats for Cosmetologist, Barber, Esthetician, Nail Technician, and Massage Therapist. The platform shows Texas-specific licensing requirements and the relatively short training timeline compared to degree-requiring careers.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Sentence Stems:** For the licensing research: 'To become a licensed cosmetologist in Texas, you must _____. The total training takes approximately _____. After completing training, you must pass _____.''
- **Think-Pair-Share:** After exploring the TDLR website, students think about whether they would prefer the salon employment or salon ownership path, pair to discuss pros/cons, then share.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: Human Services + Personal Care Careers

**WARM-UP: How many different professionals did you interact with the last time you got a haircut? (Receptionist, shampoo person, stylist, etc.)**

#### H&L Human Services Cluster Tour (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "This week we are exploring Human Services, with a specific focus on Personal Care -- cosmetology, barbering, esthetics, and more. This is a cluster where creativity meets customer service. Open H&L and explore."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore the Human Services cluster in H&L. The workbook (Ch 11) lists NEISD pathways: Cosmetology & Personal Care Services, and Health & Wellness. Workbook activities include "Stress Toolkit," "Job Interviews" and "Practicing for a Job Interview" (interview skills practice), and "Get Out and Move!" (planning a community wellness fair for the city of Fairview). Students use the Hat Finder to explore Personal Care Hats: Cosmetologist, Barber, Esthetician, Nail Technician.

#### From the Field + Career Profiles (20 min)

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students watch From the Field interviews and explore career profiles for personal care careers, comparing education and licensing requirements.

**DOK 2: How would you compare the training requirements for a Cosmetologist versus a Nail Technician in Texas?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name two personal care careers and one difference in their licensing requirements. (d(2)(A))**

### Day 2: Texas Licensing Deep-Dive

**WARM-UP: What does it mean to be 'licensed'? Why would the government require a license to cut hair?**

#### TDLR Website Exploration (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "In Texas, you cannot just decide to cut hair for money. You need a LICENSE from the state. Today you are going to explore the TDLR website and figure out exactly what it takes to become a licensed cosmetologist in Texas."

Students navigate tdlr.texas.gov and research: hours of training required (1,500), exam requirements, renewal process, and cost. They complete a licensing pathway worksheet.

#### Enrollment Steps Research (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Now let us talk about HOW you get this training. At Cardwell/Ratteree, Irving ISD actually offers a Cosmetology pathway where you start earning hours toward your license while still in high school. That is a huge advantage. But you can also attend a cosmetology school after high school. Let us compare."

Students compare: starting the Cardwell/Ratteree HS pathway vs. attending a post-HS cosmetology school. They note time, cost, and advantages of each route.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about the advantages of starting the Cosmetology pathway in high school versus waiting until after graduation?**

**EXIT TICKET: List the three main steps to become a licensed cosmetologist in Texas. (d(2)(A), d(3)(G))**

### Day 3: Entrepreneurship in the Beauty Industry

**WARM-UP: Would you rather work in someone else's salon or own your own? What are the pros and cons of each?**

#### Salon Ownership Research (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Many cosmetologists start by working in a salon, but their dream is to own one. Let us explore what it takes to be a salon owner -- you need business skills ON TOP of your cosmetology skills. Rent, payroll, marketing, inventory -- it is a full business."

Students research salon startup costs, business requirements, and income potential. They compare employee stylist salary vs. salon owner income.

#### Business Plan Brainstorm (15 min)

Students sketch a brief business concept for their own salon or barber shop: name, services offered, target customers, location, and one unique selling point.

**DOK 4: If you were opening a salon in the Irving area, what data would you need to analyze before choosing a location? Consider demographics, competition, and local demand.**

#### eDynamic Unit 4.2 Part 1 (10 min)

Students begin eDynamic Unit 4.2: Experimenting with Careers Part 1, covering Human Services careers.

**EXIT TICKET: Name one entrepreneurial opportunity in the beauty industry and explain what business skills it requires beyond cosmetology skills. (d(3)(I))**

### Day 4: Salon Concept Presentations

**WARM-UP: What makes one salon more successful than another? Is it just skill, or is it also business?**

#### Salon Concept Mini-Pitches (25 min)

Students present their salon business concept to the class in 2-minute pitches. They explain: concept, target market, services, unique selling point, and connection to cosmetology training. Class provides feedback.

#### Apprenticeship Discussion (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "Apprenticeship is another path into cosmetology. Instead of going to a school, you learn by working directly under a licensed professional while earning your required hours. This is a centuries-old model that still works today."

Brief overview of apprenticeship as an alternative training model. Students compare apprenticeship vs. school vs. HS pathway.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about the advantages and disadvantages of learning through apprenticeship versus formal schooling?**

**EXIT TICKET: Describe one advantage and one disadvantage of the apprenticeship model for cosmetology training. (d(3)(G))**

### Day 5: Human Services Wrap-Up + Favorites

**WARM-UP: Could you see yourself in a personal care career? What would be the best and worst parts?**

#### H&L Favorites + Profile Update (15 min)

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students favorite personal care careers and update their Climber Profile. Teacher highlights the Cardwell/Ratteree Cosmetology pathway.

#### Career Cluster Check-In (20 min)

Students update their My Career Journey reflection with notes on the Human Services cluster. They check their SMART goal progress from the 2nd Six Weeks.

**DOK 2: How would you describe how your career interests have evolved since the beginning of the year?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name the personal care career that interests you most and explain one step you would take to pursue it. (d(2)(A), d(3)(G))**

## Formative Assessment

- TDLR licensing worksheet accuracy (Day 2). (d(2)(A), d(3)(G))
- Business concept quality (Day 3). (d(3)(I))

Cosmetology Career Report: Students submit a one-page report covering: licensing requirements in Texas, comparison of 3 education pathways (HS, school, apprenticeship), and one entrepreneurial opportunity. (d(2)(A), d(3)(G), d(3)(I))
