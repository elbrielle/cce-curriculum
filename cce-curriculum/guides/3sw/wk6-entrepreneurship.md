## Lesson Objective

Students will explore the Business Management cluster through H&L, define entrepreneurship, investigate entrepreneurial opportunities across multiple fields, create a personal budget using H&L MoneySkills, and design a company logo for Glowforge cutting.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can define entrepreneurship, identify entrepreneurial opportunities in at least two career fields, create a basic personal budget, and design and laser-cut a professional company logo."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(3)(I):** Define entrepreneurship and identify entrepreneurial opportunities within a field of personal interest.
- **d(5)(D):** Prepare a personal budget reflecting the student's desired lifestyle.
- **d(4)(F):** Define and identify examples of work ethic, integrity, dedication, and perseverance.

## Career Connection

Entrepreneurship is not just a career -- it is a mindset. Entrepreneurs exist in EVERY cluster: tech startups (IT), food trucks (Hospitality), solo law practices (Law), salon ownership (Human Services), freelance graphic design (Arts). This week ties together entrepreneurship themes from throughout the semester. At Irving ISD, the Business & Marketing pathway is available district-wide, and Nimitz HS offers Entrepreneurship & Small Business specifically.

**Irving ISD Pathway: Business & Marketing (District Wide) and Entrepreneurship & Small Business (Nimitz HS).**

## Vocabulary

- **Entrepreneur:** A person who creates, organizes, and manages a business, taking on financial risk in hopes of making a profit.
- **Revenue:** The total money a business earns from selling goods or services before expenses are subtracted.
- **Profit:** Revenue minus expenses. The money a business owner keeps after paying all costs.
- **Budget:** A plan for managing income and expenses over a specific time period.
- **Work Ethic:** A set of values centered on the importance of doing work and reflected in a person's diligence and dedication.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In H&L, the Business Management & Administration cluster introduces students to management, finance, and administrative careers. The MoneySkills module provides budgeting activities that connect directly to TEKS d(5)(D). This week is THE primary d(3)(I) week of the year, pulling together entrepreneurship threads from earlier weeks (legal entrepreneurship, salon ownership, restaurant ownership) into a comprehensive understanding.

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Think-Pair-Share:** After exploring entrepreneurship careers in H&L, students think about what kind of business THEY would start, pair to discuss their ideas, then share with class.
- **Active Monitoring:** During Glowforge design and budgeting activities, circulate to ensure students are making meaningful connections between design, business, and career skills.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: The Business Cluster + Entrepreneurship

**WARM-UP: If you could start any business tomorrow, what would it be? Why?**

#### H&L Business Cluster Tour (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "This is our final topic for the 3rd Six Weeks: Business and Entrepreneurship. This cluster is about understanding how businesses work -- whether you are working for someone else or running your own company. Open H&L and explore."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore the Business, Marketing, and Finance cluster in H&L. The workbook (Ch 5) lists six pathways: Accounting and Financial Services, Business Management, Entrepreneurship, Marketing and Sales, Real Estate, and Retail Management. The workbook includes "Marketing on the Move" (designing billboard/bus wrap ads), "Think Inside the Box" (designing a subscription box MVP as an entrepreneur), and "Pitching Investors." Students use the Hat Finder to explore Hats: Entrepreneur, Small Business Owner, Marketing Manager, Operations Manager, Business Analyst.

#### Defining Entrepreneurship (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Let us be clear about what entrepreneurship IS and is NOT. An entrepreneur starts and runs their own business. They take risks. They solve problems. They create jobs. But entrepreneurship also means incredible responsibility -- work ethic, dedication, and perseverance are not optional."

Class discussion on entrepreneurship across all clusters explored this year. Students identify one entrepreneurial opportunity in each cluster they have studied.

**DOK 2: How would you define entrepreneurship in your own words, and how is it different from just having a job?**

**EXIT TICKET: Define entrepreneurship and name one entrepreneurial opportunity in a cluster you have explored. (d(3)(I))**

### Day 2: MoneySkills: Personal Budget

**WARM-UP: If you earned $3,000 per month, how would you spend it? Write your estimate.**

#### H&L MoneySkills Module (15 min)

> [H&L PLATFORM] Note: The H&L workbook does not include a standalone "MoneySkills" budgeting module in Ch 5 (Business). However, Ch 16 "My Next Steps" includes a "Lifestyle Snapshot" activity where students consider the financial aspects of their career choices. The workbook's "Think Inside the Box" activity (Ch 5, p. 78) has an entrepreneurship budgeting component where students design an MVP subscription box and consider pricing, target audience, and business viability. Use these workbook activities alongside the teacher-led personal budget activity below. If the H&L app has a separate MoneySkills module beyond the workbook, explore it as a supplement.

#### Personal Budget Activity (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Every entrepreneur needs to manage money. Today you are creating a personal budget. I am going to give you a salary based on a career you have explored in H&L. You need to plan your monthly spending: rent, food, transportation, phone, savings, entertainment, and more."

Students receive an assigned salary based on a career they favorited in H&L. They complete the printed Personal Budget worksheet, allocating monthly income across categories. Teacher provides DFW cost-of-living data (average rent, utilities, food costs).

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about how your career choice directly affects the lifestyle you can afford? Use specific numbers from your budget to support your answer.**

**EXIT TICKET: What percentage of your budget goes to rent? Is your salary enough to live the lifestyle you want? (d(5)(D))**

### Day 3: Company Logo Design

**WARM-UP: Think of a famous logo (Nike, Apple, McDonald's). What makes it memorable?**

#### Logo Design in Canva (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "If you are starting a business, you need a brand. And every brand starts with a logo. Today you are designing a logo for YOUR entrepreneurial venture. It will be cut on the Glowforge, so it needs to be clean, bold, and work as a single-color design."

Students design their company logo in Canva. Design must reflect their business concept, be convertible to SVG for laser cutting, and demonstrate design principles (simplicity, balance, contrast).

**DOK 2: How would you describe the connection between your logo design and the type of business you want to start?**

Engineering Design Process: Define & Ideate -- Students define their brand identity and design a logo that communicates their business concept.


**EXIT TICKET: Explain one design choice in your logo and why it represents your business concept. (d(3)(I))**

### Day 4: Glowforge Cutting + BizKids

**WARM-UP: What is the hardest part about starting a business? What would keep you going when things get tough?**

#### Glowforge Cutting Queue (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Today we cut your logos on the Glowforge. When it is your turn, convert your Canva design to SVG and bring it to me. While you wait, you have two tasks: first, watch BizKids, and second, start thinking about your elevator pitch for tomorrow. An elevator pitch is a 1-minute summary of your business idea -- clear enough to explain to a stranger in an elevator."

Students convert logos to SVG and send to Glowforge. Teacher manages cutting queue. Students not currently cutting work on BizKids and elevator pitch prep below.

#### BizKids Videos + Work Ethic Discussion (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "BizKids features real young entrepreneurs -- some of them were your age when they started. As you watch, I want you to pay attention to ONE thing: what happened when things went wrong? Every entrepreneur fails at some point. What matters is how they respond. That is perseverance."

Watch 1-2 BizKids video segments featuring young entrepreneurs. Discuss: What work ethic qualities did these entrepreneurs demonstrate? How did they handle failure?

**DOK 3: Based on the BizKids stories and your own research, what conclusions can you draw about why perseverance is the most important quality for an entrepreneur?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name one work ethic quality that entrepreneurs need and give a specific example from a BizKids story. (d(4)(F))**

### Day 5: 3rd Six Weeks Capstone Reflection

**WARM-UP: We have explored Ag, Hospitality, Human Services, and Business this six weeks. Which cluster surprised you the most?**

#### Logo Gallery + Business Pitches (15 min)

> **Teacher:** "Display your logos on your desk for a gallery walk. Then each of you gives a 1-minute elevator pitch for your business. Structure: what is your business, who is your customer, and what makes YOUR business different from competitors? One minute. Go."

Students display their Glowforge-cut logos and give 1-minute elevator pitches for their business concepts. Class provides brief feedback after each pitch.

#### 3rd Six Weeks Reflection (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "This is our last day of the 3rd Six Weeks. You have explored Agriculture, Hospitality, Human Services, and Business. Pull up your My Career Journey reflection and add an update. I want to see: which clusters you explored, any new favorites, your SMART goal progress, and a reflection on entrepreneurship -- do YOU see yourself as an entrepreneur? Why or why not?"

Students update their My Career Journey reflection document. They add: clusters explored this six weeks, new favorites, SMART goal progress, and entrepreneurship reflection. This is the third update to the living document.

**DOK 4: Looking at all the career clusters you have explored so far (Manufacturing, IT, Law, Health Science, Ag, Hospitality, Human Services, Business), what patterns do you see in the types of careers that attract you? What does this tell you about who you are as a future professional?**

**EXIT TICKET: Write: The most important thing I learned about entrepreneurship is _____, and the career cluster I am most drawn to overall is _____. (d(3)(I))**

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide a pre-filled budget worksheet with DFW cost-of-living data and category labels.
- Offer logo design templates in Canva for students who need design support.
- Sentence stems for the reflection.

### Extensions

- Create a full business plan including revenue projections, expenses, and break-even analysis.
- Research the Irving ISD Business & Marketing pathway and create a 4-year plan.
- Design a complete brand identity (logo, color scheme, tagline, business card) for their venture.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Entrepreneur = Emprendedor/a, Budget = Presupuesto, Profit = Ganancia, Revenue = Ingresos.
- Bilingual budget worksheet with Spanish category labels.
- Visual logo design guide with labeled examples.
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers.
