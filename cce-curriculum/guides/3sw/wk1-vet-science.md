## Lesson Objective

Students will explore the Ag, Food & Natural Resources cluster through H&L, investigate veterinary science careers from Vet Tech to Veterinarian, compare education pathways, and design a 3D-printed assistive device for an animal patient using TinkerCAD.

## Demonstration of Learning

> "I can describe at least three veterinary careers, explain the difference between a Vet Tech and a Veterinarian, and use the engineering design process to prototype a 3D-printed animal prosthetic or splint."

## TEKS Alignment

- **d(1)(B):** Explore and describe the CTE career clusters.
- **d(1)(C):** Identify various career opportunities within one or more career clusters.
- **d(2)(A):** Research and describe applicable academic, technical, certification, and training requirements.
- **d(2)(B):** Use available resources to research and evaluate educational and training options.

## Materials Needed

- Chromebooks with internet access
- Hats & Ladders student accounts
- Computers with TinkerCAD access: https://www.tinkercad.com
- 3D Printer and PLA filament
- Digital calipers or rulers
- Patient Profile cards (animal cases)
- BLS - Veterinarians: https://www.bls.gov/ooh/healthcare/veterinarians.htm
- BLS - Vet Technologists: https://www.bls.gov/ooh/healthcare/veterinary-technologists-and-technicians.htm

## Career Connection

Veterinary professionals are the healthcare workers of the animal world. Vet Techs assist in surgeries, perform lab tests, and increasingly use technology like 3D printing for prosthetics and surgical guides. At Nimitz HS, Irving ISD offers the Veterinary Science pathway leading to the Elanco Veterinary Medical Applications certification. This is one of the most popular CTE pathways in the district.

**Irving ISD Pathway: Veterinary Science (Nimitz HS) leads to the Elanco Veterinary Medical Applications certification.**

## Vocabulary

- **Veterinary Technician:** A professional who provides support to veterinarians in medical and surgical procedures. Requires an associate degree and certification.
- **Prosthetic:** An artificial device that replaces a missing body part.
- **Additive Manufacturing:** The process of creating objects layer-by-layer (3D printing).
- **Animal Systems:** The CTE pathway focused on the care, management, and health of animals.
- **Diagnostic Testing:** Medical tests used to identify diseases or conditions in animals.

## Bridge to Theory (Hats & Ladders)

In H&L, the Agriculture, Food & Natural Resources cluster includes the Animal Systems pathway, which covers veterinary careers. Students can explore Hats for Veterinarian, Vet Technician, Animal Trainer, and Wildlife Biologist. The H&L career profiles show the significant education difference between Vet Tech (2-year degree) and Veterinarian (8+ years including vet school).

## IISD Instructional Strategies

- **Active Monitoring:** During TinkerCAD design days, circulate checking: anatomical accuracy of designs, proper use of scale tools, and connection between design choices and career skills.
- **Chunking:** Days 1-2 = career exploration in H&L, Days 3-4 = TinkerCAD design and prototyping, Day 5 = presentations and reflection.

## Lesson Sequence

### Day 1: The Agriculture Cluster + Animal Systems

**WARM-UP: If you have a pet, who takes care of them when they are sick? What does that person actually do?**

#### H&L Ag Cluster Tour (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Welcome to the 3rd Six Weeks. We are leaving Law and Health Science and entering a completely new cluster: Agriculture, Food, and Natural Resources. This is NOT just farming -- watch the tour video and see how many different careers you can spot."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore the Agriculture, Food, and Natural Resources cluster in H&L. The workbook (Ch 2) lists six pathways: Agriculture Business/Leadership/Communications, Animal Science, Agricultural Technology and Mechanical Systems, Environmental and Natural Resources, Food Science and Technology, and Plant Science. The workbook includes a "Making Connections" pair activity discussing how the Ag industry affects everyone. Key workbook activities: "Candy Conundrum" (food science team problem-solving), "Farm to Table" (agricultural communications infographic), and "Ag-Tech Pest Patrol" (drone design for pest detection). The workbook directs students to explore the cluster in the app for 15 minutes.

#### Animal Systems Pathway (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "Now explore the Animal Systems pathway. Focus on vet careers: Veterinarian, Vet Technician, Animal Trainer, Wildlife Biologist. Compare the education requirements for each."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students explore Animal Systems Hats in H&L and begin comparing education pathways.

**DOK 2: How would you compare the daily responsibilities of a Vet Tech versus a Veterinarian?**

**EXIT TICKET: Name two vet careers and one key difference in their education requirements. (d(1)(B), d(1)(C))**

### Day 2: Vet Career Deep-Dive + Patient Profiles

**WARM-UP: Did you know that 3D printing is being used to create prosthetics for animals? What kind of animal do you think would need a prosthetic?**

#### Career Research (15 min)

Students complete a Vet Career research sheet comparing Vet Tech vs. Veterinarian using H&L and BLS data: education, time, salary, daily tasks, certifications.

#### Patient Profile Assignment (20 min)

> **Teacher:** "For the rest of this week, you are Vet Tech engineers. Your team has been assigned an animal patient who needs an assistive device. It could be a splint, a prosthetic, or a support brace. You will design it in TinkerCAD and 3D print it."

Assign student pairs a Patient Profile card (e.g., 'Tripod' the cat needs a leg extension, 'Rex' the dog needs a custom paw splint). Teams research the anatomy of their assigned animal.

**DOK 3: What conclusions can you draw about why a 'one-size-fits-all' prosthetic would not work for different animal species?**

Engineering Design Process: Define the Problem -- Teams analyze their patient's medical case and identify what device is needed.


**EXIT TICKET: Describe your animal patient's medical problem and the type of device they need. (d(2)(A))**

### Day 3: TinkerCAD Design Day

**WARM-UP: What measurements do you need before you start designing? How will you figure out the right size?**

#### 2D Sketches to 3D Design (35 min)

> **Teacher:** "Yesterday you researched your animal's anatomy. Today you are moving from paper sketches to 3D digital models in TinkerCAD. Focus on the Group, Hole, and Scale tools to build your prototype."

Students create 2D sketches from three angles, then transition to TinkerCAD. Teacher demonstrates key tools. Teams build their prosthetic/splint prototype.

**DOK 4: What information can you gather to support your claim that this specific material and design will improve your animal patient's mobility?**

Engineering Design Process: Research, Ideate, Prototype -- Teams move from sketches to digital 3D models.


**EXIT TICKET: List one way you applied math or geometry skills to your TinkerCAD design today. (d(2)(A))**

### Day 4: Peer Review + 3D Printing

**WARM-UP: If you were the veterinarian reviewing your team's design, what would you check for?**

#### Peer Review Gallery Walk (15 min)

Teams display their TinkerCAD models on screen. Peer teams provide feedback on durability, comfort, and anatomical fit using a checklist.

Engineering Design Process: Test and Evaluate -- Peer review of designs before printing.


#### 3D Print Queue (20 min)

Finalize designs and send to 3D slicer. First batch begins printing. While waiting, teams prepare their presentation.

**DOK 3: How would you adapt your design for a much larger animal, like a horse?**

**EXIT TICKET: Describe one piece of peer feedback you received and how you used it to improve your design. (d(2)(A))**

### Day 5: The Clinic Pitch + Cluster Reflection

**WARM-UP: If you were presenting to a real veterinarian, what would be the most important thing to explain about your design?**

#### The Clinic Pitch (25 min)

> **Teacher:** "Each team will present your 3D-printed prototype to the Chief Veterinarian -- that is me. You must explain: the medical problem you solved, the Engineering Design Process steps you followed, and how this task connects to the career of a Vet Tech in a high-tech clinic."

Team presentations: 3-4 minutes each. Teacher evaluates using rubric aligned to TEKS.

Engineering Design Process: Evaluate & Share -- Teams present their solution with career connection.


#### H&L Favorites + Nimitz Pathway Connection (10 min)

> **Teacher:** "If vet science interests you, Nimitz High School offers the Veterinary Science pathway with the Elanco certification. That is a real credential you can earn before you graduate."

> [H&L PLATFORM] Students favorite 2-3 Ag/Animal Systems careers. Teacher highlights the Nimitz HS Veterinary Science pathway.

**EXIT TICKET: Name the vet career you favorited and explain whether the Nimitz HS pathway interests you. (d(1)(C), d(2)(B))**

## Formative Assessment

- H&L cluster exploration notes (Day 1). (d(1)(B))
- Career research sheet accuracy (Day 2). (d(2)(A))
- TinkerCAD progress check for structural integrity (Day 3). (d(2)(A))
- Peer review participation (Day 4). (d(2)(A))

## Summative Assessment

The Clinic Pitch Presentation: Teams present their 3D-printed prototype explaining the medical problem, EDP steps, and career connection. Scored on: career knowledge (d(1)(C)), education pathway understanding (d(2)(A)), design quality, and presentation skills.

## Differentiation

### Scaffolded Learning

- Provide simplified TinkerCAD tutorial with step-by-step screenshots.
- Offer pre-made Patient Profile cards with guided research questions.
- Allow struggling teams to design in 2D and describe their 3D vision verbally.

### Extensions

- Advanced teams design a second prosthetic for a different animal species.
- Students research the cost of veterinary education and calculate ROI.
- Students explore 3D printing applications in human medicine and compare.

### ELL Language Support

- Pre-teach: Veterinarian = Veterinario/a, Prosthetic = Protesis, Animal = Animal, Surgery = Cirugia.
- Bilingual Patient Profile cards with medical terms in both languages.
- Visual TinkerCAD guide with labeled screenshots.
- Pair ESL students with bilingual peers during team design work.
