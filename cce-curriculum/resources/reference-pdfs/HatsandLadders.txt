Career Clusters

The career options you just saw are called career clusters. Career clusters are groups of
occupations that are related to the same field. Career clusters can help you explore a variety of
careers without getting too overwhelmed.
But there's even more to them!

Each career cluster breaks down into pathways. These are more specific categories that
focus on what you actually do in that line of work and what you need to know to do it well. For

Powerskill: Design
Thinking
Ever thought about coming up with the worst idea possible? It might sound

strange, but it can actually help you think of great ideas! Design thinking is
a process where you purposely come up with the most ridiculous,

silly, and impractical ideas you can imagine. This technique
helps you break free from regular thinking and look
at problems in a totally new way. By focusing on
the opposite of what you think might be a good
solution, you can discover ideas you never
expected. This is why some workplaces use this
method: to find new ideas that may have been
hidden by "traditional" thinking.

a team offood scientists working to make sure it tastes great,

Architects don't just design buildings to be useful - they also want them to catch your eye.
They use creative designs to make sure their buildings stand out and get noticed. Sometimes,

they even design a building to look like the very thing it's related to, like a university shaped

By mixing creativity with purpose, architects turn ordinary places into attractions people wantto
experience.

Oil refiners play an important role in turning crude oil into usable fuel
like gasoline, diesel, and jet fuel. Before oil can be processed,
it must be tested for contaminants that could damage
machinery, lower fuel quality, or harm the environment.
Oil testers collect samples, run lab tests, and look for

problems in the oil. A reading outside of normal
range is considered to be a contamination. A

contamination is a problem because it can
damage any machine, such as an engine, that

uses it. If they find a contamination, they must
figure out what caused it, how to fix it, and how
to prevent it in the future.

failure? Water leaks? Improper storage?)
Find a Solution - What can fix the problem?

⚫ Prevent Future Contamination - What can be done to stop this from happening
again?

Job Interviews

A job interview is like an important game - practice, preparation, and
confidence can help you score the winning shot! It isyour
chance to show an employer why you are the right person
for a job. You want to make a good impression by being
prepared, confident, and professional. Employers
look for people who are skilled, reliable, and wellprepared. Preparing for an interview can help
you feel confident, answer interview questions
clearly, and stand out from the other applicants.
Dress for Success

First impressions matter! The way you dress for
an interview shows the employer that you're

serious about the job. No matter what position
you're applying for, looking clean, neat, and

professional will help you make a great impression.
Here are some tips:

• Dress one level above the job - Ifthe job is casual,
wear business-casual clothing like a collared shirt or

blouse with slacks or nice jeans. If the job is more formal, consider a
button-down shirt, dress pants, or a blazer.

• Stick to neutral or classic colors - Navy, gray, black, white, or beige
tend to look professional.

• Wear clean and appropriate shoes - Closed-toe shoes like loafers.
dress shoes, or clean work boots are usually best. Avoid flip-flops.
sandals, or overly worn-out sneakers.

• Keep grooming in mind - Hair should be neat, and if you have facial
hair, make sure it looks well-kept.

⚫ Limit accessories - A watch or simple jewelry is fine, but avoid
anything too flashy or distracting.
•

Be comfortable and confident - Choose clothing that fits well and

makes you feel confident while still looking professional.

Getting Ready for an Interview
Preparation is key! Follow these steps to set yourself up for success:
•

Research the company - Know what they do and what the job involves.

Use good body language - Sit up straight, make eye contact, and offer a firm handshake.

Engage actively - Maintain eye contact on video and use a positive tone over the phone.

Imagine that you are trying to convince someone that you are the best basketball player on

What is a Job Reference?
A job reference is someone who can talk about your skills, work ethic, and personality to

a future employer. Employers ask for references to get an outside opinion on what kind of

employee you would be. References are important because they help employers make sure

you are a good fit for the position they are hiring for. Choosing the right references can make a
huge difference when applying for a job! Not everyone can be a reference. A good reference is
someone who:

Knows you well professionally.
Can talk about your skills, strengths, and work ethic.
Will give an honest and positive review ofyou.

How to Select and Use References
Here are some steps for choosing a good reference:

1.

Choose People Who Know Your Work Ethic - This could be teachers, coaches, mentors.

volunteer coordinators, or former employers who can talk about your skills and character.
(Friends and family members usually don't count!)

2. Ask for Permission - Always ask your references before listing them! This will give them
a heads-up and time to prepare in case an employer contacts them.

3.

Collect Their Contact Information -A good job reference should include:
◆

Full name and job title

◆

Relationship to you (supervisor, coworker, mentor)

◆

Phone number and email address

4. Keep It Professional - Your list of references should be neatly organized and formatted.
just like your resume.
5. StayIn Touch - Keep your references updated on your job search and thank them for
being a reference.

Background

Warehouse managers help make sure everything in a warehouse is organized, safe, and running
smoothly. They keep track of inventory, organize the warehouse, coordinate shipping and
deliveries, and many other tasks. Without warehouse managers, packages get lost, deliveries
are late, stores and businesses wouldn't have their necessary inventory, and the productivity

in the warehouse would be slow. They help keep the supply chain running so the stuffwe buy
online or in stores gets to us safely and on time!
Project Goal

Sometimes, people develop apps and other systems to help solve problems or make jobs
easier. In order to develop an app that will be helpful and useful, the app developers need to
talk to people who actually work in that certain career. These are called focus groups.
For this project,you will be a warehouse manager who was asked by a companyto join their
focus group in order to share your knowledge of working in the warehouse. This willhelpthem
develop and design an app that connects clients, delivery drivers, and warehouses in real time.
The purpose of the app is to have live communication between the warehouse, delivery
drivers, and the clients. This is to help cut down on some issues clients have been havinglike
not knowing when the truck will arrive, lack of communication between the clients and the

warehouse, late deliveries, and wrong deliveries.

short-term goals, like learning new skills or taking
certain classes, and long-term goals, like choosing
a career path that fits your interests. Having a
career plan helps you stay focused, make good
decisions, and feel more confident about your
future. Even if your plans change, it gives you a
starting point to explore different opportunities
