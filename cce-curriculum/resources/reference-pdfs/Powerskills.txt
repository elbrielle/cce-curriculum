Design Thinking
Ever thought about coming up with the worst idea possible? It might sound
strange, but it can actually help you think of great ideas! Design thinking is
a process where you purposely come up with the most ridiculous,

silly, and impractical Ideas you can imagine. This technique
helps you break free from regular thinking and look
at problems in a totally new way. Byfocusing on
the opposite of what you think might be a good
solution, you can discover ideas you never
expected. This is why some workplaces use this
method: to find new ideas that may have been
hidden by "traditional" thinking.

Teamwork

is a skill that helps people work togetherto reach a common goal. It means

sharing ideas, listening to others, and supporting each other to getthingsdone. In most
careers, teamwork is very important because almost everyjob requires working with others,
whether it is with coworkers, clients, or customers. Good teamwork helps solve problems

faster, makes work more enjoyable, and leads to better results. Here are some different types
of teamwork:

• Collaborative

Teamwork: Where everyone shares ideas and works together equally.

• Supportive Teamwork: Where team members help
each other with tasks or challenges.

• Leadership Teamwork: Where one person
guides the team but listens to everyone's
input.

No matter what type, teamwork needs good

communication, respect, and a willingness

to compromise. Learning to be a good team
member is an important skill that will help you
succeed in any career.

Adaptability

Adaptability is the abilityto adjust when things change or don't go as expected. It

helps you solve problems, stay focused when plans change, and find new ways to

succeed. In school, this might mean adapting to a new teacher's style or finding a

different approach when your first project idea doesn't work. At work, adaptability
helps you handle challenges, like learning new technology or collaborating with

different people. Since plans often change beyond our control, being flexible, open-

minded, and committed to learning gives you an advantage.
Tips for Being Adaptable:

• Embrace a Growth Mindset - See challenges as chances to learn. Instead of
saying "I can't do this," ask "What can I learn from this?"
Practice Flexible Thinking - Look atproblems
from different angles. Try new approachesand
consider multiple solutions.

• Manage Uncertainty - Stay calm when

facing the unknown. Ask questions, break

tasks into steps, and seek feedback.

Receiving Feedback
Feedback is how we help each other grow! Whether it's about a school project, a creative

idea, or a class presentation, feedback helps us improve our work and sharpen our skills. At
work, people receive feedback from their colleagues or supervisors on a regular basis.The
feedback might be related to how you do your job, attitude and work ethic, or behavior. The
purpose of regular feedback is to tell you what you are doing well and areas you can improve.
There are two main types of feedback: verbal and written. Verbal feedback refers to spoken

comments orsuggestions. Written feedback refers to notes, edits, or reflections shared in writing.

When giving feedback, you can use a technique called the
"feedback sandwich":

First layer: Give positive feedback, highlighting
something the person did well.
Second layer: Address an area of
t

improvement, giving clear, specific
language.

Third layer: End with another positive
remark, emphasizing the person's
strengths and room forgrowth.

whether it is a person, a cause, or an idea. In the workplace, advocacy

Advocacy can take many forms. In this activity, you will join a small group and
advocate for healthier food choices in a community. By working together, you'll
design a mobile farmers' market that will deliver healthy food options to people

who need them.Follow the steps to complete the activity.

Step 1: Understand the Need
In your group, read about the need for mobile farmers' markets in different communities. Mobile

farmers' markets are often needed in food deserts. A food desert is an area where people have
limited access to affordable, nutritious food, often because grocery stores are too far away and

transportation is limited. Without fresh food options, people may rely on fast food or processed
foods, leading to health problems like obesity, diabetes, and heart disease. In the United States,

at least 24 million people live in a food desert. They can be found in every state, most often in
rural areas.

Learning Agility

Learning agility means being flexible and ready to adjust when something doesn't
go as planned. People with strong learning agility don't just follow the same steps
over and over; they look at what's working, what isn't, and try new ideas to get better
results. Whether you're planning an event, working on a team, or solving a problem,

learning agility helps you stay creative, smart, and open to change. Learning agilityis
important because:

It helps you solve problems faster.
When you're willing to adjust and try new ideas,you

can fix issues more quickly instead ofgetting
stuck.

•

It prepares you for the real world.

Things don't always go as planned. Being

flexible and learning from experience is a
big part of success in school, work, and
life.

It makes you a better teammate.

People who show learning agility listen,

improve, and support group goals, which
makes them great to work with.
