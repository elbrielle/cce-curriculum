# CFA Template (Common Formative Assessment)

Common Formative Assessments (CFAs) are the block-level checkpoints teachers use to gauge student understanding across a six-weeks. CCE CFAs are Extended Constructed Response (ECR) style, 20 minutes, and align to the TEKS the block actually teaches. CFAs are application-based, not recall. They are scored on a 4-level rubric and inform the next block's pacing.

This page is the format spec. See per-block CFAs linked below.

## Format

| | |
|---|---|
| **Length** | 20 minutes |
| **Style** | ECR (Extended Constructed Response), 3-4 connected parts |
| **Stimulus** | A scenario, data table, or career brief (not a single question) |
| **Rigor** | DOK 2 (compare, classify) and DOK 3 (cite evidence, justify). NOT recall |
| **TEKS coverage** | Every part cites the TEKS it demonstrates |
| **Rubric** | 4-level (4=Advanced, 3=Proficient, 2=Developing, 1=Beginning), plus 0=Not Attempted |
| **Self-knowledge spiral** | At least ONE part pulls forward data from Wk0 (RIASEC, Work Values, or Building Blocks) |

## Structure (4 parts)

- **Part A (Identify).** Student names a specific career, pathway, or concept from the block. DOK 1-2. 3-5 min.
- **Part B (Compare or Analyze).** Student compares two options, cites specific data (salary, education time, daily tasks, certification), and explains the difference. DOK 2. 5-7 min.
- **Part C (Connect to Self).** Student ties the block content to their Wk0 data (RIASEC + at least one Work Value) with specific evidence, not generic "it fits me." DOK 3. 5-8 min.
- **Part D (Forward Action).** Student names ONE concrete, time-bounded step they can take this school year. Pulls in an Irving ISD pathway or a named campus (Singley, MacArthur, Nimitz, Cardwell, Irving High, Ratteree) when relevant. DOK 3. 2-3 min.

Parts can be reordered if the block's content calls for a different flow (e.g., 5SW Wk5 Budget CFA may front-load the data-analysis part). Keep total time to 20 minutes.

## Rubric (4-level ECR)

| Level | Label | Descriptor |
|---|---|---|
| **4** | Advanced | All 4 parts complete. Part B cites specific data (numbers, named Hats, education-year counts). Part C connects RIASEC + at least one Work Value with specific evidence. Part D names a concrete Irving ISD pathway AND a concrete time-bounded step. |
| **3** | Proficient | All 4 parts attempted. Most include specific data. Part C connection is meaningful but may be general. Part D is present but may lack time-bounding. |
| **2** | Developing | 3 of 4 parts. Some general information without specific data citations. Part C may be thin ("I like it"). Part D may be vague or missing. |
| **1** | Beginning | 2 or fewer parts. Mostly recall. Self-knowledge spiral absent. |
| **0** | Not Attempted | Blank or off-topic. |

Score each part 0-4, then take the lowest score across all four parts as the overall CFA score. This prevents students from scoring Advanced while skipping a full part.

## Administration Guidance

- **Timing.** 20 minutes. Give students a 5-minute warning at the 15-min mark. Do not extend; the CFA is a formative snapshot.
- **Allowed resources.** H&L Climber Profile, student workbook, engineering notebook, Hat Research templates from the block. NOT permitted: the CFA rubric itself, peer consultation, internet search beyond H&L.
- **Scoring window.** Score within 48 hours so results can inform the next block's opening day.
- **Data use.** If more than 30% of students score 2 or below on any part, reteach that concept in the first two days of the next block. Adjust pacing accordingly.
- **Student reflection.** After scoring, give students their rubric back and 5 min to write ONE thing they would strengthen if they rewrote their response. This reflection becomes a data point for 4SW Wk1 (mid-year) and 6SW Wk6 (capstone).

## Design Notes

- **Round-1 CFAs need teacher round-2 feedback.** Ship the first CFA, administer it, then collect teacher notes on timing, clarity, and rubric calibration before building 2SW-6SW. Do not batch-ship all six before any have been used.
- **Keep stimulus short.** A one-paragraph scenario or a 3-row data table. Students need 15+ min to WRITE, not read.
- **Avoid block-internal vocabulary traps.** If the CFA uses a term that only one week in the block covered, the CFA is measuring week-specific recall, not block-level understanding.
- **Build the self-knowledge spiral into Part C on every CFA.** Wk0 data is the only content that runs through all 6 blocks. It is the unifying thread.
