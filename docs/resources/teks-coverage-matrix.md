# TEKS Coverage Matrix

Three-tier depth map for TEKS §127.2 Career and College Exploration (Adopted 2023), aligned to CCE 36-week scope.

## Tier Definitions

- **EXPLICIT (E).** The TEKS is the stated Lesson Objective or DOL of at least one week or day. Students produce an artifact that directly demonstrates the TEKS.
- **IMPLICIT (I).** The TEKS is demonstrated as a side-effect of other activities, not the main objective. Students practice the skill but it is not the week's stated goal.
- **GAP (G).** Coverage is thin, incidental, or absent. Flagged for deepening in round-2 revisions or pre-2027 district-wide updates.

A TEKS can be both Explicit in some weeks and Implicit in others. The tier column shows the STRONGEST coverage the curriculum currently offers.

## Flagged for Round-2 Deepening

These codes are covered but thinly. Consider deepening during round-2 revisions if teacher feedback surfaces student weakness. Thresholds updated 2026-04-17 after matrix reconciliation tightened day-level tag counts.

- **d(3)D (Impact of effective planning).** Only 4SW Wk2 Days 3 and 5 tag this code after reconciliation. Students build Career Plans but the metacognition about WHY planning matters is not explicitly taught. Consider a 10-min framing activity in 4SW Wk1 or 6SW Wk6 where students articulate the cost of NOT planning.
- **d(3)F (Co-curricular/extracurricular importance).** Two Implicit touchpoints: 4SW Wk2 Day 4 (Course Plan extracurricular slot) and 4SW Wk6 Day 3 (SkillsUSA/TSA). Consider a 4SW Wk1 Day 4 admonition: "Every pathway has a student organization. Joining it doubles your career-exploration speed." Or convert 4SW Wk6 Day 3 exit ticket to require a specific extracurricular plan.
- **d(4)E (Community service and volunteerism).** Two touchpoints after reconciliation: 6SW Wk1 Day 4 (Explicit) and 3SW Wk1 Day 4 Xello Volunteer Hours (Implicit). Consider cross-linking to Building Blocks in Wk0 Day 3 (volunteering IS a Building Block) and adding to 4SW Wk6 transferable-skills work.

## Buffer-Week TEKS Risk (d(8) block)

Per PLANNING §6: every d(8) week (4SW Wk1, 6SW Wk6) is framed as a flex/buffer week. If a teacher cuts both buffers entirely, d(8)(A), d(8)(B), and d(8)(C) are uncovered. The Climber Profile pathway selection (4SW Wk1 Day 2) happens regardless of buffer framing, which protects d(8)(A). But d(8)(B) (HS course documentation) and d(8)(C) (career plan) depend on the Career Plan being completed. Mitigation options: (1) make 6SW Wk6 non-buffer-framed; (2) move Career Plan documentation to a non-buffer week; (3) add a d(8) backup admonition naming the minimum artifact required regardless of buffer use.
