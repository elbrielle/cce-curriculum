# Day 4: Xello Quizzes + Meet the Career Clusters

## Lesson Overview

| | |
|---|---|
| **Time** | 50 minutes |
| **Objectives** | Set up Xello accounts; complete the 5 onboarding quizzes (Matchmaker, Personality, Skills, Learning Style, Mission Complete); explore the 14 H&L career clusters using the workbook's interest-rating activity |
| **TEKS** | d(1)(A), d(1)(B) |
| **Deliverable** | All 5 Xello quizzes completed + Meet the Career Clusters interest ratings in workbook (Ch 1, pp. 4-6) |
| **Materials** | H&L Workbook Ch 1 (pp. 4-6), Xello accounts, Chromebooks, projector, 16 CTE Cluster poster (or printed cluster cards) |

---

## Warm-Up (5 min)

**WARM-UP: How many career clusters do you think exist? Take a guess and write it down. Then guess: out of all those clusters, how many do you think you would actually want to explore?**

Collect guesses. Most students will guess "20 to 50." The actual answer (Texas CTE: 16; H&L: 14 listed in workbook) will surprise them. Bridge: today they meet ALL the clusters and rate which ones interest them.

---

## Activity 1: Xello Onboarding Quizzes (25 min)

**Source:** Scope and sequence — Xello 7th-grade onboarding tasks: Matchmaker Quiz, Personality Style Quiz, Skills Lab Quiz, Learning Style Quiz, Mission Complete Quiz

Students log into Xello (district SSO). For new students, walk through the dashboard on the projector. Tell them: "Xello is a second platform we use alongside H&L. Think of it as a second opinion. The more data you have about yourself, the better your career plan will be."

Students complete the 5 onboarding quizzes in order:

1. **Matchmaker Quiz** — Career interest survey that suggests careers matching student preferences.
2. **Personality Style Quiz** — Personality assessment (parallels H&L's Discover My Core).
3. **Skills Lab Quiz** — Self-assessment of current skills.
4. **Learning Style Quiz** — Identifies how the student learns best.
5. **Mission Complete Quiz** — Closing activity that summarizes Xello onboarding.

Walk the room. The Skills Lab and Learning Style quizzes are short; Matchmaker and Personality take longer. Students who finish early move ahead — do not stop the class for the slowest student.

!!! tip "Facilitation Tip"
    Some students will complain "I already did this in H&L." Tell them: "Yes, and that is on purpose. We are checking if both platforms agree. If they do, that is strong evidence about you. If they disagree, that is interesting too — it means you are more complex than one quiz can capture."

!!! warning "Common Issue"
    Xello and H&L logins are usually different SSO providers. If a student cannot log into Xello, check their roster status with the campus tech coordinator before troubleshooting on your own.

## Activity 2: Meet the 14 Career Clusters (15 min)

**Source:** H&L Workbook Ch 1, pp. 4-6 — "Meet the Career Clusters"

Open the workbook to page 4. The workbook lists 14 career fields with descriptions and an emoji rating system:

- 😍 You love this field and are very interested in it
- 🙂 You find this field interesting but you don't love it
- 🤔 You don't know much about this field but you would be willing to look into it
- 😐 You have absolutely no interest in this field at all

Read through the 14 fields together (Ch 1, pp. 4-5). For each, give a one-sentence example career so students can ground the description:

- **Agriculture, Food, and Natural Resources** — Farmer, Veterinarian, Food Scientist
- **Architecture and Construction** — Architect, Carpenter, Plumber
- **Arts, Audio & Visual Technology, and Communications** — Graphic Designer, Filmmaker, Musician
- **Business, Marketing, and Finance** — Marketer, Accountant, Business Owner
- **Education and Training** — Teacher, School Counselor, Coach
- **Energy** — Wind Turbine Technician, Solar Installer, Power Plant Operator
- **Engineering** — Mechanical Engineer, Civil Engineer, Robotics Engineer
- **Health Science** — Nurse, Doctor, Dental Hygienist
- **Hospitality and Tourism** — Chef, Hotel Manager, Travel Agent
- **Human Services** — Social Worker, Counselor, Hairstylist
- **Information Technology** — Software Developer, IT Support, Cybersecurity Analyst
- **Law and Public Service** — Police Officer, Lawyer, Firefighter
- **Manufacturing** — Welder, CNC Operator, Quality Inspector
- **Transportation, Distribution, and Logistics** — Truck Driver, Pilot, Logistics Manager

Students rate each field with one of the four emojis directly in their workbook. This is a snapshot of where they are TODAY — they will revisit it at end-of-year (6SW Wk6) and compare.

After rating, have students count how many "love it" and "interested" ratings they gave. Then read the workbook explanation of how clusters break down into Pathways and Hats (Ch 1, p. 6) — for example, Hospitality and Tourism contains the Culinary Arts pathway, which contains Hats like Chef, Food Critic, and Recreation Manager.

**DOK 2:** How would you classify the career clusters into groups based on whether they involve working with people, with data, with things, or with ideas?

## Exit Ticket (5 min)

**EXIT TICKET:** Compare your Xello quiz results to your H&L Discover My Core results. Do they agree? Write one similarity and one difference. *(d(1)(A))*

---

## Differentiation

- **Support:** Provide a simplified visual cluster handout (pictures + 1-sentence description per cluster) for students who struggle with the workbook's text-heavy format.
- **Extension:** Students who finish all 5 Xello quizzes early explore one of their recommended Xello careers in depth. They write down salary, education, and one daily task.
- **ELL:** Show ELL students how to enable Xello's built-in language preferences (if supported by your district). Pre-teach cluster names in Spanish: Agricultura, Arquitectura, Artes, Negocios, Educación, Energía, Ingeniería, Ciencias de la Salud, Hospitalidad, Servicios Humanos, Tecnología de la Información, Ley y Servicio Público, Manufactura, Transporte.
