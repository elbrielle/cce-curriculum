## Activity 1: Powerskill — Design Thinking "Worst Idea" Activity (15 min)

**Source:** H&L Workbook Ch 1, pp. 9-10 — "Powerskill: Design Thinking"

Open the workbook to page 9. Read the introduction together: Design Thinking is a creative process where you intentionally come up with the *worst* possible ideas to break free from traditional thinking. By focusing on the opposite of what you think is good, you discover ideas you would never have considered.

**Student task (from workbook):**

- **Step 1: Read the Prompt** — Worst Idea Prompt: Come up with a new idea for a smartphone.
- **Step 2: Brainstorm the Worst Ideas (5 min)** — In pairs, list as many terrible smartphone ideas as possible. The workbook example: a smartphone that can only be charged at the bottom of the ocean. The crazier, the better.
- **Step 3: Share with a Partner (3 min)** — Pairs decide which idea is the absolute worst. Write it in the workbook: "Our worst idea is _____."
- **Step 4: Class Vote and Discussion (5 min)** — Each pair shares their worst idea. Class votes on the absolute worst. Then discuss the workbook prompts:
  1. What did you learn from using design thinking?
  2. How could you use your class's terrible ideas to design a *better* smartphone?
  3. How might doing this activity at work help people think outside the box?

!!! tip "Facilitation Tip"
    Students LOVE this activity because there are no wrong answers. The dumber the idea, the better. Encourage absurdity ("a smartphone that explodes if you get a B on a test"). Then connect the lesson: Design Thinking is a real Powerskill used at companies like Google and IDEO. By breaking traditional thinking, teams discover ideas they would never have considered.

**DOK 3:** What conclusions can you draw about why companies might use the "worst idea first" approach instead of trying to come up with the best idea right away?

## Activity 2: My Career Journey Reflection Handout (20 min)

**Source:** Synthesis of Week 0 H&L workbook Ch 1 + Xello onboarding quizzes

Distribute the printed My Career Journey reflection handout. This is the major summative assessment for Week 0 — it pulls together everything students learned this week.

**Reflection sections (students fill in):**

1. **My Top RIASEC Personality Type:** _____ (from H&L Discover My Core, Day 2)
2. **My Top Xello Personality Style:** _____ (from Day 4)
3. **My Top 2 Work Values:** _____ and _____ (from Day 3 H&L Explore My Work Values)
4. **My Top 3 Building Blocks:** _____, _____, _____ (from Day 3 H&L app entries)
5. **My Top 3 Cluster Interest Ratings:** Which 3 clusters did you mark "love it" or "interested" on the workbook page 4-5 emoji rating?
6. **My Initial H&L Cluster Recommendations:** _____ (from Day 3 — what did the app suggest after you completed all your inputs?)
7. **One Career I Am Curious About:** Name one specific Hat from this week and explain in 2 sentences why it caught your attention.
8. **One Question I Have:** Write one question you still have about careers, education, or your future.

This document will be revisited at mid-year (4SW Wk1-2 Career Plan) and end-of-year (6SW Wk6 Final Career Plan). Tell students: "Save this. We will look at it again in January and in May. You will be amazed at how much you grew."

Walk the room as students fill it in. Help students who are stuck on item #7 ("One Career I Am Curious About") by asking what jumped out at them this week.

---

## Activity 3: Career Cluster Curiosity Gallery Walk (8 min)

Post the 14 H&L cluster posters (or printed cluster cards) around the room — one cluster per poster. Give each student a sheet of sticky dots.

Students walk the room and place sticky dots under the 2-3 clusters they are most curious about. After everyone has placed their dots, lead a quick whole-class discussion:

- Which clusters got the most dots?
- Which clusters got the fewest?
- Does that mean the unpopular clusters are less valuable? (NO — and this is important. Many of the highest-paying, most stable careers are in clusters that don't sound exciting at first.)

**DOK 4:** Based on what you know about yourself from this week's assessments, what criteria would you use to decide whether a career cluster is worth exploring even if it was NOT in your top recommendations? Name 2 criteria.

- **ELL:** Bilingual reflection handout with Spanish sentence stems. Pair ELL students with bilingual peers for the Gallery Walk so they can talk through their cluster interests aloud.
