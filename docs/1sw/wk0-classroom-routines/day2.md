# Day 2: Discover My Core — RIASEC Personality Assessment

## Lesson Overview

| | |
|---|---|
| **Time** | 50 minutes |
| **Objectives** | Learn the six core personality types (RIASEC); predict your top type; complete the H&L "Discover My Core" assessment; record results in the workbook |
| **TEKS** | d(1)(A), d(1)(B) |
| **Deliverable** | RIASEC results entered in workbook (Ch 1, p. 12-13) and visible on Climber Profile |
| **Materials** | H&L Workbook Ch 1 (pp. 12-13), Chromebooks, projector, RIASEC visual reference (Doer / Analyzer / Creator / Helper / Persuader / Organizer) |

---

## Warm-Up (5 min)

**WARM-UP: Look at these six words: Doer, Analyzer, Creator, Helper, Persuader, Organizer. Without looking anything up, write down the ONE you think describes you best.**

Collect quick verbal responses. Tell students they are going to test their prediction in the next 30 minutes — they will see how close their gut instinct matches the formal assessment.

---

## Activity 1: Introduction to the Six Core Personality Types (10 min)

**Source:** H&L Workbook Ch 1, p. 12 — "Learning My Core Personality Type"

Project the six personality types from the workbook page on the screen. Walk through each with one quick example career. Read them straight from the workbook so students see the language matches what they will see in the app:

- **Doer** — Likes to work with tools, machines, or animals. Hands-on tasks and problem-solving. *Example: Auto Mechanic, Veterinarian, Electrician.*
- **Analyzer** — Likes to think, research, and solve puzzles. Enjoys learning and figuring things out. *Example: Software Developer, Scientist, Detective.*
- **Creator** — Enjoys creative activities like drawing, painting, acting. Prefers unstructured environments. *Example: Graphic Designer, Musician, Architect.*
- **Helper** — Enjoys working with others. Likes to teach, support, or help people. *Example: Teacher, Nurse, Counselor.*
- **Persuader** — Likes to lead, persuade, and organize people. Selling, managing, leading. *Example: Salesperson, Lawyer, Business Owner.*
- **Organizer** — Likes to work with data, numbers, and details. Following rules and systems. *Example: Accountant, Librarian, Project Manager.*

Then have students complete the **Reflection** prompts in the workbook (Ch 1, p. 12):

1. Which personality type do you think will match you best? Why?
2. Which one would surprise you the most if you got it? Why?

Students write 1-2 sentences for each.

!!! tip "Facilitation Tip"
    Tell students up front: "There are no wrong answers and no 'better' types. The most successful adults usually have careers that match who they ARE, not who they think they should be. Be honest." Repeat this multiple times.

---

## Activity 2: Take the H&L "Discover My Core" Assessment (25 min)

**Source:** H&L Workbook Ch 1, p. 13 — "Learn Your Personality Types" (directs students to the H&L app's "Discover My Core" feature)

Students log into Hats & Ladders and navigate to the "Discover My Core" assessment.

> [H&L PLATFORM] The H&L app's "Discover My Core" assessment presents age-appropriate, illustrated survey questions. Students answer honestly and the platform calculates their top personality types. Students should record their results on workbook page 13 in the space provided. Results feed directly into the cluster recommendations the app will generate later this week — so honest answers matter.

**Student task:**

1. Open the assessment in the H&L app.
2. Read each question and answer honestly. Do not rush.
3. After completing the assessment, write your top 1-2 personality types in your workbook (p. 13).
4. Read the description of your top type carefully. Note one thing that surprised you or felt accurate.

Walk the room while students take the assessment. Watch for students clicking through without reading. Stop them and reset.

!!! warning "Common Issue"
    Some students will rush to "finish first." Tell the class: "There is no first place in this. The students who go fast usually have to retake it. Slow is smart." If you see a student clicking randomly, sit next to them and read questions aloud with them.

---

## Activity 3: Think-Pair-Share — Results Discussion (8 min)

After all students have results, have them turn to a partner and share:

1. What is your top personality type?
2. Were you surprised, or did it match what you predicted?
3. Do you agree with the description?

Then call on 3-4 students to share with the whole class. Listen for students who say "I don't agree with this" — that is fine. Tell them the assessment is a starting point, not a verdict, and they will take more assessments throughout the week to triangulate.

**DOK 2:** How would you describe the difference between your top RIASEC personality type and the type you predicted before taking the assessment? What might explain the difference (or the match)?

---

## Exit Ticket (2 min)

**EXIT TICKET:** Write down your top personality type from the H&L assessment AND name one career from your top type's example list. *(d(1)(A))*

---

## Differentiation

- **Support:** For students who struggle with the assessment language, sit with them and read each question aloud. Do not let them skip ahead.
- **Extension:** Students who finish early explore 2-3 Hats in the H&L app that match their top personality type. They write down one Hat that interested them and why.
- **ELL:** Show ELL students how to use Google Translate page translation (right-click → Translate to Spanish). Pre-teach: Personalidad = Personality, Hacedor = Doer, Analizador = Analyzer, Creador = Creator, Ayudante = Helper, Persuasor = Persuader, Organizador = Organizer.
